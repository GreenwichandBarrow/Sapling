HOW THIS GROUP CAME TOGETHER

The Women's Search Network (WSN) runs mentorship cohorts that pair active searchers with a Search CEO mentor. Kay signed the WSN Mentorship Commitment and Confidentiality Pledge on March 16. Sarah Rowell organized scheduling via Doodle poll in a thread titled "WSN Group: We are the ones!" over March 12-17. There were 11 emails exchanged. Sarah was proactive and enthusiastic about getting the group off the ground. Megan is tentative depending on conference travel. This is the first time the full group is meeting together.

The group has a formal confidentiality pledge, which means open sharing of deal details, search strategies, and challenges is expected and encouraged.

HOW THIS GROUP COULD BE USEFUL TO YOUR SEARCH

1. Sarah Rowell is the single most relevant contact for the compliance training niche thesis. She acquired, operated, grew, and exited a compliance training company (Kantola). She has the exact playbook: market fragmentation patterns, target identification methods, recurring revenue growth strategies, and exit mechanics with a PE-backed consolidator. Multiple niche intelligence reports have flagged a deep-dive call with Sarah as a priority.

2. Fellow searcher perspective. Adilene, Megan, and Ali are all in the active search phase. They each bring different industry lenses (medical devices, M&A/accounting, engineering/sales). Sharing sourcing strategies, deal flow, and operational challenges with peers who understand the search fund model is high-value.

3. Confidentiality-protected space. The WSN pledge means you can discuss deal specifics, niche theses, and challenges openly. This is rare outside of investor calls.

4. GT Entrepreneurs connection. Both Adilene and Megan are backed by GT Entrepreneurs. This is a shared investor network data point that could surface deal flow or introductions.

5. Recurring relationship. Sarah is collecting preferred days/times for future regular meetups. This is not a one-off. Invest in the relationships now.

---

WHAT TO SHARE ABOUT YOURSELF

- The group already knows the basics from WSN. This is a first meeting, so keep it conversational.
- Share your search journey at a high level: how long you have been searching, what you have learned, how your thesis has evolved.
- You can share niche focus areas since this is a confidentiality-protected group. Compliance infrastructure and insurance are fair game.
- Mention the AI-powered operating system you have built. Fellow searchers will find this interesting and it differentiates your approach.
- Frame mutual value: you are happy to share what you have learned about niches, sourcing, and outreach. Ask what they are working on.

---

WHAT NOT TO OVER-SHARE

- Specific deal names, LOI details, or financial terms from active deals. Even with a confidentiality pledge, keep coded names for deals until you know the group dynamic.
- Investor relationships and specific investor feedback. Keep this general ("my investors suggested...") rather than naming names initially.
- Budget details or fund position. Not relevant to a peer group and could shift the dynamic.

---

SUGGESTED TALKING POINTS

1. Sarah's Kantola journey. Ask about the acquisition process, what she looked for, and what surprised her most about operating. This is genuinely interesting and directly relevant to your thesis. Do not make it feel like a due diligence interview. Let it come up naturally.

2. What everyone is searching for. Ask each person about their thesis, how they are sourcing, and what stage they are at. Listen for overlap, complementary angles, or niches you have already evaluated.

3. Sourcing strategies. Compare notes on what is working: brokers vs. proprietary, cold outreach vs. warm intros, conferences, list-building tools. This is the highest-value tactical exchange for active searchers.

4. The group itself. Ask what everyone hopes to get from the mentorship group and how often they want to meet. Sarah is already collecting preferred days/times. Be proactive about scheduling cadence.

5. One thing you wish you knew earlier. Good icebreaker that surfaces real lessons. Share yours, ask for theirs.
