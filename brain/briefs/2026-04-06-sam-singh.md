1. **Outsourced outreach at scale.** DealsX scrapes 30+ databases and runs personalized multi-channel campaigns (email + LinkedIn). This could handle discovery, enrichment, and outreach as a single provider, freeing you to focus on evaluation and relationship-building.
2. **Email deliverability expertise.** One client testimonial specifically highlights Sam's command of email deliverability and staying ahead of algorithm changes. After the Salesforge spam incident (April 5), this is especially relevant. DealsX sends from their own infrastructure, not your domain.
3. **Search fund experience.** DealsX works with search funds specifically, not just PE firms with different deal sizes. Sam should understand search fund volume, deal size, and thesis constraints.
4. **Done-for-you model comparison.** Megan reports ~6% email response rate and says the system "runs autonomously, never has to talk to them." Christine Kobel also endorsed DealsX over Caprae for email quality. Two independent searcher references is meaningful.

## SUGGESTED TALKING POINTS

1. **How DealsX works end-to-end.** "Walk me through what happens after I sign up. What does the first 30 days look like?" Get specifics: who builds the target list? How is outreach personalized? What does reporting look like? How do you handle niche-specific messaging?
2. **List building vs. outreach.** "Do you handle the company identification and list building, or are you primarily running outreach against lists I provide?" This is critical. You have your own target lists. Understanding whether DealsX adds discovery value or just execution value changes the pricing calculus.
3. **Email infrastructure.** "Do you send from my domain or yours? How do you handle deliverability?" This is the single most important technical question after the Salesforge experience. If they send from your domain with SMTP credentials, that is a hard stop.
4. **Cold calling.** "Does your service include phone outreach, or is it email and LinkedIn only?" If email/LinkedIn only, you still need a calling function for certain niches (Pest Management is calls-first).
5. **Search fund references.** "Can you connect me with 2-3 search fund clients I could speak with?" Megan's experience is promising but early (3 months in, and her LOI came from a warm Twitter intro, not DealsX volume).
6. **Pricing and terms.** "What does engagement look like? Monthly fee, success fee, contract length?" Get specific numbers. Megan mentioned a success fee in her contract. Understand what that looks like and whether it is negotiable.
7. **The $200M claim.** "You mention $200M in deal value uncovered. Can you break that down? Closed deals vs. intros made vs. meetings set?" Understand what "uncovered" actually means in practice.
8. **Confidentiality.** "How do you handle confidentiality around my target criteria and niches? Are you working with other search funds in the same spaces?" Overlap with other clients would be a problem.
