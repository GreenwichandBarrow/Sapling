## 7. INTERESTING TRENDS TO SHARE

- **Banks entering art advisory for free:** BofA, Morgan Stanley, and UBS all building dedicated art advisory teams as client retention. This compresses independent firm pricing but creates acquisition opportunity. Firms with services revenue (not just advisory fees) are insulated.

- **Outsourced sourcing is the norm among searchers now.** Megan Lawlor, Austin Yoder, and others have fully outsourced list building, enrichment, and outreach. G&B is one of the few running an AI-native in-house stack. The tradeoff is control and cost ($64/mo Apollo vs. $2,000+/mo outsourced + success fees).

- **Salesforge spam incident as industry lesson:** Third-party outreach tools with SMTP access can send unauthorized emails from your domain. G&B caught it early, changed passwords, and revoked access. Worth flagging to other portfolio companies doing cold outreach.

- **PE consolidation continues in insurance services.** Every insurance sub-niche explored has PE rolling up independents. This validates the thesis (PE sees recurring revenue + regulation moat) but makes proprietary sourcing essential.
