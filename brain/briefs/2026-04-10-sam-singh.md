### Specialty Insurance Brokerage -- Refined Thesis for Sam

The insurance niche is G&B's highest-conviction thesis. Share this framing with Sam so he targets correctly:

**What you're looking for:**
- Independent insurance brokerages focused on HNW personal lines (fine art, collectibles, jewelry, wine, watches, couture) OR commercial lines serving family offices and wealth managers
- Owner is retiring, succession-planning, or tired of PE roll-up pressure
- Small enough that multiples haven't been bid up by strategic buyers (sub-$3M revenue sweet spot)
- Geographic priority: Midwest and secondary markets first (lower multiples), with a plan to open a NY office post-acquisition. NY metro targets welcome but expect higher pricing.

**What you're NOT looking for:**
- PE-backed or recently rolled-up agencies
- MGAs or wholesalers (retail brokerages only)
- Agencies where the owner IS the entire book with no institutional client relationships
- Large commercial-heavy generalist brokerages

**Why this matters for Sam's targeting:** His instinct to go "broad" on industry niches may work for other verticals, but insurance brokerage has very distinct sub-segments. A generalist P&C agency in Ohio is a completely different business than a HNW personal lines shop in Connecticut. Sam should filter for: specialty/niche focus, HNW or family office clientele, independent ownership, and retirement-age principals.
