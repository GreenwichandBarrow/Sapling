## SUGGESTED TALKING POINTS (15-min cadence)

The slot is 15 minutes. Keep it tight. Build a relationship, surface one or two specific asks, close with a clear next step.

1. **Open with curiosity about her world.** "Tell me what you're working on right now — sale-leasebacks in this market have to be telling you something about where owner-operators are heading." Let her talk for the first three to four minutes.
2. **Bridge to the operating-company angle.** "When an owner runs a sale-leaseback with you, how often does it lead into a full business sale within the next couple of years? Who do they tend to call when they're ready?" This surfaces both the leading-indicator pattern AND her sell-side M&A advisor rolodex in one question.
3. **G&B framing — brief.** "I'm building a long-term holding company that buys operationally critical B2B services businesses. Asset-light, so we're not the buyer for the real estate side — but the owners you're working with five years before they sell are exactly the conversations we want to be in." Two sentences max.
4. **Specific ask.** "Are there one or two M&A advisors or owner-operators in your network you'd suggest I get to know?" Direct ask, easy to answer.
5. **Reciprocal.** "If anything comes through our pipeline where the owner is thinking about monetizing real estate as part of the exit, can I send it your way?" Closes the loop on mutual value.
