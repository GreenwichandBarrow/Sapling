# Acumen Discovery Call — Levi Phelps

**Date:** 2025-09-17
**Attendees:** Kay Schneider (G&B), Levi Phelps (Partner, Acumen)
**Source:** Kay's handwritten notes — restored 2026-04-19 from raw text. Original Fireflies recording exists but API key expired before sync.

## Notes

### 1. Company Overview
- Founded 2008. Levi joined 2012 (4th FT employee), partner in 2014.
- Operates through separate LLC branches but functions as one company.
- HQ NY with branches in LA, Miami, Hudson Valley (Poughkeepsie).
- **75 employees nationwide.**
- Bootstrapped, limited debt (warehouse mortgage on Poughkeepsie + vehicles only).

### 2. Growth Timeline
- 2012-2017: Expanded Queens facility.
- 2017: LA branch opened.
- 2020: Miami and Hudson Valley branches launched.
- 2021: Achieved IAC export certification (TSA-recognized).
- 2022: Customs broker license obtained.
- 2025: Investing in **new booking system** + opening additional Hudson Valley warehouse.

### 3. Business Mix
Revenue split across:
- **Storage** (largest, growing in Hudson Valley) — **16% margin in Queens specifically**
- Trucking — race-to-bottom shuttle market is outsourced
- Air freight / export — has IAC export certification
- Cross-country exclusive runs (non-shuttle)
- Crating — shrinking due to client reuse
- Import/export — low margin but drives trucking
- Installation
- Art fairs — major growth driver post-COVID, Basel Miami especially

**Blended margins: 8-11% net** typical. Stronger relative to logistics peers. Profits reinvested into expansion.

### 4. Operations & Assets
- Own Hudson Valley warehouse; others leased.
- 5 trucks; shuttle service outsourced.
- **Proprietary inventory tool → migrating to Voxme system** (see [[entities/voxme-software]]).
- Custom brokerage drives trucking demand.

### 5. Client Base
- Primary: Galleries (majority, high retention; some >$1M annual spend).
- Secondary: Corporate (Chanel), state capital building, collectors, art advisors, museums.
- LA branch has strong museum ties (Getty, etc.).
- **High switching costs** (8-9k items in custody per client).

### 6. Market Position
- Strong reputation among shippers.
- Competes on quality and relationships, not price.
- Art fairs as entry channel → recurring shipping/storage business.
- Lean vertical (vs subcontracting).

### 7. Financial / Strategic
- Margins under-monetized due to reinvestment.
- Rocketship years 2021-22 (+40%); still building into that growth.
- Heavy capex in Hudson Valley + new warehouse down the street.
- Asset-heavy model not appealing to all buyers.

### 8. Risks
- Multi-partner ownership → complex equity governance.
- Hudson Valley not attractive for UHNW collectors (distance).
- LA branch struggling.
- Saturated shuttle market.
- Cracking new clients is hardest (1-2 years museum cycle).

### 9. Seller Context
- Partners at different life stages.
- **Levi personally interested in liquidity** — kids in middle school, aging parents.
- Not actively pursuing exit, but open to conversations.
- Pride in building with little debt.

### 10. Key Takeaways
- **Strengths:** Strong brand, diversified services, loyal gallery base, proprietary tech (migrating to Voxme), growth in fairs + international.
- **Challenges:** Complex ownership, capital intensity, geography limitations, LA performance.
