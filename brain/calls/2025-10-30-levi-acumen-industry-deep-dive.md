# Acumen Industry Deep-Dive Call — Levi Phelps

**Date:** 2025-10-30
**Attendees:** Kay Schneider (G&B), Levi Phelps (Partner, Acumen)
**Source:** Kay's handwritten notes — restored 2026-04-19 from raw text. Original Fireflies recording exists but API key expired before sync.

## Notes

### Footprint & Assets
- Multi-branch art logistics + storage:
  - **NYC (Queens)** — core operations hub
  - **Hudson Valley (Poughkeepsie)** — large storage facility (owned)
  - **Los Angeles** — West Coast operations
  - **Miami** — Basel-focused operations
- Trucks: NY & Miami owned, LA leased
- Real estate: Poughkeepsie owned (sellers want to retain post-transaction and lease back), other locations leased

### Regulatory: California vs NY
- **CA:** Heavy regulatory burden on trucks (emissions, eventual electric-only). New truck lead times ~1.5 years.
- **NY:** Much longer life cycles on building systems (sprinklers 35yr vs 3-5yr in CA), more favorable capex cycle.
- **Insurance:** Truck insurance premiums roughly doubled since COVID.

### Operations & Service Mix
**Core activities:**
- Storage (largest long-term driver, time-based pricing by volume/sqft, sticky)
- Local trucking and installation
- Cross-country exclusive runs (non-shuttle)
- Air freight coordination
- Customs brokerage (modest margins, drives trucking)

**Storage economics:**
- Highly sticky (8-9k items per client)
- Most storage long-term, especially galleries and estates
- **Storage at 16% margin in Queens specifically**

**Client mix (storage):**
- ~65% galleries
- ~20% estates / artist studios
- ~5-10% private collectors
- LA skew: more museum-related work than NY

### Demand & Market Conditions
- **Art Basel Miami:** Major revenue event. December = "double month."
- **2025 performance:** Roughly on par with prior year. More clients but similar total cash flow.
- **LA performance:** On track to be profitable this year, further improvement expected next year.
- **Client acquisition:** "Cracking the client" is hardest part. Museums attractive but slow-moving (1-2 years).

### Capacity & Expansion Plans
- **Poughkeepsie:** **Currently 90-95% full.** Looking for expansion — first option failed environmental review.
- **LA:** Adding new warehouse in same parking lot, ~20% storage expansion. Two large clients waiting to move in. Break-even in 3-6 months expected.
- **Maspeth (Queens):** ~85% utilization, intentionally kept under for shipping volume.
- **Miami:** Small warehouse. ~50% of yearly revenue from fairs (Dec-Jan).

### Systems & Process — CRITICAL
- Limited "guardrails" — misplaced pieces are rare and well-controlled operationally.
- **Main weakness is data input and accessibility.** Hard to get info into system and pull it out.
- Need workflows that force correct data entry before work proceeds.
- **They are migrating from a proprietary tool to Voxme (see [[entities/voxme-software]]).** Levi: Voxme is "more robust" — there is no software in the industry that does it all.

### Labor & Talent
- **Biggest bottleneck:** finding and retaining qualified art handlers and drivers.
- Intentionally grown more slowly than competitors to maintain quality standard.
- Acumen wins the more complex, "this has to go right" jobs.

### Competitive Landscape
- **Maquette (NY):** Bought by 2 Wall Street guys. Grown aggressively via volume + better financing. 3x trucks, same skilled labor headcount.
- **SAT (Safe Art Transport):** Specialty player.
- **Art Crating:** Specialty player.
- **Crozier:** Cited as example of degraded service after corporate / PE roll-up. "Greatest gift since they are terrible to work with."
- **UOVO:** Cheap storage, expensive everything else. Mega-warehouse insurance issues forced clients out.

**Industry dynamic:** Large companies that roll up operators and run them as corporate machines often see service quality drop, creating opportunity for mid-sized service-focused firms.

### Risk & Insurance Constraints
- **Warehouse risk:** Insurers limit total value per building. Drives Acumen's strategy to limit warehouse size.
- **Truck risk:** High-value loads split across multiple trucks. Acumen offers cargo insurance up to ~$1M per truck.
- Most big collectors carry their own insurance.

### Ownership, Structure & Deal Considerations
- **4 main partners** hold the majority interest (NY).
- NY corporate owns ~85% of each branch.
- Local partners: LA (2 partners, 15% split), Miami (1 partner, 15%).
- = 7 people total, but 4 strategic decision-makers in NY.
- Sellers want to retain Poughkeepsie warehouse and lease back.
- Capex to open new warehouse: $600-750k+ for climate-control + build out.
- Some partners may stay on as operators post-transaction.
- **Levi personally:** Smaller equity holder in NY. Assumes he'd oversee transition period.

### Customs Brokerage Detail
- "Byzantine" to set up.
- No tariffs on art currently.
- Brokerage drives trucking from airport.

### Defensible Position
- "Patience and relationships" — what makes the company defensible.
- Storage transition cost for clients is high (8-9k items per client).
