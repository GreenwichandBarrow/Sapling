## Notes

### Overview
Follow-up call one month after first meeting. Kay had deepened her insurance thesis significantly. Key themes: investor pushback on multiples, a specific acquisition target identified, Art Basel carrier meetings planned, and the wealth transfer tailwind.

### Investor Pushback
- Investors less excited than expected despite insurance being a "core pillar in search"
- August's diagnosis: "It's just expensive. It's expensive to buy them."
- Richard Augustine (industry contact) also flagged multiples as concern
- Additional pushback: industry favors experienced operators, not generalist searchers
- QSBS issue: insurance does not qualify for QSBS tax benefits (unlike other sectors), another investor negative
- August's counter: "If you can find a smaller deal, I don't think the multiples are gonna be that high. I think they're coming down."

### Specific Target Identified
- Two women running a specialty brokerage, near retirement age
- "Very women-centric brokerage" focused on HNW personal lines (yachts, houses, high-value assets)
- Did not want to be acquired by PE
- Personal lines only (no commercial) -- which means potentially lower acquisition multiples
- Kay has a potential partner/producer who would come on board post-acquisition
  - This person comes from a firm with both personal and commercial lines
  - Could bring commercial relationships to expand the business post-acquisition
  - Has been educating Kay about the specialty insurance space

### August's Assessment of the Target
- "I mean that's a great target. Those are really valuable."
- Personal lines for HNW: "very sticky, very high recurring revenue, like 100%"
- Investment bankers confirming personal lines are "some of the most valuable parts of the business right now"
- Caution on commercial cross-sell: "I don't think it's a layup. Otherwise they would have done it."
- Key risk: transition/key person risk if founders retire
  - Standard in insurance: 3-year earn-out
  - Must have 5-year non-compete for retiring sellers
  - Non-solicitation agreements essential for remaining staff

### Wealth Transfer Tailwind (Deloitte Art & Finance Conference)
- Kay attended a Deloitte Private art and finance conference
- Major finding: the "silver tsunami" is creating massive insurance demand
  - Collectors historically bought art in secret, didn't insure pieces
  - Now transferring wealth to next generation, need to properly manage and protect assets
  - 80% of family offices/wealth managers have been asked to manage hard assets
  - Family offices lack operational structure for this (new demand)
  - First priority: making sure assets are protected = insurance
- This demand is decoupled from art market growth -- it's driven by generational wealth transfer

### Market Dynamics
- M&A activity in specialty fine art insurance at the 3-year mark of PE roll-ups
- Roll-ups losing high-touch service quality, clients starting to feel it
- Insurance pricing going up double digits annually (climate risk driving premiums up)
- More risk = higher premiums = higher commissions for brokers
- Climate is a tailwind, not a threat: "People are going to need insurance and insurance is just going to go up and up"
- Being independent is a "real advantage" as so many agencies have been sold to roll-ups

### Sourcing Strategy
- Kay's target list: started with thousands, narrowed to hundreds, now at ~30 targets
- Using overseas research support for list building
- Cross-validating targets with insurance carriers at Art Basel (first week of December)
- Carriers can share: where they're focused, who's doing good work, validate the target list
- Bank of America art services team recommends 4 large + 3 small specialty brokerages (fragmented market confirmed)

### August's Offer

- Happy to keep brainstorming on the thesis
