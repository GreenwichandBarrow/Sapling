# Margot Romano — Art Advisory Niche Discussion

**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Attendees:** [[entities/kay-schneider|Kay]], [[entities/margot-romano|Margot Romano]] (AVP Art Services, [[entities/bank-of-america|Bank of America]])

---

## Notes

### BofA Art Advisory Model
- BofA does art advisory for free as a service to existing private bank clients
- Revenue comes from art lending, not advisory fees
- No commissions from transactions, won't negotiate or get involved in deals
- Can partner with independent advisors because no fee conflict
- Separate team handles corporate art accounts
- Art lending is growing, but next generation wants to physically see the art when it transfers to them

### Independent Art Advisory Landscape
- No regulation. Anyone can call themselves an art advisor
- Ranges from one-person shops to large teams
- All structured differently. Fee models vary widely
- Good advisors are transparent about transactions, fees, commissions
- Bad ones are not (referenced Lisa Schiff as cautionary example)
- Auction houses sometimes do advisory work too (competitive overlap)
- Family offices could work with outsourced advisory teams

### Revenue Model Concerns
- Margot not sure there is good money in being a pure advisory firm
- Retainer structure is more attractive than commission, and some firms are moving to retainer now
- Advisory alone not interesting enough to acquire. Business needs another service to make it compelling: valuation/appraisal, collection management, or similar
- Advisory + services = more defensible, more acquirable

### Schwartzman & Associates Intel
- Margot thinks Schwartzman is smaller than expected. Approximately 4 people
- Focused on expanding existing client collections, not acquiring new clients
- Could be right size for G&B buy box

### Bank Competition Signal
- [[entities/bank-of-america|BofA]] has dedicated art advisory team
- Morgan Stanley has Blue Rider Team
- UBS is building a team too
- Banks offering advisory for free as client retention tool = price pressure on independents

### Insurance Brokerage Angle
- NY insurance brokerage market is very consolidated
- Suggested: buy a Midwest brokerage and open their NY office
- Geographic arbitrage. Lower valuations outside NY, then expand into consolidated market

### Margot's Personal Take
- Sees more opportunity in art services (storage, logistics, lending) than advisory
- Advisory connects the ecosystem but services have more defensible revenue
- Many of the issues she described are reasons she doesn't love the advisory industry
