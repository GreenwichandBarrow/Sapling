### NEW NICHE: Specialty Coffee Equipment Service
Kay introduced. High-end Italian espresso machines (Lavazza-type); US-based local servicers install and maintain them, often multi-brand.

**Growth drivers:**
- Specialty coffee market growing broadly.
- Luxury brands (e.g., Chanel) adding cafés inside boutiques.
- Commercial office "private clubs" (example: Paramount Group) adding coffee-forward shared amenities.

**Servicing dynamics:**
- Machines last 10–15 years — long asset life.
- Service skill set is distinct from general kitchen equipment / hood servicing.
- Frequency of calibration/maintenance varies by model — Kay's husband (fine restaurant) reports "constantly serviced."

**Guillermo's diligenced-adjacent-verticals experience:**
- [[entities/ashford-ventures|Ashford]] has diligenced medical equipment servicing, kitchen equipment (~2 years ago), elevator. Ashford has not closed a pure equipment-servicing deal but has backdoored via equipment-leasing players that layered on servicing.
- "Generally speaking in most markets, there's such an opportunity to improve on local service quality, accuracy, and timeliness that you have a right to win."
- Labor + churn management are core operational levers.

**Guillermo's flagged risks:**
1. **OEM in-house servicer dynamic** (Lavazza example). Kay believed Lavazza was bringing servicing in-house. Guillermo's view: many "factory servicers" in these categories are actually branded third parties operating as exclusive — worth diligencing whether Lavazza/La Marzocco "factory service" is W-2 or contracted.
2. **Commoditization → margin race-to-bottom.** "That is a bad dynamic to be a part of." More defensible for higher-complexity, higher-value equipment.
3. **Contract length ≠ asset life.** Simple equipment = ~1-year contracts. Complex equipment (MRI-scale setup costs) = 3+ years, because upfront calibration/install cost needs to amortize. Coffee machines likely closer to the 1-year end.

I formed that relationship. The revenue is sticky. You can raise prices and extract more margin."
