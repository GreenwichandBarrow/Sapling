# River Guides — Specialty Healthcare Software B2B

Warm-intro targets for vertical SaaS serving specialty healthcare practices (home health, hospice, behavioral health, PT, fertility/IVF, midwifery, menopause, pelvic floor, perinatal, senior living/home care). Ranked list of real people at real orgs. No PE firms — trade associations, industry-specialized advisors, and adjacent operators.

## Associations & Trade Bodies

### 1. William A. Dombi — President, National Association for Home Care & Hospice (NAHC, now Alliance for Care at Home after Jan 2025 NAHC/NHPCO merger)
- Category: Association leader
- Why: 40+ years in home-health policy; knows every home-health tech vendor and mid-market agency owner. The single most-connected person in home care.
- Channel: LinkedIn (search "Bill Dombi NAHC") or intro via state home-care association chapters
- Priority: HIGH

### 2. Ken Albert — CEO, Andwell Health Partners / Board Chair, Alliance for Care at Home
- Category: Association leader + operator
- Why: Operator-turned-association-chair; knows both the tech stack and the consolidator landscape
- Channel: LinkedIn, or via Alliance events (Financial Management Conference, annual meeting)
- Priority: HIGH

### 3. Dr. Steven Landers — CEO, National Alliance for Care at Home (merged NAHC+NHPCO)
- Category: Association leader
- Why: New CEO of merged entity; physician + operator background (VNA Health Group); actively mapping the vendor ecosystem for members

- Priority: HIGH

## Channel Strategy
- Associations first (low-threat, thesis-free outreach — "learning about the space")
- M&A advisors second (NDA relationship, they benefit from a disciplined buyer)
- Operators/SaaS alumni third (warmest, most specific intros, but require validated thesis)
- Avoid: buy-side healthcare PE, growth-equity healthtech funds (per Kay's no-thesis-leak rule)
