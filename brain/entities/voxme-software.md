# Voxme Software Inc.

**Status:** Prospect — potential vertical SaaS acquisition or strategic partnership target for G&B services-to-luxury HoldCo strategy. Identified 2026-04-19 via [[calls/2025-10-30-levi-acumen-industry-deep-dive]] — Acumen is migrating to Voxme as their inventory management system.

- **HQ:** 28 Finch Avenue West, Unit 201, Toronto, Ontario, Canada M2N 2G7
- **Secondary office:** Netanya, Israel

## Product Suite

**Voxme (core):** Moving software / CRM for moving and relocation industry.
**Voxme Art Manager:** Vertical SaaS for fine art logistics — ERP + WMS + CRM combined.
**Voxme Estimator:** Mobile app for pre-move surveys.
**Voxme Inventory:** Mobile app for crews/handlers, captures inventory at item, crate, transaction level.
**Bingo Digital Inventory Portal (bingo.voxme.com):** $300/year subscription + $3 per completed job. New addition.
**AI integration ("Capybara style"):** Recently announced — uses Claude (Anthropic) and Perplexity to bridge customer CRMs with Voxme inventory portal. Sets up personalized workflows that don't require API development.

**Use cases beyond art:** Trade show logistics, white glove transport, interior design logistics, last-mile delivery, removals/moving. Art is one of 5 verticals; moving/relocation is the broader market.

## Strategic Fit for G&B Hybrid HoldCo

**Why a potential acquisition or partnership target:**
- Small (~14 employees) = manageable acquisition size, likely $5-25M valuation range
- Founder-led + bootstrapped (20+ years) = founder may be exit-ready in 2-3 years
- 20+ years of customer relationships + industry memberships = deep moat
- Art Manager would slot into G&B services HoldCo as the platform layer all portfolio companies use
- Existing customer base (Cadogan Tate, etc.) becomes a natural acquisition pipeline for HoldCo tuck-ins
- Max's AI direction matches G&B's thesis — co-development could be very productive

**Caveats / risks:**
- Art is NOT their primary vertical (moving/relocation is bigger) — would acquire multi-vertical SaaS
- Toronto/Canada-based — cross-border M&A friction
- Small team — may need to invest in scaling team to support multiple new HoldCo customers
- Founder-built software — typical SaaS quality concerns (technical debt risk)
- Cadogan Tate already a customer — concentration risk if they compete with G&B HoldCo

## Recommended Engagement Path

1. **Strategic partnership / OEM first** (preferred starting point) — become Voxme's biggest art customer through Acumen + tuck-ins. Co-develop AI integration features. Lower risk, higher signal of fit.
2. **Then minority investment / board seat** at year 1-2 if partnership goes well.
3. **Then acquisition (full or carve-out) at year 2-3** when Max wants liquidity. G&B becomes the natural acquirer because the relationship is deep.

This sequence is lower-risk and more aligned with how Max appears to operate (relationship-driven, thoughtful, not transactional).
