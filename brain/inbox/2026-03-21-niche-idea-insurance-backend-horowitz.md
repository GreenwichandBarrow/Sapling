## Niche Ideas: Insurance Back-End Services (from Mike Horowitz)

**Source:** [[entities/mike-horowitz]] (Streetlight Capital, investor) shared a list of businesses that touch the back end of insurance during a call (Nov 2025). Kay copied the list to OneNote.

**Context:** Mike has direct experience evaluating insurance companies through his own fund. He identified these as the operational infrastructure that carriers and brokerages depend on.

**The list (from Mike):**
1. Credit checks (carrier underwriting support)
2. Property info (property data for underwriting)
3. Replacement costs (replacement cost estimation services)
4. Data analytics (insurance data/analytics)
5. Catastrophe modeling (cat modeling services)
6. Policy forms & loading (policy administration)
7. Software intake claims (claims intake platforms)
8. Claims adjusting/inspecting services (third-party claims)
9. Fraud investigation (SIU / insurance fraud)

**Also mentioned separately:** Home security assessment/consultation — Mike liked the idea but noted top 20% is great, bottom 50% not so much. Not insurance-related.

**Initial fit assessment:**
- Fraud investigation (SIU): B2B, asset-light, compliance-driven, likely recurring. Worth investigating.
- Underwriting support (credit checks, property info, replacement costs): B2B infrastructure, carriers must have this. Worth investigating.
- Claims adjusting: Overlaps with premium audit/loss control already on WEEKLY REVIEW.
- Policy forms/loading: Overlaps with insurance BPO (KILLED — needs override).
- Cat modeling and data analytics: Likely too tech-heavy / VC territory.
