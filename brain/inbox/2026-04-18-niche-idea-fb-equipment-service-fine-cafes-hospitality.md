## Description

**Source:** Saturday 4/18 conversation. Kay surfaced as new niche idea immediately after Saltoun software-multiple compression signal — explicitly chosen as a non-software, services-led, luxury-adjacent counterweight for the DealsX sprint.

**Original framing:** "Is there a business that services the high-end coffee machines in the small coffee shops?"

**Expanded scope (Kay's call):** Full F&B equipment service offering for fine cafes and hospitality. Not just espresso — combined route density across categories.

### What it is

Independent service companies that maintain commercial F&B equipment for specialty cafes, boutique hotels, fine-dining restaurants, private clubs, and members-only venues. Business model: preventive maintenance contracts + emergency repair calls + parts mark-up + water filtration + new install / commissioning revenue.

**Equipment categories in scope:**
- Espresso machines (La Marzocco, Slayer, Synesso, Modbar, Victoria Arduino, Mavam, Rocket)
- Commercial grinders (Mahlkönig, Mazzer, Anfim, Eureka)
- Brewers / batch (Fetco, Curtis, Bunn, Marco)
- Water filtration (Everpure, OptiPure, 3M Aqua-Pure, BWT, Pentair)
- Refrigeration (under-counter, prep tables, walk-ins)
- Ice machines (Hoshizaki, Manitowoc, Scotsman)
- Glasswashers / dishwashers (Hobart, Champion, CMA, Meiko)
- Combi ovens / specialty cooking (Rational, Convotherm, Alto-Shaam)

**Customer set:**
- Specialty/3rd-wave coffee shops (Blue Bottle, Joe Coffee, Stumptown, Sey, % Arabica, Devoción, regional indies)
- Boutique hotels (Aman, Soho House, Cheval Blanc, Auberge, 1 Hotels, individual indies)
- Michelin-tier and fine-dining restaurants
- Private members clubs (Casa Cipriani, Zero Bond, Core Club, Aman Club)
- Members-only co-workings with F&B (Fotografiska, NeueHouse)
- High-end catering / events

### Why it fits G&B Charter

- **Operationally critical B2B service to luxury-adjacent industry** — espresso pulls and ice quality are make-or-break for the brand
- **Services, not software** — Saltoun-signal immune; counterweights DealsX sprint software concentration
- **Physical-workflow protected** — technician on-site, AI-immune
- **Recurring revenue** — PM contracts (quarterly visits) + filter changes + parts
- **Fragmented + owner-operator succession** — most regional shops are 1-5 techs, founder-led
- **Skilled-labor + factory-cert moat** — La Marzocco USA / Slayer / Hobart certifications scarce
- **Route density advantage** — full-equipment scope means same tech visit covers multiple machines per account

### Where it gets harder

- **Revenue scale** — most regional service shops sit at $1-5M; reaching $5-20M needs multi-region, hospitality/chain accounts (Blue Bottle, % Arabica, hotel F&B), or roll-up of 2-3 regional shops
- **Customer concentration risk** — geographic and chain-account dependent
- **Manufacturer disintermediation** — La Marzocco and Slayer have been expanding direct factory service in major cities (SF, Seattle, NYC)
- **Technician hiring** — same constraint that gives the moat is also the growth ceiling
