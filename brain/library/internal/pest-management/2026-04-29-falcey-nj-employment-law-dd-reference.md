## NJ-specific contractor classification (CRITICAL for pest-target DD)

**ABC test (NJ rule):** to classify someone as an independent contractor, all 3 must be true:

1. Person is free from company control
2. Work is outside the usual course of the company's business
3. Person has an independent business of their own

**Hard rule:** **pest-control technicians performing core services CANNOT be classified as 1099 contractors** under the NJ ABC test. They are W-2 employees, full stop, regardless of whether they have their own equipment or vehicle.

**Can be 1099:** HVAC maintenance, fleet servicing, other non-core services performed by genuine third parties.

**Misclassification triggers:** overtime liability, minimum-wage violations, DOL audit risk, owner personal liability.

**DD checklist for any NJ pest target:**

- [ ] Are any technicians classified as 1099? If yes, **inherited liability risk**
- [ ] Has the seller faced any wage-and-hour claims, DOL audits, or class actions?
- [ ] Are arbitration agreements in place?
- [ ] Verify W-2 classification of technicians via payroll review during DD

## NJ Family Leave Act — June/July 2026 expansion

**Current rule:** 30+ employees, 1 year minimum employment to qualify
**June/July 2026 rule:** **15+ employees, 3 months minimum employment to qualify**
**Coverage:** 12 weeks unpaid leave (state pays employee from disability fund); employer must restore to same/equivalent position
**Risk:** cannot terminate for taking leave; creates retaliation exposure

**Why this matters for G&B:** any NJ pest target with 15-30 employees that operated under the old rule will be NEW to compliance burden mid-2026. Pre-acquisition, verify:

- [ ] Does target's handbook reference NJ Family Leave?
- [ ] Has handbook been updated for the June 2026 expansion?
- [ ] What is the current employee count? If 15-30, target is newly covered

## Wage-and-hour landmines

**Take-home vehicle rule:** day starts when work requirements begin (loading, shop stops, etc.). Driving home at end of day is NOT compensable, but pre-route loading IS.

**Travel between job sites:** compensable.

**Mobile apps, paperwork, after-hours email:** must be paid (creates "off-the-clock" liability).

**Lunch breaks:** NJ has no mandatory lunch-break rule, BUT if an unpaid break is given, the employee cannot work during that break (or it becomes paid time).

**Overtime:** all non-exempt employees due overtime regardless of "salary" or "day rate" structure. Salaried-but-non-exempt is a common misclassification.

**Owner personal liability:** in NJ, owners and managers can be held personally liable for wage-and-hour violations. This affects the buyer (Kay) post-close, not just the target seller.

## Other NJ employment-law landmines

- **Cannot deduct from employee pay** for damages, equipment loss, etc.
- **Must pay final paychecks** regardless of unreturned property
- **5-day sick leave policy** — must comply with NJ Earned Sick Leave law
- **Handbook update cadence:** every 3-4 years (laws change constantly)
- **DOL audits:** 50% random, 50% complaint-driven — small operators get hit too
- **GPS tracking** creates evidence of work time — must align with timekeeping

## Risk mitigation strategies (post-acquisition operator playbook)

1. **Arbitration agreements** — prevent class actions, but employer pays arbitration costs
2. **Severance agreements with releases** — for problematic terminations
3. **Clear timekeeping policies** with overtime pre-approval requirements
4. **Progressive discipline** for policy violations
5. **Updated employee handbooks** — match NJ Family Leave 2026 changes
