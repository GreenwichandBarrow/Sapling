## Key Patterns

- Pattern 1: Handwritten letters had a 67% advancement rate (4/6), making snail mail the highest-converting outreach channel in this dataset
- Pattern 2: River guide validation strongly predicts advancement. All 4 letter recipients who advanced had SD positive ratings
- Pattern 3: Art storage niche stalled en masse at Stage 2 with no progression beyond initial warming, except Acumen (Stage 4) and Archive (Stage 3)
- Pattern 4: Attio is significantly behind the tracker for art storage companies (15+ at Stage 2 in tracker but Identified in Attio)
- Pattern 5: The tracker captures outreach content (letter text, email text) that is not stored anywhere in the current system
- Pattern 6: SD comments contain acquisition thesis intelligence (succession gaps, carrier quality, commercial book size) not captured elsewhere
- Pattern 7: Late-add insurance companies (rows without category labels) suggest the search expanded beyond the original two niches

## Lessons Learned

### What Went Well
- River guide model works. SD and BN both provided differentiated, actionable signal
- Handwritten letters outperformed email by a wide margin
- The tracker captured granular outreach history that would otherwise be lost

### What Could Improve
- Attio was not kept in sync with the tracker, creating a 15+ company stage gap
- Art storage outreach stalled at Stage 2 across the board, suggesting a channel or messaging problem
- Only 2 companies received email outreach out of 71 total
- LinkedIn connection data is sparse (38 companies with no data)
