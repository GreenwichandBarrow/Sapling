# Luxury Infrastructure Niche Scoring: Search Fund Acquisition Analysis

Scoring framework for owner-operated businesses serving the UHNW ecosystem across Private Aviation and Yachting/Marine verticals.

## Scoring Key

Each criterion scored 1-3:
- **3 (+)** = Favorable for acquisition
- **2 (+/-)** = Moderate / neutral
- **1 (-)** = Unfavorable

**Criteria:**
1. **Growth** — relative to GDP (3 = >3x GDP, 2 = 1-3x, 1 = at/below)
2. **Margins** — EBITDA (3 = 30%+, 2 = 10-30%, 1 = <10%)
3. **Recurring** — revenue recurrence (3 = >80%, 2 = 60-80%, 1 = <60%)
4. **Fragmentation** — number of players (3 = thousands, 2 = hundreds, 1 = dozens)
5. **Mission-Critical** — switching costs (3 = high, 2 = moderate, 1 = low)
6. **Tech Risk** — disruption risk (3 = low risk, 2 = moderate, 1 = high risk)
7. **Right to Win** — luxury background advantage (3 = strong, 2 = some, 1 = none)
8. **Acquirability** — owner-operators at retirement age (3 = many, 2 = some, 1 = few)

## ADDITIONAL NICHES NOT ON THE LIST

These niches fit the luxury infrastructure thesis but were absent from the tracker:

### 1. Luxury Estate/Property Management for UHNW ($100K-$500K+ annual per client)
- **Score: ~21** | Est. 200-400 US firms | $1M-$20M revenue
- Managing multiple residences, household staff, vendors, maintenance for UHNW families. Monthly retainer model (recurring). Highly fragmented -- mostly local owner-operators. Strong luxury right-to-win. Many aging founders. Firms like Xerendipity, Celebrity Estate Management.

### 2. Luxury/Household Staffing Agencies (UHNW-focused)
- **Score: ~20** | Est. 100-200 US firms | $1M-$15M revenue
- Placing estate managers, private chefs, butlers, nannies for UHNW households. Placement fees + temp staffing revenue. Fragmented. Starkey International, British American Household Staffing, etc. Strong luxury right-to-win.

### 3. Fine Art Logistics & Storage (UHNW collections)
- **Score: ~19** | Est. 30-50 US firms | $2M-$50M revenue
- Climate-controlled storage, transportation, installation, insurance coordination for private collections. Recurring storage revenue. UOVO, Cadogan Tate, Crozier. Moderate fragmentation. Mission-critical (irreplaceable assets).

### 4. Luxury Vehicle/Collection Management
- **Score: ~18** | Est. 50-100 US firms | $500K-$10M revenue
- Storage, maintenance, transport, insurance coordination for classic car and exotic vehicle collections. Recurring monthly fees. Fragmented across major metros. Strong luxury right-to-win.

### 5. Private Security & Executive Protection Firms
- **Score: ~19** | Est. 200-400 US firms | $2M-$30M revenue
- Residential security, travel security, threat assessment for UHNW individuals. Recurring contracts. Highly fragmented. Mission-critical with high switching costs. Moderate luxury right-to-win.

### 6. UHNW Tax/Family Office Accounting (specialized)
- **Score: ~20** | Est. 100-200 US firms | $2M-$30M revenue
- Specialized CPA firms serving family offices, trust accounting, multi-entity structures, investment partnership reporting. Extremely sticky, high margins, many aging partners. Different from aviation/yacht accounting but same economics.

### 7. Private Aviation Catering / In-Flight Services
- **Score: ~17** | Est. 50-100 US firms | $1M-$10M revenue
- Gourmet catering for private flights. Recurring relationships with management companies and FBOs. Luxury right-to-win (food quality, presentation, dietary expertise). Fragmented.

### 8. Marina Management Companies
- **Score: ~19** | Est. 100-200 US firms | $2M-$30M revenue
- Managing marina facilities, slip rentals, fuel sales, amenities. Recurring revenue from slip contracts. Location moat. Growing demand with superyacht fleet expansion. Some overlap with FBO economics.

## STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### Highest-Conviction Acquisition Targets

**The "Golden Triangle" niches** score highest on the combination of margins, recurring revenue, acquirability, and luxury right-to-win:

1. **Aviation/yacht insurance brokerage** -- 25-30% EBITDA, 85-90% renewal rates, aging owner-operators, proven PE roll-up model (8-14x multiples). The single strongest financial profile in this analysis.

2. **Aviation/yacht accounting firms** -- 30-40% margins, extremely sticky clients, many sole practitioners at retirement age, roll-up economics proven in broader accounting M&A.

3. **Aircraft/yacht management companies** -- Monthly recurring fees, UHNW relationship depth, fragmented market, strong luxury right-to-win. More operationally complex than insurance or accounting but higher strategic value.

### Roll-Up Strategy

The most powerful play may be a **multi-niche roll-up within a single vertical**, acquiring an aircraft management company and then bolting on an aviation insurance brokerage and aviation accounting practice to create a full-service "aviation family office" for UHNW aircraft owners. The same logic applies in yachting.

### Niches to Avoid for Search Fund

- **Fractional operators** -- too capital-intensive, too consolidated
- **Engine MRO** -- OEM squeeze, capital requirements
- **Software/tech platforms** -- VC-backed, wrong buyer profile
- **Fuel procurement** -- commodity margins, dominated by majors
- **Aircraft paint** -- capital-intensive physical plant, project-based
