## New Niches Identified

### 1. Premium Audit & Loss Control Services (Outsourced) — Score: 2.32/3

**Thesis:** Insurance carriers must annually audit every commercial policyholder's payroll, revenue, and exposures to set correct premiums. Most carriers outsource this to independent audit firms. The market is fragmented (40-70 independents), asset-light, recurring (annual carrier contracts), and largely invisible to searchers focused on front-end brokerage. Recent PE entry (Davies/Afirm, 2023) validates exit pathway without saturating the market.

### 2. Surplus Lines Compliance & Tax Filing Services — Score: 2.65/3

**Thesis:** Every surplus lines (E&S) insurance policy requires state-specific tax filings, stamps, and regulatory reports. The E&S market has grown from 3.6% to 12.3% of total P&C premium ($129.8B direct premiums written in 2024), and compliance filing volume is exploding (3.7M items at mid-2025, up 12.4%). States are migrating to new platforms (SLIP+), creating regulatory complexity that benefits outsourced service providers. This is the exact same "embedded compliance" thesis as [[calls/iplc-industry-map|IPLC]] but applied to a larger and faster-growing underlying market.

**Detailed Scorecard:**

|----------|--------|-------|-----------|
| Growth, Penetration & Catalyst | 25% | 3.00 | E&S market 9.7% growth (2025), compliance segment 10-15% CAGR. SLIP+ platform migrations creating active demand catalyst. Kansas (April 2026) and Montana (Jan 2026) go-lives. 3.7M filings up 12.4%. |
| Size & Fragmentation | 10% | 1.50 | THIN POOL: only 10-20 independents after removing PE-acquired (InsCipher/Vertafore, ILSA/ReSource Pro, 3H/Saratoga). Largest concern for this niche. |
| Industry Economics | 10% | 3.00 | 60-70% gross margins (compliance + software platform). 30-45% EBITDA. Extremely asset-light. |
| Mission Criticality | 15% | 3.00 | Every surplus lines policy MUST be filed with the state. Non-compliance = fines + license suspension. State-specific expertise creates deep switching costs. |
| Exogenous Risks | 10% | 2.25 | Double-edged sword: SLIP+ standardization could simplify filing enough to reduce outsourcing demand. But near-term, platform migrations INCREASE complexity. Regulatory environment is the demand driver. |
| Porter's Five Forces | 15% | 2.50 | Low VC interest. High barriers to entry (regulatory knowledge, state relationships). But consolidation is active (3 acquisitions in 2 years). Customer power moderate — brokers can switch filers. |
| Value Creation Opportunities | 10% | 2.50 | Strong tech-enablement opportunity: automate filing, add surplus lines + IPLC on one platform. Professionalization of small shops. |

### 3. Workplace Health & Safety Compliance Training (eLearning) — Score: 2.51/3

**Thesis:** Employers are legally required to provide OSHA safety training, harassment prevention, and industry-specific compliance training. The eLearning delivery model is asset-light, recurring (annual refresh mandates), and high-margin (75-85% gross). PSG/Traliant rolled up harassment prevention (acquiring Kantola and Clear Law Institute), but workplace SAFETY compliance training remains fragmented with many independent providers. Kay's mentor [[entities/sarah-rowell|Sarah Rowell]] executed this exact playbook: acquired Kantola, operated 5 years, exited to PSG/Traliant. This is the single most relevant precedent in Kay's network.
