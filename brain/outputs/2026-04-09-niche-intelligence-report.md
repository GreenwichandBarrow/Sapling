# Niche Intelligence Report: Vertical Compliance SaaS

## Thesis
SaaS tightly tied to a vertical with compliance + people aspects. The "people + platform" model: software-enabled services with compliance requirements. Not pure software, not pure services.

## Focus Areas Researched

### 1. Collection Management Software (Art World) -- FAIL
- **Margins:** Strong (SaaS margins 70%+)
- **Recurring Revenue:** High (SaaS subscriptions)
- **Industry Growth:** Moderate (8.9% CAGR)
- **Growth TAM:** FAIL. US TAM ~$430-700M (36-40% of global $1.2-1.7B). However, art-specific collection management is a small fraction.
- **Why it failed:** ArtBinder and Gallery Systems already acquired by Volaris/Constellation. Kay's own learnings note "deceptively small market" and "galleries resist paying for software." Existing scored niche (Art Platform, 4/28/25). Target pool decimated by PE consolidation.

### 2. Estate Planning Platforms (HNWI/Family Offices) -- FAIL
- **Margins:** Strong (SaaS margins)
- **Recurring Revenue:** High
- **Industry Growth:** Strong (12.3% CAGR)
- **Growth TAM:** Pass. US TAM ~$370-600M (30-40% of global $920M-$1.5B).
- **Why it failed:** Already scored and tabled (8/11/25). WealthCounsel/Vanilla/Trustate consolidating under common corporate ownership. Hidden customer awareness problem per Kay's learnings. VC-funded players competing aggressively.

### 3. License Renewal Compliance Platforms -- FAIL (Narrow/Overlap)
- **Margins:** Strong
- **Recurring Revenue:** High
- **Industry Growth:** Strong (8-12% CAGR depending on segment)
- **Growth TAM:** Pass. US license management TAM ~$590M-$1.3B.
- **Why it failed:** Insurance Producer License Compliance already in WEEKLY REVIEW with its own folder. Broader professional licensing (healthcare, real estate, contractors) is dominated by large players (Vertafore/Sircon, Avalara, CSC Global). The independently-owned niche-specific players (Certemy, iWorQ, Harbor Compliance) exist but the space is fragmenting across too many verticals to constitute a single acquirable niche.

### 4. Environmental Compliance Software -- FAIL
- **Margins:** Strong (SaaS margins 70%+)
- **Recurring Revenue:** High
- **Industry Growth:** Strong (10.7% CAGR)
- **Growth TAM:** Pass. US EHS software TAM ~$860M-$1.5B.
- **Why it failed:** HEAVY PE consolidation. 88 M&A deals in latest year, PE add-on acquisitions up 69%. Seven large players dominate (Sphera, VelocityEHS, Cority, Intelex, etc.). Warren Equity, Susquehanna Private Capital actively rolling up. Acquisition multiples at 10-15x EBITDA for platforms. The independently owned long tail is being systematically harvested.

### 5. Pest Management Compliance Software -- PASS (FINALIST)
- **Margins:** Strong. SaaS gross margins 70-85%, EBITDA 25-40% mature.
- **Recurring Revenue:** High. Monthly/annual SaaS subscriptions, 90%+ recurring.
- **Industry Growth:** Strong. 7.8% CAGR for pest control software. Compliance subset growing faster.
- **Growth TAM:** Pass. US pest control software ~$940M. Compliance subset $200-400M. Customer base: $26.1B industry, 32,700 companies.
- **Target Pool:** 15-25 independently owned vendors (GorillaDesk, Pocomos, Fieldster, PestScan, PestBoss, Pest-App, others).
- **PE Consolidation Risk:** Medium. Top-end consolidated (ServiceTitan/FieldRoutes, EverCommerce/Briostack, WorkWave/PestPac) but long tail untouched.
- **People + Platform Fit:** Strong. Software alone insufficient without compliance advisory, regulatory update services, training content, audit prep support.
- **Synergy:** Adjacent to Premium Pest Management (already Active-Outreach). Same customer base, different angle (software vs. services).

### 6. Insurance Compliance Platforms -- FAIL (Overlap)
- **Margins:** Strong
- **Recurring Revenue:** High
- **Industry Growth:** Strong (9.1% CAGR)
- **Growth TAM:** Pass. US insurance compliance software ~$1.4-2.7B.
- **Why it failed:** Insurance Producer License Compliance already on tracker with folder in WEEKLY REVIEW. AgentSync raised $161M at $1.2B valuation -- VC-dominated space. Vertafore/Sircon is the dominant incumbent. Already covered.
