# Validation Contacts: Specialty Coffee Equipment Service

Pre-sprint validation for the Specialty Coffee Equipment Service niche (advanced to WEEKLY REVIEW 2026-04-19, score 2.55/3.0). Purpose: stress-test the risk assumptions in §8 of the one-pager ([[outputs/2026-04-19-specialty-coffee-equipment-service-niche-onepager]]) before handing the niche to Sam's DealsX outreach sprint.

## Risks to Validate

1. **OEM direct-service disintermediation (residual)** — one-pager concludes Italian-HQ OEMs (La Marzocco, Slayer/Cimbali, Nuova Simonelli) structurally cannot run US direct service, but US-origin OEMs (Synesso Seattle, Modbar Indiana) are called out as a live residual risk. Questions: (a) Are La Marzocco USA, Slayer, or Synesso adding direct-service capacity in NYC/SF/LA/Chicago/Seattle in the next 24 months? (b) If so, are they displacing independent dealers entirely, or running a hybrid model that keeps independents in the long tail?

2. **Talent constraint — growth ceiling, not just a hiring nuisance** — §8 claims master-level espresso techs are scarce and apprenticeship pipelines are the bottleneck. Questions: (a) What is the realistic hire rate for a shop trying to add 1-2 master techs per year in a tier-1 metro? (b) Is factory certification (La Marzocco USA, Slayer) the binding constraint, or is it the multi-brand diagnostic experience that only accrues on the job?

3. **Customer concentration — chain accounts as double-edged** — §8 flags hotel-chain / cafe-chain accounts as both growth engine and key-account risk. Questions: (a) When a Soho House or Aman signs a multi-property service contract, do they sign one national preferred provider or multi-metro regional providers? (b) Do they run RFPs every 2-3 years or does the incumbent keep the account on reputation?

4. **Willingness to switch providers under new ownership** — §9 validation protocol requires confirming that high-end coffee shop owners and hotel F&B directors would switch to an acquired shop under new (G&B) ownership. Questions: (a) What's the actual switching cost (tech relationship, parts inventory carry-over, water filter contract entanglement)? (b) Does the lead tech leaving trigger account loss regardless of ownership, or is the service-company brand sticky once the machines are commissioned?

5. **Tech-forward competitive dynamic** — §8 names Espresso Parts, Pull Caffe, and "others" as tech-forward startups organizing the market via online parts + tools. Risk: G&B could roll up at the same time a VC-backed consolidator emerges and wins the category. Questions: (a) Are any of these competitors pursuing acquisition-led roll-ups vs organic marketplace models? (b) What's the real market share of online-parts marketplaces vs direct dealer-ordering from OEMs?
