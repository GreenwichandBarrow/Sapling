# TheDealSheet PE-POV Cross-Reference Memo

Synthesis across 5 TheDealSheet industry deep-dives (plus 2 already analyzed today: Commercial Cleaning, Pest Control) against G&B's 6-niche portfolio.

## Bottom line

**TDS's PE-POV validates G&B's portfolio discipline.** All three rejected niches (homecare, landscaping, laundromat) confirmed as correctly rejected on thesis-alignment grounds, not just execution grounds. The two variation niches (insurance → Specialty Insurance, restaurant → Coffee Equip Servicing) hold up against the generic PE view — G&B's sub-niching is sharper than TDS's consolidated analysis.

**One strategic takeaway:** G&B's 6 active niches sit inside thesis space that the PE-POV data independently validates. The portfolio isn't over-selected or wishful.

**One adjacent opportunity surfaced:** Commercial Kitchen Equipment Servicing (Hobart/Vulcan/True/Hoshizaki/Rational) — same DNA as Coffee Equip Servicing but 10-20x installed base. Worth niche-intelligence pass if portfolio expansion is on the table post-Saturday.

**One competitive sharpening:** Hub International is the ONLY real competitor in G&B's Specialty Insurance layer. BroadStreet/Hilb/King Risk/AJG roll up generic P&C — irrelevant noise in the generic TDS framing.

## Section 1 — Rejected niches (confirmations)

### Laundromat — REJECTION CONFIRMED
- $6.8B market, **1.6% CAGR declining to flat/negative post-2030**
- 28-35% EBITDA margins (real, but in shrinking category)
- **PE explicitly fails to scale**: "margins don't improve materially at 50 locations vs 2"
- **No contracted recurring revenue** (90% repeat is behavioral, not underwritable)
- Consumer-retail customer base (renters, 67% of urban housing) fails G&B's luxury/HV-asset filter
- Named PE platforms are search-fund tier (CSC ServiceWorks, SuperSuds, Laundry Growth Partners)
- **Note:** luxury hospitality linen service and specialty textile restoration are adjacent industries, not laundromat sub-niches. Commercial laundry equipment service remains PASSED per 4/19 session decisions.

### Landscaping — REJECTION CONFIRMED (firmly)
- EBITDA 10-15% industry / 18-22% top quartile — **fails G&B's 25% services floor**
- PE saturated: **6 consolidators already built** (Shoreline/American Landscaping Partners, Ares/Landscape Workshop, Brentwood+Tenex/Perennial, Wind Point/ExperiGreen, SavATree, Riverside/U.S. Lawns). 76% of 2025 deals are PE.
- **Labor crisis permanent**: 171,600 open roles, 31% turnover, 20% projected wage inflation through 2029, H-2B gap (97K requested / <65K approved)
- Structural seasonality: 49% of operators do seasonal layoffs; 50%+ Northern revenue variance
- No G&B-shaped sub-niche; arboriculture (SavATree archetype) inherits core labor dynamics
- **Investor-conversation angle**: landscaping's failure modes are the mirror-image of what makes Storage for HV Assets work. Useful framing on thesis discipline.

### Homecare — REJECTION CONFIRMED (narrow revisit flag)
- EBITDA 10-15% industry / 18-25% top quartile — **fails G&B's 25% floor**. Only hospice sub-segment clears (25-35% gross) but is specialized clinical, not search-fund ops.
- Labor structurally broken: 79% caregiver turnover (BLS 2024), 765K unfilled openings/yr, 18% YoY wage growth, 59% of agencies report shortages — **inverse of Premium Pest / Coffee Equip tech stickiness**
- PE field crowded: 8 platforms (Altocare/Waud, Enhabit/Kinderhook $1.1B Feb 2026, Amedisys/Optum $3.3B, Addus public, Havencrest, Rallyday, Help At Home, Choice Health)
- Regulatory headwind: 1.3% CMS rate cut 2026, OASIS-E1, HHCAHPS, EVV mandates
- **Revisit flag (NOT recommended as DealsX next-wave trigger):** private-pay concierge home care for HNW seniors ($4-8K/mo, 5-8x EBITDA premium). Absence from PE consolidation trackers suggests not yet a roll-up vertical. Requires primary research (HCAOA data, private-pay agency interviews) before reopening.

## Section 2 — Variations (gap analysis)

### Specialty Insurance Brokerage — VARIATION HOLDS WITH SHARPENING

**G&B's sub-niche thesis is sharper than TDS's generic P&C view.** Three specific sharpening opportunities:

**Transferable benchmarks from TDS:**
- Valuation bands: $1.25M-$5M = 3.2x-3.8x SDE (G&B's sub-$3M buy-box sits here). **25%+ specialty-revenue mix unlocks 4.0x-6.0x premium** — single most important multiple rule for G&B's variation
- Market size: $261.7B, 4.14% CAGR. Specialty/MGA slice = 9% of market, 5.92% CAGR. **Cyber 27% CAGR through 2026.**
- Succession wave: 30,000+ sub-$1.25M aging independents, ~50% workforce retirement by 2028, 400K retirements by 2026
- Economics floor: median $195K SDE, $650K median sale — scorecard floor should require SDE ≥$195K
- M&A velocity: 695 deals YTD 2025; PE = 72% of buyers; 90% of insurers expect more M&A in 2026

**Where G&B's thesis is sharper than TDS:**
1. **Compliance as moat, not cost** — TDS treats as $2-8K/yr drag. In specialty lines it's a relationship moat generic P&C roll-ups can't absorb
2. **Single-line focus = premium, not discount** — TDS's discount rule (single-line P&C = 2.0-2.5x) inverts for deep specialty expertise
3. **Hub International is the ONLY real competitor in G&B's layer** — 750 HNW advisors, 70K HNW clients, explicit fine-art + jewelry focus. BroadStreet (57 deals YTD), Hilb/Carlyle (200+), King Risk, AJG roll up GENERIC regional P&C — irrelevant to G&B's variation. TDS's generic framing obscures this.

**Blind spots to incorporate into one-pager:**
- **Cyber UHNW at 27% CAGR** deserves standalone sub-line narrative (currently listed co-equal among 11 sub-industries)
- **12-18 month valuation-ceiling drift risk** — specialty books may drift from 3.2x-3.8x → 4.0x-4.5x SDE as Hub/AmWINS/Risk Strategies price up. Fiscal-year urgency.
- **NY = avoid for domicile** (no license reciprocity, 40% higher compliance, PE saturation). Sharpens "Midwest-acquire / NY-open" strategy. Don't acquire NY-domiciled agency.
- **89% of new agents quit within 3yr + specialty underwriters rarer** → operator succession HARDER in specialty. Sharpens "institutional client relationships, not owner-is-the-book" filter.
- **Embedded insurance $116.5B** (jewelry retailer embedded, auction-house embedded transit) — future bolt-on lens.

### Restaurant (→ Coffee Equip Servicing) — THESIS HOLDS, adjacent opportunity

**Relevance verdict:** INDIRECT SIGNAL. TDS restaurant page has zero equipment-service coverage — it's all operator economics.

**Validation:**
- Fine dining + independent full-service stay **78.6% independent** — G&B's specialty-coffee / fine-dining / hotel customer base remains decentralized
- PE consolidation concentrated in QSR / fast casual (Roark, Blackstone, Franchise Equity Partners, Thompson Street) — different equipment stack from specialty espresso
- Full-service 11.07% CAGR 2026-31 = expanding installed base needing service
- TDS treats "deferred maintenance / equipment end-of-life" as valuation discount factor — implies chronic under-investment, validating scheduled-PM demand

**Watch item:** Franchise Equity Partners acquired 7 Brew Coffee — first PE drive-thru coffee move. Different equipment from specialty espresso OEMs (La Marzocco, Nuova Simonelli, Gruppo Cimbali, Slayer, Sanremo, Rancilio). Not yet threatening, but flag if roll-ups expand to specialty concepts.

**Adjacent opportunity flagged:** **Commercial Kitchen Equipment Servicing** (Hobart / Vulcan / True / Hoshizaki / Rational) — same structural DNA as Coffee Equip Servicing:
- OEM authorization moat
- Scheduled PM recurring revenue
- Emergency service pricing power
- **10-20x installed base vs coffee**

**Reframing from 4/19 session decisions:** 4/19 passed on "fine-dining commercial kitchen" (bad end-customer economics). TDS's framing is broader (all commercial kitchens, not fine-dining-specific). If portfolio expansion is on the table post-Saturday, this is the niche-intelligence evaluation worth running — broader customer base than the rejected 4/19 framing.
