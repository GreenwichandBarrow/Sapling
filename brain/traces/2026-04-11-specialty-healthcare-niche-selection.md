### Female-led specialty filter layered onto broad healthcare SaaS niche
**Reasoning:** "Women's health software" alone was too narrow (~60-100 vendors) to hand to Sam. Broadening to Specialty Healthcare PM/EHR (1,500-2,500+ vendors) gave DealsX enough volume to actually source. Kay's right-to-win is preserved via a *filter* (behavioral health, PT, home health, OB/GYN, women-led clinical specialties) applied inside the broader niche, not by narrowing the niche itself. This separates sourcing breadth from conviction filter.
**Trigger:** When Kay's niche pull is too narrow for DealsX volume, broaden the sourcing niche and encode her right-to-win as a sub-filter inside it — never hand DealsX a niche so narrow it can't produce 200+ targets.

### Compliance-driven SaaS is a filter, not a niche
**Reasoning:** "Compliance-Driven System-of-Record Vertical Software" describes how Kay selects sub-verticals (Jake Stoller/Riverside framework). It is NOT a niche Sam searches. Confusing filter with niche would send DealsX on an impossible Apollo query.
**Trigger:** When documenting thesis layers, explicitly label each as "filter" (Kay applies during sub-vertical selection) or "niche" (Sam searches in Apollo/LinkedIn). Niche-intelligence skill must enforce this distinction.

### REJECT InsurTech despite thesis alignment: VC contamination
**Reasoning:** Insurance agency management software fits the compliance-driven filter on paper. Rejected because the acquirable universe is too thin — most companies of the right size are VC-backed, meaning wrong valuations and wrong seller motivations. Thesis fit ≠ acquirable universe.
**Trigger:** For every thesis-aligned niche, run a VC-contamination check before approval. If >60% of size-appropriate companies took institutional VC, reject regardless of fit.

### REJECT Field Service despite highest volume: low defensibility
**Reasoning:** 5-8K vendors is tempting but Riverside defensibility score was lowest (75/100) and ServiceTitan is actively consolidating. Volume alone is not opportunity when a dominant consolidator is removing exit optionality.
**Trigger:** Vendor count is a necessary-not-sufficient signal. Always score against a defensibility framework and check for active consolidators before approving a niche.

## Learnings
- niche-intelligence: Encode "filter vs niche" distinction + VC-contamination check + active-consolidator check as explicit screens
- target-discovery: Within a broad niche, apply Kay's right-to-win as a ranking filter on the target list (not a narrowing of the search)
