### Adam Aisen (ECP) had no senior-care operator background yet succeeded
**Reasoning:** Adam Aisen acquired ECP (assisted living software) and grew it through a 5-year hold to a Level Equity exit at ~15x ARR. His right-to-win was NOT industry-operator experience — it was adjacent tech fluency (Google) + sector pattern recognition (prior healthcare tech investing). Kay initially asked whether the Senior Living & Home Care Ops Software niche was still viable given incumbent dominance (PointClickCare, MatrixCare, WellSky). Adam's precedent validates the answer: long tail is real, sub-scale vendors are acquirable, and right-to-win can come from adjacent expertise.
**Trigger:** When evaluating DealsX SaaS niches, accept three forms of right-to-win: (1) industry-operator experience (traditional), (2) adjacent tech/sector expertise (Adam's model), (3) investor/network credibility for pattern recognition. Kay's right-to-win for Specialty Healthcare is combination of (2) and (3) — women's health nonprofit credibility + finance/PE pattern recognition + luxury operations for premium-service customer segments.

### Don't auto-kill niches on incumbent concentration alone
**Reasoning:** Senior Living software has PointClickCare (~22-26% share) and MatrixCare/WellSky as PE-backed consolidators — heavy Porter's drag on the G&B scorecard (1.60). Under the old frame, this looks disqualifying. Under Adam's precedent, it's the OPPOSITE signal: majors existing validates the category is structurally defensible; long tail exists because the category is too big to consolidate fast; majors are also Kay's exit buyers.
**Trigger:** For SaaS niches, evaluate three separate questions: (1) Are incumbents squeezing the long tail faster than you can acquire? (kill only if yes), (2) Is there a sub-scale acquirable universe >15 vendors at $3-8M ARR? (go if yes), (3) Do incumbents acquire mid-market tuck-ins at 10-18x ARR? (strong exit optionality if yes). Premature kill on "there are big players" loses real opportunities.

## Learnings
- niche-intelligence: Validation contacts step (5b) should identify Adam-pattern precedents (search-fund or LMM PE acquirers within the same category) as the strongest defensibility data point. If no precedents exist, flag as higher-risk niche.
- scorecard: Porter's Five Forces category may need a "incumbent-as-exit-buyer" offsetting factor in future calibration, but NOT a structural rework — that would break the filter/scorecard separation.
- target-discovery: Kay's right-to-win filter should match Adam's template — accept adjacent tech + sector pattern recognition, not just direct industry operator experience.
