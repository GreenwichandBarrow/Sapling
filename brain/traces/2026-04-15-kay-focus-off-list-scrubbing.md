### Kay's operational focus is shifting off list-scrubbing and manual email outreach
**Reasoning:** On 2026-04-15, Kay said: "I need to get out of list scrubbing and that kind of email outreach for the moment." Context: she had just asked to push all non-JJ SaaS/luxury niches through DealsX (5/6 launch), and simultaneously demoted Private Art Advisory from Active-Outreach to Active-Long Term — not because the niche is bad, but because "it's not worth my focus right now."

This is a **time-allocation decision**, not a niche-quality decision. Kay is consolidating her high-leverage hours onto judgment work (C-suite agent architecture, deal evaluation, investor relationships, post-LOI DD when it arrives) and letting DealsX absorb the list-management workload.

**Trigger:**
- **COO:** default new niche workflows to DealsX-routable paths whenever possible. Kay-Email route only when there's a specific reason (e.g., Kay's personal relationship network is the moat). Don't propose expanding Kay-Email niches without flagging the focus cost.
- **CMO:** Kay's voice-review bandwidth is scarce. Batch outreach drafts for review rather than surfacing one-by-one. Favor DealsX's universal copy (Kay reviewed once, scales) over per-niche bespoke copy.
- **outreach-manager:** For any new niche, default to DealsX routing if it fits Sam's 6 buckets. Only propose Kay-Email route with an explicit right-to-win argument.
- **target-discovery:** Never run Kay-Email-route list-building tasks that require Kay's manual approval > 10 targets/week. If target pool exceeds that, route to DealsX or flag for tabling.

**What this does NOT mean:**
- Kay is NOT retreating from owner conversations once DealsX starts booking them. Those are judgment-heavy and stay with her.
- Kay is NOT abandoning existing warm relationships (Ashley Emerole, Dan Tanzilli, Kate Reibel, etc.). Those stay in relationship-manager as nurture contacts.
- Kay is NOT killing niches — Art Advisory went to Active-Long Term (finish cadences with warm referrals), not Tabled. The warm path stays open; it just isn't actively fed.

## The pattern to protect
Kay's day should be mostly:
1. Morning briefing review (20 min)
2. Owner calls, investor calls, team calls (scheduled)
3. Judgment calls surfaced by C-suite agents (15-30 min, as they arise)
4. Deep work on system architecture, new niches, exit planning

Kay's day should NOT be:
- Scrubbing target lists row-by-row
- Hand-drafting cold emails at volume
- Re-reviewing Attio contacts one at a time
- Any high-toil, low-judgment work that could be agent-handled or Sam-handled

If the briefing or any skill surfaces work that would put Kay in the wrong mode, the agent should flag it ("this is list-scrub work — route to DealsX or skip?") rather than queue it.

## Learnings
- "Not worth my focus" is a first-class reason to demote a niche. It doesn't mean the niche is bad; it means Kay's time is scarcer than the niche's upside.
- DealsX is a time-leverage play, not a pipeline-volume play. The value is that Kay reviews copy once and spot-checks lists once per niche — not that more emails go out.
- Every new workflow proposed in the system should answer: "Does this add to Kay's toil, or remove from it?"
