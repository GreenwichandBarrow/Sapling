# Trace: Relationship-Time ROI Filter

## Trigger

Kay reviewed today's inbound (Mark Gardella reply with 2 warm intros + meeting ask, Nikki Higgins reply, Amanda Lo Iacono event invite) and observed:

> "Notice all the engagement on these spaces where I have been looking. No deals though. I want to engage with all but my learning from the year is I've spent a lot of time schmoozing that isn't turning into deals."

## Decision

Establish a formal relationship-time ROI filter. De-prioritize insurance niche for active cycles. Decline all three today (Mark call + 2 intros, Amanda event, Nikki west-coast offer). Set 14-day LOI target (5/1) to force focus.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Continue current networking pattern** — engage all warm outreach, hope for owner-layer to emerge from ecosystem connections over time.
2. **Partial filter — only decline obvious peer chatter (Nikki, Amanda), keep Mark's intros** — the usual move, preserves optionality.
3. **Full filter — decline all three, apply Gate A/B test to all future contacts, de-prioritize insurance cycle entirely** — chosen.

Option 1 was the default behavior that produced a year of zero conversions. Option 2 felt safe but would perpetuate the pattern (JoAnne/David are wrong layer; adding them to the nurture queue would compound, not solve). Option 3 was the honest response to a clear pattern.

## Reasoning

### The ecosystem-vs-owner pattern

All four active-engagement contacts today were ecosystem-side, not owner-side:
- Mark Gardella (ZI Advisor CEO, ex-Sertis founder) — industry expert, not a seller.
- JoAnne Artesani (Sproutr) — insurance platform startup founder. Peer.
- David Gritz (InsurTech NY / MGA Labs / small fund) — incubator + VC. Peer.
- Nikki Higgins (Jet Aviation sales) — aircraft management salesperson. Wrong side of the table entirely.

Pattern across Kay's year of networking: wide ecosystem reach (platform builders, connectors, service providers, fellow investors) but shallow owner-side connection. Ecosystem people give validation, education, and peer respect. Owners give deals.

### The startup-vs-mature layer sharpening (Kay's addition)

CIO's initial framing was "peer, no seller access." Kay sharpened: JoAnne and David aren't even "industry experts who know the right space" — they're **startup ecosystem**. They operate in the MGA-startup / insurtech incubator / VC-fund-invests-in-MGAs world. Kay acquires mature $5-20M businesses. Different industry entirely, not just different role.

Mark himself, by offering these two intros as his best cards, reveals his own network is startup-oriented. His new MGA platform model (the call he wanted to discuss) is also startup-world. Same asymmetry.

### The filter (Gate A + Gate B)

Before investing >15 min with any new contact:

- **Gate A:** Can this person make a named, personal intro to a business owner of a MATURE, transactable business (not startup / platform / fund portfolio co) within 5 years of a transition?
- **Gate B:** Will they do it within 30 days, unprompted by Kay?

Fail either → industry expert. 30-min capped, no recurring calendar, no follow-up unless intro lands in 30 days. The 30-day clock exposes which category a person actually belongs to — river guides make introductions because they want to; ecosystem people talk about it and don't deliver.

### Time-weight pyramid

- **River guide** (named-intro, active owner-side relationships): 60% / up to 6 hrs/wk
- **Industry expert** (knows the space, no direct seller access): 20% / 30-min capped
- **Peer/validation/ecosystem:** 20% / only when they initiate

Kay's pyramid was inverted — 60-70% ecosystem, starving the river-guide tier. Re-balancing.

### Insurance niche verdict

CIO flagged: insurance fails G&B Charter (not luxury/prestige, not operationally critical B2B to luxury businesses). A year of wide outreach without conversion is signal, not bad luck. Sunk cost.

Policy: stop active outreach cycles in insurance. If a deal surfaces via warm intro that clears the buy box, evaluate on numbers. Don't chase.

### Exit ramp script (for de-escalating over-invested ecosystem relationships)

> "I've really appreciated this — I'm in a phase where I'm going deep on a smaller number of relationships before our deal closes, so I'll be less visible for a bit. I'll absolutely reach back out when things shift."

Non-transactional, sets focus as the reason, leaves door open. Used in today's Mark decline (adapted to Kay's preferred frame: "For the next 12 months I'm heads-down on acquiring, not starting").

## Why This Trace Matters

Future agents evaluating relationship investment decisions should apply Gate A + Gate B before recommending calendar time with any new contact. The rule was implicit in `feedback_river_guides_not_intermediaries.md` but lacked the 30-day unprompted delivery test and the startup-vs-mature layer distinction. Those two additions change the call on a large class of contacts (anyone working in startup/platform/VC-adjacent roles) who otherwise would have been mis-classified as "industry expert" and given too much time.

The 14-day LOI target is a forcing function layered on top: for the next 14 days, no relationship investment above Tier 1 (river guide with active owner-side rolodex) is justified.

## Key Insight

> "I've spent a lot of time schmoozing that isn't turning into deals."

Ecosystem engagement is not a leading indicator of deal flow. It's often a lagging indicator of *the wrong focus*. When a year produces zero conversions from a category, that's signal. The courage move is to stop, not to try harder at the same thing.
