# Trace: Saltoun Software Multiple Compression Signal

## Trigger

On Saturday 4/18, Kay reported a conversation with Andrew Saltoun (Saltoun Capital Partners) in which he said he'd "have to be very hard pressed to invest in another software deal." The reason: he is currently prepping a software business for sale and the valuation feedback coming back is so compressed that as an allocator he's pulling back from the category.

## Decision

Treat the Saltoun signal as primary sell-side market data confirming Kay's 4/16 AI-native-vs-AI-enhanced stress test from the opposite side of the transaction.

**Software filter for G&B tightened:** future software targets must satisfy all three —
1. Physical-workflow protected (AI-native substitution structurally blocked)
2. Recurring revenue already growing faster than overall revenue (Andrew Freiman frame from 4/2025 Kay investor call — already in use; Saltoun signal reinforces)
3. Purchase price cheap enough that today's compressed exit comp is already priced in (new constraint)

**AI-native-exposed niches flagged for pressure-test** (mapping to Kay's 4/16 documentation-heavy concern):
- Healthcare Regulatory Compliance SaaS
- Digital Accessibility Compliance Services
- Home Health Agency Software
- Behavioral Health Practice Management

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Treat as one investor's sentiment, no scoring change.** The default. Rejected — Saltoun is not a cap-table investor speculating; he is actively in-market getting offers. Sentiment ≠ price discovery; his data is price discovery.

2. **Blanket pause on all software sourcing.** Rejected as over-rotation. Not all software is equally AI-exposed. Physical-workflow-protected verticals (vet, funeral, childcare, PT, IVF, senior living, luxury asset services) still pass the substitution test.

3. **Tighten filter on three dimensions + keep sourcing live with the tighter filter** — chosen. Preserves optionality on defensible software while ensuring the sprint / pipeline isn't over-indexed on what the market is actively discounting.

## Reasoning

The distinguishing move is treating this as **market data**, not sentiment. Kay's cap-table investor Andrew Freiman (different Andrew) already coached her on ROIC and recurring-revenue-growing-faster frames in April 2025. Saltoun is an independent data point from the exit side. Two independent investors converging on "software is risky right now" from different vantage points is a structural signal, not noise.

The Saltoun signal also retroactively validates Kay's 4/16 AI-native-vs-AI-enhanced stress test. She identified documentation-heavy SaaS verticals as exposed; Saltoun confirms the market is now pricing that risk into exit comps. Her framework was correct before the market moved — now the market is agreeing with her.

The three-condition filter (physical-workflow + recurring-growing + compressed-comp-priced-in) collapses three previously independent lenses (Kay's substitution test, Freiman's ROIC test, Saltoun's valuation test) into one compound test. Any target that fails any condition is a weaker bet than the same target would have been six months ago.

## Why This Trace Matters

Without this trace, a future agent scoring a software target might:
- Weight software neutrally against services (pre-Saltoun default)
- Accept "recurring revenue = good" as sufficient (pre-Freiman default)
- Not know to pressure-test physical-workflow protection as a first-line filter (pre-4/16 default)

With this trace, the agent sees the three lenses as cumulative, not alternatives, and knows the market (not just Kay) is now enforcing the compound filter.

## Key Insight

Sell-side data from an insider actively in a transaction outranks investor sentiment and broker-side chatter. Two Andrews converging on "software is compressed" from opposite sides of the deal table (cap-table investor coaching + active seller) is the moment the thesis hardens into a scoring rule.

**Filter hierarchy for software going forward:**
1. Physical-workflow protection (binary pass/fail)
2. Recurring revenue growing faster than overall revenue (quantitative, Freiman frame)
3. Purchase multiple discounted enough that current compressed exit comp leaves upside (Saltoun-derived)

Fail any → de-prioritize. Pass all three → defensible even in a compressed multiple environment.
