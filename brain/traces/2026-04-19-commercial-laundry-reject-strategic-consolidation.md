## Trigger

During an exploration of "asset-light equipment service" niches as alternatives to the rejected linen service model (asset-heavy capex). CIO initially saw Commercial Laundry Equipment Service as a structural twin to HVAC / pool / elevator service roll-ups. Kay asked the sharp question: "but PE hasn't touched this area? why not?" Triggered a 20-minute desk check that flipped the thesis.

## Decision

**PASS / TABLE Commercial Laundry Equipment Service.** Not a G&B fit now. Re-check in 12 months post-Alliance-IPO settlement.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Proceed despite consolidation risk, target multi-brand non-Alliance dealers.** Rejected — the Alliance share (~60%) is too large to acquire around, and the multi-brand independents are exactly what EVI is also targeting.
- **Go after dealers in secondary markets where Alliance direct can't reach.** Rejected — EVI's 32-deal track record includes secondary markets; no geographic safe-haven.
- **Narrow to luxury hotel laundry only.** Rejected — luxury hotel subset is ~50-100 service firms US, fails target-count floor, still exposed to Alliance OEM risk.

## Reasoning

Desk-check findings (via general-purpose web research subagent, sourced from EVI investor relations, PrivSource, American Laundry News, Distribution Strategy Group, Mordor Intelligence):

**Consolidation already advanced:**
- **EVI Industries (NYSE: EVI)** — 32 dealer acquisitions since 2016. Buy-and-build platform specifically targeting commercial laundry equipment distribution. Largest recent: Girbau North America (~$43-50M ARR) in FY25. Public strategic pays above searcher multiples.
- **Alliance Laundry Systems** — dominant OEM (60%+ share), BDT/MSD-owned since 2015, IPO'd Oct 2025 at $836.8M H1 revenue. Going direct aggressively — acquired Star Distributing (Nashville), Parts King, Metropolitan Laundry Machinery Sales, LES Hagerstown, Bestway Laundry Solutions, Statewide Machinery since 2023-24. Sales force +6% to 190 reps. Service techs +5% to ~400.
- **Southeastern Laundry Equipment Sales** — took 4612 Group equity Feb 2024, doing its own rollup (acquired Cochrane Repair Oct 2024).
- **No searcher has acquired a commercial laundry equipment dealer** — absence itself is signal. EVI's 32-deal track record prices searchers out.

**OEM channel risk is existential:**
- Alliance's direct-channel strategy disintermediates its own dealer network. Any dealer with Alliance concentration (most of the market) faces 3-5yr channel compression.
- Industry press (National Laundry Equipment) flags direct competition between Alliance and its third-party dealers.

**End-market drag:**
- Laundromat count -0.7% YoY (IBISWorld, 2025), -0.2% avg 2020-2025. Volume end of market shrinking.
- Hotel laundry mixed: ~65% outsource but luxury / chain going in-house (tailwind) — narrower end-customer subset than it first appears.

## Why This Trace Matters

The surface pattern — fragmented market, recurring service contracts, HVAC-like economics — initially looked like a clean searcher-scale opportunity. The **underlying** pattern is a category being consolidated by two different strategic buyers simultaneously (EVI horizontally across dealers + Alliance vertically through direct-channel expansion). Both are better-capitalized than G&B's check size, both are pricing the market.

**Reusable heuristic for future niche evaluation:**
1. Before getting excited about a fragmented service category with strong recurring revenue, check for roll-up activity: named sponsors, named deals, named public strategics.
2. If you find EVI-style accumulators (10+ deals in <5 years), the window is likely closing for searcher-scale acquisition.
3. Separately check the dominant OEM's channel strategy: if OEM is going direct, dealer channel has terminal compression timer.
4. **No searcher activity** in a structurally-attractive-looking category is itself a warning sign, not a greenfield opportunity.

The "PE hasn't touched this area" instinct Kay articulated was the right question — not because it was true, but because the answer (it HAS, you just hadn't found the evidence yet) is what killed the thesis.

## Key Insight

**"PE-untouched + fragmented + recurring = obvious opportunity" is a trap.** If a category looks that clean, either (a) the economics are actually bad behind the surface, or (b) PE has touched it in a way your search missed. Run the 20-minute M&A desk check before investing more thinking.
