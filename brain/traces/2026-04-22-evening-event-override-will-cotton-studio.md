# Decision Trace: Accepting an Evening Event — Will Cotton Studio, May 12

## Context

[[entities/amanda-lo-iacono|Amanda Lo Iacono]] emailed Kay an invitation to "Women Shaping the Art World," a 5-7pm Tuesday May 12 networking event at Rose Dergan + [[entities/will-cotton|Will Cotton]]'s home and studio, 14 Harrison Street, Tribeca. Co-hosted by Rose Dergan on behalf of Elizabeth Goldberg, Amanda Lo Iacono, Annette Schwaer, and Amy Wexler. Target audience: women across art, advisory, gallery, auction, finance, law, marketing.

Kay has a durable [[memory/feedback_no_evening_conferences|daytime-events-only rule]] (`feedback_no_evening_conferences.md`): *"Daytime events only, flag timing before registration."*

## Decisions

### Rule-softening under strategic-convergence override

**AI proposed:** RECOMMEND YES, attend. Justification: (a) 5-7pm is a soft-evening window, not a late-night event; (b) the audience is Kay's exact thesis convergence zone — women across art / finance / advisory — which is nearly impossible to replicate in a daytime-only filter; (c) Will Cotton is a serious contemporary artist (Katy Perry *California Gurls* set, collected widely), the venue is a prestige art-world location, not generic networking; (d) potential river-guide relevance for the recently reactivated Art Storage niche, and separately for Art Advisory.

**Chosen:** Accepted. Kay did not push back on the RECOMMEND, did not invoke the daytime rule, and approved the draft acceptance with minimal edits.

**Reasoning:** The daytime-only rule exists to protect Kay from low-value evening events that consume family time. An evening event that delivers (a) hard-to-replicate audience density, (b) multi-niche relevance (Art Storage + Art Advisory + broader luxury), and (c) prestige-venue access is not the category the rule was designed to exclude. The rule is a soft filter, not an absolute.

**Pattern:** #pattern/rule-softening-with-reason — when a durable rule exists, default to applying it, but surface the override candidate with an explicit reasoning package so Kay can accept or veto with context. Do not silently drop the rule, and do not rigidly apply it without surfacing the trade-off.

### Voice: "the kind of room I love being in" — rejected as cheesy

**AI proposed:** Draft line: *"The mix of women across the art field, finance, and advisory is exactly the kind of room I love being in."*

**Chosen:** Rejected by Kay: *"that line is very cheesy."* Final draft stripped the explicit enthusiasm and kept a neutral "sounds like a wonderful evening / I'd love to come."

**Reasoning:** Kay's voice rules favor warm-but-understated acceptance, not explicit room-labeling. Calling out the strategic audience makeup in a reply to the host can read as transactional — like Kay is telling Amanda why the room matters to her, instead of just accepting graciously.

**Pattern:** #pattern/voice-understated-warmth — strategic rationale belongs in internal notes, not in the thank-you reply. When Kay finds a line "cheesy," strip the explicit evaluation of the event's value from the acceptance copy.

## Learnings

### For the evening-event rule going forward

1. **The daytime-only rule has an exception shape:** cross-sector HNWI convergence audiences at prestige venues where the audience mix is rare. When all three conditions hold, surface as RECOMMEND YES with the reasoning package. Kay will decide.
2. **Conditions that do NOT trigger the exception:** single-sector networking (even in her niches), evening-only cocktail formats at generic venues, events where "the right people will also be there in the daytime somewhere."
3. **Use the RECOMMEND framing.** Don't auto-decline evening events without surfacing the trade-off — Kay reads them faster than Claude can pre-filter, and she wants the decision in her hands.

### For the May 12 event specifically

4. **Prep triggers a meeting-brief task around May 8-10.** Claude should pull dossiers on all four co-hosts (Rose Dergan, Elizabeth Goldberg, Amanda Lo Iacono, Annette Schwaer, Amy Wexler) before May 12. Relevant for: art-advisory river guides, art-storage river guides, and broader luxury-women network.
5. **Check if Amanda Lo Iacono is tied to fine-art logistics / storage.** If so, this invitation is also a direct entry point into the reactivated Art Storage niche's river guide layer. Verify before the event.

### For draft-reply voice

6. **Skip explicit room-value labeling in acceptance copy.** Thank-you replies should be warm and understated. Save "exactly the kind of room I'd love to be in" framing for internal notes and meeting-briefs, not outbound.
