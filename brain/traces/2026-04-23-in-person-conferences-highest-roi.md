# Trace: In-Person Conferences Are the Highest-ROI Channel

## Context

Kay committed to a 1-in-person-conference-per-week cadence starting 2026-04-14. Week 1 was a soft run. Week 2 was XPX (Exit Planning Exchange) on 2026-04-23. At XPX, Matthew Luczyk (SVP, Head of Corporate Advisory, Peapack Private) pitched Kay a NY Metro aerospace/defense business — founder-led, widow-owned, absentee-run, strong contracts, legacy-over-multiples seller.

Kay observed mid-session: "In this second week, in the second conference, we have a deal being pitched to us. Prioritizing in person is 100% the correct plan for us."

This trace locks the observation as strategic doctrine.

## Decisions

### Defend the 1-in-person-conference/week cadence against all calendar pressure.
**APPROVE:** Conferences are the top-of-funnel for legacy-motivated sellers via their advisors. The advisor/intermediary layer does not forward deals to LinkedIn inbox cold-email recipients. They forward deals to people they have met and trust. In-person meeting is the unlock.

**APPROVE:** When proposing conferences (conference-discovery skill), weight in-person meaningfully higher than virtual or digital-equivalent sourcing. Don't let digital channel expansion (DealsX, Apollo outreach, JJ cold calls) cannibalize Kay's conference bandwidth.

**APPROVE:** When a deal surfaces at a conference, treat follow-up as same-day priority. The warm in-person intro is the unlock; stale warm intros cool fast.

**APPROVE:** Use this pattern in trade-offs. If Kay is deciding between a conference and a lower-value deliverable, the conference wins. If Kay is deciding between two conferences, prioritize intermediary density (XPX-style — exit planners, M&A, wealth, CPAs) over owner-direct trade shows.

## Alternatives Considered

- **Scale conferences up to 2/week now.** Rejected. The engine (Claude, JJ, DealsX) is still absorbing the 1/wk cadence outputs (8 cards needed a skill built to process). Scale up only after sustained throughput is demonstrated.
- **Substitute with virtual conference attendance.** Rejected. The conversion evidence is specifically from in-person (Matthew Luczyk wouldn't have pitched an aerospace deal to a Zoom face in a breakout room). Virtual is lower ROI and can't replace the cadence.
- **Replace conferences with scaled cold outreach (DealsX + JJ).** Rejected. DealsX + JJ are parallel channels, not substitutes. Cold channels bring quantity; conferences bring quality referrals from intermediaries. Both matter.

## Reasoning

**The conversion arithmetic is striking.** 2 conferences produced 1 live deal pitch. That's a 50% conversion rate at the conference level — and each conference brings Kay into contact with 8-20 intermediaries who are now in the network. No digital channel comes close to this at comparable size.

**The referral mechanics are specific to in-person context.** Intermediaries refer deals to people they remember. They don't remember cold emails; they remember conversations. A business card exchanged in person, followed by a 24-hour thank-you with the buy-box paragraph, becomes a standing reference they can pull up next time a deal crosses their desk. Digital channels don't produce this anchoring.

**The cadence is self-reinforcing.** Every conference produces intermediary follow-ups, which become river-guide candidates, which become ongoing deal sources. Missing a week doesn't just miss that week's deal — it breaks the compounding of the network.

## Why This Trace Matters

Future agents will face pressure to reallocate Kay's time away from conferences — "this week's calendar is packed," "JJ needs a review session," "the analyst meeting ran long." Without this trace, the conference slot becomes negotiable. With it, the conference slot becomes the non-negotiable anchor, and other items negotiate around it.

This also feeds the `feedback_engine_architecture` model: Kay's job is to be in front of owners and intermediaries. Conferences are the highest-density version of that. The engine (Claude/JJ/DealsX) absorbs everything that would otherwise crowd the conference slot.

## Key Insight

**Channels are not interchangeable at comparable volume.** 8 cards from a conference ≠ 8 Apollo contacts from a list ≠ 8 JJ cold dials. The conference cards arrive with warm-intro equity, intermediary context, and a reason to follow up — the list and the dials don't. ROI per unit of Kay's time is an order of magnitude higher for conferences.

The validation is not "conferences work" — it's "conferences work *in a way that no other channel can substitute for.*"
