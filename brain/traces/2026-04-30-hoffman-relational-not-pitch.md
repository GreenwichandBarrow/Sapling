# Hoffman LinkedIn DM — relational, not pitch

## Decision

Industry-advisor outreach to a senior, respected operator (William Hoffman, just sold his $15M pest-control business, staying on by choice, well-regarded community figure) defaults to **humble student-of-industry framing**, NOT *peer with thesis*.

## What was rejected

V1 draft used "buyer-with-thesis" framing — direct who-Kay-is-and-what-she-wants. Kay rejected: "I think you are a little off in the approach. He runs a 15M business he just sold 2 weeks ago and is staying on because he loves the community. We need to acknowledge he is busy. He is highly regarded in the community. Show more respect and deeper level of appreciation for any time he can give in the draft."

V2 draft restructured to acknowledge his time + position him as exemplar. Better but still missing personal bond.

V3 added a discrete shared-interest paragraph: "Your stories about visionaries like Walt Disney also stayed with me. The kind of continual learning that's shaped your leadership is something I deeply aspire to."

## What landed

The successful framing has 4 elements:
1. **Specific anchor from the workshop** ("customer service with a little extermination" — proves Kay was actually present and listening)
2. **Personal-bond paragraph** with named exemplar (Walt Disney) and Kay's relationship to it ("aspire to")
3. **Explicit acknowledgment of his time** ("I know your time is in real demand, so I won't assume on it")
4. **Three-part flexibility** ("come your way, work entirely around your schedule, keep it as brief as you'd like") — strips friction, makes it a low-cost yes

## Why this matters for future agents

The default voice for ANY external outreach drifts toward buyer-pitch / thesis-statement. For senior respected operators (high reputation, scarce time, exited-or-exiting), that voice is wrong. The right voice is: humble, deferential, specific to them, low-pressure ask.

Failure mode: producing a draft that's "professional" but reads as cookie-cutter. Even if every voice rule is followed (no em dashes, no fund language, day-aware close, etc.), the email can still fail by being insufficiently warm/specific to the recipient as a person.

## How a future agent should apply

When the recipient profile matches:
- Successful exited or about-to-exit operator
- Active in community / industry leadership role
- Kay's relationship is *learning from them*, NOT *trying to sell them anything*

…apply the humble-student framing. Specific anchor + personal-bond paragraph + explicit time acknowledgment + flexibility. Skip the buy-box, skip the thesis statement, skip any transactional ask beyond "would love a short conversation."
