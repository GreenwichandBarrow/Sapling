## [09:15] web-intelligence
Research complete. Here are findings across all dimensions.

**Industry Definition:**
Estate management companies provide comprehensive oversight of luxury residential properties for UHNW individuals and families. Distinct from traditional property management (rental-focused, 8-12% of rent) and concierge/lifestyle services (event-driven). Core services: property maintenance coordination, vendor management, household staff oversight, seasonal opening/closing, security monitoring, budget management, project oversight for renovations. Revenue model is monthly retainer per property ($2,000-$15,000+), not percentage-of-rent. The typical UHNW client owns 3-4 homes (Knight Frank 2024) and needs a "COO of household operations."

**TAM Estimate:**
- US UHNW households ($50M+ net worth): ~100,000+ (Altrata/Cerulli 2024-2025)
- Estimated 30-40% outsource to independent firms = ~30,000-40,000 customer households
- No industry association, registry, or trade group exists
- Geographic analysis of wealthy enclaves: major markets (Hamptons, Palm Beach, Aspen, Greenwich, Nantucket, Martha's Vineyard, Malibu, Montecito, Lake Tahoe, Jackson Hole, Scottsdale, Naples) at ~15-25 firms each = 200-375 firms
- Secondary markets (Sun Valley, Hilton Head, Telluride, Vail, Park City, Newport, Kiawah, Sea Island, etc.) at ~5-15 each = 100-300 firms
- Metro luxury markets (NYC, LA, SF, Miami, Chicago, DC, Boston) at ~10-20 each = 70-140 firms
- Scattered independents: ~200-500 firms
- **Total estimate: 600-1,300 independently owned firms**

**Margins & Revenue:**
- Monthly retainers: $2,000-$15,000+ per property
- Vendor markup: 10-25% on maintenance/repair coordination
- Project management: 10-15% of renovation cost
- Seasonal open/close: $1,000-$5,000 per event
- Typical firm revenue: $500K-$5M (10-30 properties, 3-10 staff)
- EBITDA margins: 15-25% (labor = 65-80% of costs)
- Valuation multiples: 3.3x-7.1x EBITDA (broader property management comps)
- Retention: 85-95% (trust-based, high switching costs)

**Competitive Landscape:**
- No dominant national player. Extremely fragmented by geography.
- Virtually zero PE consolidation in luxury estate management specifically
- PE activity concentrated in traditional property management (apartments, HOAs) not UHNW estate care
- Nines Living provides SaaS platform for estate managers but does not manage properties
- Adjacent: family offices with in-house staff, concierge companies (Quintessentially)

**Key Risks:**
1. TAM too small for Sam's model: 500-1,300 total firms, not 500/month
2. Hyper-local nature limits geographic roll-up synergies
3. Key-person risk: client relationships tied to founder
4. No industry association, conference, or directory for list building
5. Revenue concentration in seasonal markets
6. Labor-intensive with thin margins
