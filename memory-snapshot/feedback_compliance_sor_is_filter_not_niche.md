"Compliance-driven system-of-record vertical software" is Kay's SELECTION CRITERION for picking sub-verticals. It is NOT a niche to hand to Sam.

**Why:** Sam can't type abstract thesis concepts into Apollo or LinkedIn. He needs searchable categories with NAICS codes, industry keywords, and trade publication targeting. "Vertical SaaS" is too broad. "Compliance-driven SoR software" is unsearchable. "Specialty Healthcare Practice Management" is searchable.

**How to apply:** When presenting niches to Kay, separate the two layers clearly: (1) the thesis/filter (Kay's internal logic for selecting) from (2) the niches (what Sam actually searches). The thesis informs WHICH sub-verticals make the cut. The sub-verticals are what Sam runs outreach on.

**Same pattern applies to:** Right-to-win angles — "women-led clinical specialties" is Kay's conviction filter; "Specialty Healthcare PM/EHR" is what Sam searches.
