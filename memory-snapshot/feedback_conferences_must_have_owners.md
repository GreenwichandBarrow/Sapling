Conference strategy must prioritize events where the actual acquisition targets (business owners) are in the room. InsurTech conferences are full of tech vendors, VCs, and advisors — not the 60-year-old brokerage owners thinking about succession.

**Why:** Kay wants to talk to owners. That's where her highest conversion happens. Intermediary networking is secondary to direct owner access. Jeff's conference advice was about being in the room WITH owners, not with people who know owners.

**How to apply:** Conference-discovery skill must filter for:
- Industry association events (IIABA, PIA state chapters, NAIC)
- Specialty insurance association conferences
- Regional insurance conferences where local agency owners attend
- Owner/principal-focused events (perpetuation planning, succession workshops)
- Trade-specific events (FCIB for TCI, PLUS for professional liability, etc.)

NOT: InsurTech conferences, innovation summits, startup competitions, VC demo days. These are intermediary/network events, not owner events. Tag them differently.
