When picking vertical SaaS niches, score sub-verticals individually against each of Jake Stoller's 7 Riverside criteria. Do NOT pre-filter by multi-stream convergence.

**Why:** Convergence (appearing in search fund deals + SBA data + defensibility scoring) measures POPULARITY and FRAGMENTATION. It does not measure Riverside defensibility directly. Some of the highest-scoring defensibility categories (Gov't Investigations 92/100, Pharmacy 83/100, LIMS 84/100) only appeared in one stream. Conversely, field service/trades converged in all 3 streams but scored LOWEST on Riverside criteria (75/100).

**How to apply:** Present criterion-by-criterion scoring tables to Kay. Let her see which sub-verticals dominate on the criteria she cares about (compliance-driven, high cost of failure) rather than pre-filtering to a shortlist based on convergence. Convergence is a useful secondary signal but not the primary ranking.
