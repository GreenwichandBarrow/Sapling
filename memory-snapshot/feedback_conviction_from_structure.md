Adam (ECP/assisted living), Amanda Neilson (PeaceCare), Datacor (chemical distribution) — none had passion for the vertical. Conviction came from structural fit: recurring revenue, compliance-driven, fragmented market, defensible moat, transformation upside.

**Why:** Kay has been searching for a niche that pulls her emotionally (luxury, art, Chanel model). Insurance has that pull. But for vertical SaaS, the conviction doesn't need to come from personal passion — it comes from recognizing the right structure.

**How to apply:** When evaluating SaaS sub-verticals for DealsX, don't filter for "luxury connection" or "personal passion." Filter for defensibility characteristics. The luxury niches (jewelry, marina) are a bonus, not a requirement. Non-luxury verticals (municipal software, lab testing, bridge inspection) are valid if the structure is right AND Kay has the right team around her.
