Always use Excel (.xlsx) format for documents with numbers — scorecards, financial models, budgets, analyses.

**Why:** Kay prefers Excel for numerical work. Camilla (analyst) works in Excel. Google Sheets is fine for trackers/dashboards but not for detailed scoring or financial work.

**How to apply:** Use openpyxl to create xlsx files. When a template exists (e.g., G&B Scorecard Template), copy and fill it rather than building from scratch. Upload to Drive as xlsx, not converted to Google Sheets.
