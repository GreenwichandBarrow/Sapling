Investor call preps must be short and bottom-line driven. Think about what the audience cares about and cater the message.

**Structure:** Productivity, Deal Flow, Thesis, Outlook, Discuss. Each section 2-4 lines max.

**Why:** The previous format was 7 sections, 2000+ words. Investors want to know: are you being productive, is deal flow happening, is the thesis sharpening, and what's the outlook for the next deal. They don't need research details.

**How to apply:**
- No team mentions (investors already know the team)
- No deal history recaps (they remember)
- No detailed niche breakdowns (just scores and headlines)
- No budget specifics (just "evaluating cost reductions" level)
- Mention the AI operational system and calibration loop as productivity proof
- Include the goal: 1-2 conferences/week, 1 interesting deal/week
- Tailor the Discuss section per investor (their specific intro, their network ask)
