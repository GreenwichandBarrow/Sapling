Morning briefing items must each have an **explicit question or action**. Kay should never have to guess what the system wants from her.

**Why:** Kay found the first morning briefing hard to parse — items were listed without clear asks. "Insurance back-end niche ideas from Mike Horowitz" tells her nothing about what she needs to do.

**How to apply:**
- Every item needs one of: a yes/no question, a decision to make, or "FYI only — no action needed"
- Format: "{item title} — **Action:** {what Kay needs to do}" or "{item title} — **Question:** {specific yes/no}"
- If no action is needed, say "FYI only" and don't number it in the action list
- Don't mix informational items with action items — separate them
- Niche signals that need permission to run: "**Run niche-intel screening?** Yes/No"
- Items from JJ or other sources that need context: explain WHY it's here and WHAT the decision is
