Never describe Greenwich & Barrow as a "fund" in any outreach or owner-facing communication.

**Why:** "Fund" sounds like private equity. Kay's edge is the opposite — she's a woman building something for her family, looking to continue the owner's legacy and keep their employees. That's the story that resonates with founders. PE language kills that trust instantly.

**How to apply:** In outreach, describe G&B naturally without a label. "I run Greenwich & Barrow" is enough — let the conversation reveal what G&B is. If pressed, Kay positions as an independent searcher/operator, not a fund. Internal docs and investor communications can say "search fund" but owner-facing never.
