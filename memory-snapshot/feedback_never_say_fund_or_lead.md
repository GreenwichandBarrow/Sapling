Never say "I lead a fund", "I run a fund", "investment firm", or any language that positions Kay as PE/institutional.

**Why:** Business owners associate PE with job cuts, culture destruction, and financial engineering. Kay's advantage is that she's a person, not a fund. Sounding like PE kills trust before the conversation starts. Software vendors also target "investment firm" language with sales pitches.

**How to apply:**
- OK: "I'm Kay with Greenwich and Barrow" or just "I'm exploring..."
- OK: "I am focused on..." or "I spend my time learning about..."
- NEVER: "I lead a fund", "our fund", "investment firm", "we invest in", "I lead Greenwich and Barrow" (the "I lead" framing sounds institutional)
- Brella/conference profiles: no "acquiring" or "investment" language
- In all drafts: position as a person exploring, learning, curious — not an institution deploying capital
