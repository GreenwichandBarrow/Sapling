The niche-intel skill has been searching the wrong space. Every niche it surfaces (art advisory, trust administration, estate management, art insurance brokerage) is a "nice-to-have" advisory service for wealthy individuals. These have beautiful theses but empty target pools, key person risk, and discretionary spending that dries up in downturns.

**The right search pattern:** Operationally critical B2B services that luxury/UHNW businesses NEED to function. Not services for wealthy people — services for the businesses that serve wealthy people.

**Example that works:** Commercial cleaning for luxury boutiques. Recurring, non-discretionary, low key-person risk, value in contracts not the founder. The boutique doesn't open if it's not clean. Kay rejected it only because of operating reality (odd hours, high turnover, low-skilled labor management) — not because of economics.

**The filter:**
1. Must-have (not nice-to-have) — the client's business doesn't function without it
2. B2B (not B2C advisory to wealthy individuals)
3. Serves luxury/UHNW ecosystem (Kay's right-to-win and network access)
4. 15%+ margins, recurring revenue, $500M+ TAM
5. Enough targets to buy (50+)
6. Operating reality Kay can live with (not pure labor management)

**Why:** Kay's advisory-focused niches all have 3-40 targets and key person risk. The operational infrastructure niches serving luxury businesses may have hundreds of targets and value in contracts/operations.

**How to apply:** Retool niche-intel gathering agents to search for "operationally critical B2B services to luxury industry businesses" — compliance, facilities, logistics, technology, regulatory, documentation services that luxury businesses must have to operate. The luxury connection gives Kay credibility; the operational necessity gives the business durability.

**Context:** Kay is at 13 months of a 24-month search. Commercial cleaning is the backstop at 18 months. The system needs to find something better in the next 3 months.
