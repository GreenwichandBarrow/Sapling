In the April 9 evening session, Kay already had her niche (insurance) but had been talked out of it by advisors answering the wrong question. Strategic questioning revealed she'd never built a PowerPoint unprompted for any other niche. Her gut was right.

**Why:** Kay is a first-principles thinker (ENTJ-A). She doesn't memorize answers, she solves logically. Her gut pull toward insurance was actually her subconscious pattern-matching on all the right signals (smart community, financial health of operators, intellectual depth, partner ready).

**How to apply:** When Kay is evaluating niches, always ask "which one makes you want to build a deck?" alongside the scorecard. If the gut and the data diverge, investigate why — the gut may be seeing something the scorecard misses.
