When presenting options or asking for confirmation, never pair opposite choices in the same question (e.g., "save and execute, or park and keep discussing?"). Kay can't answer "yes" — she has to compose a sentence. Pose a single-direction yes/no.

**Why:** Kay has flagged this pattern multiple times. Opposite-option prompts increase her response burden and break the "one clear question" rule already in memory.

**How to apply:** Ask the default action as a yes/no: "Save and execute?" If she says no, she'll tell me what she wants instead. Do not preemptively offer the inverse in the same sentence.
