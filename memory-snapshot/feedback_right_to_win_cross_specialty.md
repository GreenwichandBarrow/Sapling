Kay's right-to-win in a niche often cuts ACROSS multiple sub-verticals rather than mapping to one specific label.

**Example from April 11 session:** Initially tried to narrow "women's health software" as its own niche (too small, ~60-100 vendors). Kay pushed back with PeaceCare Experts — Amanda Neilson's geriatric care acquisition staffed by women. The insight: Kay's right-to-win isn't the clinical label "women's health" — it's the specialties where women are practitioners, owners, and buyers. That cuts across behavioral health, home health, PT, senior care, OB/GYN, maternal, pediatric, speech/OT.

**Why:** Forcing Kay's edge to match a single clinical/industry label narrows the TAM unnecessarily. The actual edge is a characteristic (demographic, operational, cultural) that appears across multiple sub-verticals.

**How to apply:** When defining Kay's right-to-win within a niche, ask "what's the characteristic that makes her credible?" — NOT "which sub-vertical matches her background most literally?" The niche is the full searchable category. The conviction filter is the characteristic that appears across sub-verticals within it.
