Intermediaries and industry experts have NOT converted for Kay. The goal is ALWAYS owner conversations — on calls or in person.

**River guide definition (Jeff validated this):**
- Has personal relationships with brokerage/MGA OWNERS (not just knowledge of the industry)
- Can pick up the phone and say "Kay, you need to talk to my friend John who owns this agency"
- Sees owners regularly in their day-to-day work
- Has trust and credibility with those owners
- Can make DIRECT introductions, not just provide information

**Industry expert (NOT a river guide):**
- Knows the market, trends, dynamics
- Works at a consulting firm, VC, or advisory that serves carrier CEOs
- Smart about the space but doesn't know individual brokerage owners personally
- McKinsey, L.E.K., A&M consultants fall here — they advise carriers, not owners
- VCs know startup founders, not traditional brokerage owners

**Who IS a river guide:**
- M&A lawyers who represent owners in transactions (Troutman Pepper)
- Sell-side advisors who sit across the table from owners (MarshBerry)
- People who do program-level work WITH owners daily (Sproutr, K2 Insurance)
- Distribution advisors who consult directly to agency owners (Affinity Advisor Group)
- Association chapter leaders who see owners at local events

**How to apply:** When evaluating conference attendees, networking targets, or potential river guides, always ask: "Does this person personally know owners who might sell, and can they make a direct introduction?" If the answer is no, they're an industry expert, not a river guide. Don't prioritize meeting them over actual owners or true river guides.

**Kay's time allocation:** Owner conversations > River guide meetings that lead to owner conversations > Everything else. Intermediaries who can't make direct intros are lowest priority.
