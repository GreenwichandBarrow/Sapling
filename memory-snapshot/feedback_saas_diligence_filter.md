When evaluating vertical SaaS targets (DealsX list building, deal-aggregator screening, deal-evaluation):

**Include:** Core system of record (not module), compliance/regulatory driven adoption, non-technical customers with long switching cycles, high cost of failure, module-based revenue expansion (not just seats), founder-owned, US-based, $2-5M EBITDA / $3-5M ARR.

**Exclude:** Horizontal SaaS, data-leaking modules (data leaves product to ERP), pure seat-license models with no module path, customer concentration >30% top 3, sub-10% growth with flat margins ("no man's land"), verticals where AI is already replacing the workflow.

**Why:** Jake Stoller (Riverside, $13B AUM) shared this framework at BK Growth April 10. Riverside only does vertical SaaS now. Small TAM is a positive (less AI disruption pressure). These criteria separate businesses AI makes more valuable from businesses AI eats.

**How to apply:** Filter Sam's (DealsX) sub-vertical list. Filter deal-aggregator matches. Apply during deal-evaluation Phase 1 screen. Brief Sam with these criteria when his sub-vertical list arrives.
