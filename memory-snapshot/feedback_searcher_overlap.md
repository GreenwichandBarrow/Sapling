---
name: Searcher Overlap is Negative
description: Niches already acquired and rolled up by another searcher should be scored negatively, not positively
type: feedback
---

If a niche has already been run by a searcher (acquired, operated, exited), treat that as a NEGATIVE signal, not a positive one — especially if the searcher rolled it up long-term.

**Why:** A proven searcher playbook in a niche means there's now an informed competitor or strategic buyer who knows the space intimately. It also means the low-hanging fruit targets may already be acquired, and remaining owners may have inflated price expectations from the prior transaction. G&B wants whitespace, not a niche where someone already ran the play.

**How to apply:** In Step 2 (identification) and Step 4 (scoring), if evidence shows a searcher already acquired and operated in a niche, flag it as a competitive risk. Do NOT cite it as "validation" or "proven playbook." Factor it into Porter's Five Forces (competition) and Value Creation Opportunities (less upside if professionalization already happened).

**Important nuance:** The negative signal is specific to the exact sub-niche, not the broader category. Example: Sarah Rowell ran the harassment prevention eLearning playbook (Kantola → Traliant/PSG). That specific sub-niche is now consolidated. BUT adjacent compliance training verticals (workplace health & safety, OSHA, food safety, environmental compliance) use the same business model and are still open whitespace. When a searcher has run a play in a sub-niche, look for analogous adjacent sub-niches that benefit from the same economics without the competition.
