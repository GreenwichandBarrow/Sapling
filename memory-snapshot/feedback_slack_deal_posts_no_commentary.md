Slack posts to #strategy-active-deals (and any team channel) must contain ONLY the raw deal facts: company, source, financials, link. Never include internal reasoning, filter notes, thesis framing, motivation reads, "would need Jake/Adam filter before advancing," or any Claude-to-Kay dialogue.

**Why:** #strategy-active-deals goes to Kay's team (analyst, JJ). Internal commentary — filter critiques, soft signals like "seller is 'curious' not 'exploring'", SaaS filter gating, strategic framing — is Kay-Claude terminal dialogue, not team broadcast. Leaking it to team confuses ownership and exposes internal decision logic that should stay private.

**How to apply:** For any Slack webhook post to team channels (#strategy-active-deals, #active-deals, etc.):
- Include: company name, source, revenue, EBITDA, ask, listing link, thesis-match flag
- Exclude: margins analysis, motivation reads, filter gating, "would need X before advancing," any first-person commentary
- Terminal (this conversation) is where Claude and Kay discuss filter decisions, soft signals, and advance/pass reasoning
