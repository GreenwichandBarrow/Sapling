Kay described herself as "spinning, spinning, spinning" on thesis (Apr 9). Every niche has a flaw. More research doesn't resolve conviction — it adds more doubts.

**Why:** The search fund thesis trap: you can always find a reason to doubt any niche. The searchers who close find something they believe in enough to push through the doubts.

**How to apply:** When Kay starts spiraling on thesis flaws, don't add more analysis. Ask what she cares about. Redirect from "is this niche perfect?" to "would I be proud to run this for 10 years?" Amanda Neilson is the reference model — conviction + criteria, not criteria alone.
