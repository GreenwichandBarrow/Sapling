Art storage & related services businesses typically operate across multiple channels: crating, trucking, installation, storage, and others. The blended margin across all services is ~8%, which is why owners say "it's hard and no one has any money."

**The thesis:** Pure storage has margins of 16%+ and in some cases 40%. If you acquire and shift the revenue mix toward higher-margin storage while improving operations on other lines, overall margins improve significantly. These are all mom-and-pop shops with no one analyzing profitability by revenue stream.

**Current status:** Kay's investor suggested going back to close a specific deal in this space (HQ in NY). Kay is skeptical because of the repeated "it's hard" message from owners. Not dead but not activated.

**Key deals explored:** ACU (cold outreach → NDA → financials review), HG/Hangman (cold → NDA → site visit → stepped back due to seller transparency issues).

**Why:** This is a real operational improvement play, not just a buy-and-hold. The margin analysis angle is unique and could be Kay's value-add proposition to sellers.

**How to apply:** Don't dismiss art storage/services based on blended margins alone. If this niche activates, the outreach should lead with the operational improvement angle and the multi-service portfolio analysis capability.
