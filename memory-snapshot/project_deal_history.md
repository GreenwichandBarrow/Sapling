## Deal 1: Hangman Fine Arts (HG)
- **Source:** Cold outreach
- **Owner:** David Hurwitz, age 44, NYU alum
- **Location:** Brooklyn HQ + Hamptons, LA, Miami
- **Employees:** ~80
- **Revenue trajectory:** $600K (2020) → $1.5M → $4M → $6M → $7M (2025)
- **EBITDA:** $300-500K (~8% blended margin)
- **Services:** Art storage, crating, trucking, installation. 90% client retention. Proprietary inventory management software.
- **Valuation ask:** 2.5x revenue (~$17.5M)
- **Process:** NDA signed → financials reviewed → site visit (investor Guillermo attended Oct 10, 2025) → passed
- **Why passed:** 8% EBITDA margins, capital-intensive growth model (new locations), investors uncomfortable with margin/capex profile. Sent decline email to David, left door open for future.
- **Lesson:** Art services blended margins are structurally low. Pure storage is 16-40% margin but transport (60% of revenue) drags blended down.

## Deal 2: Acumen (ACU)
- **Source:** Cold outreach
- **Owner structure:** 6-7 partners (4 NYC, 2 LA, 1 Miami). Contact: Levi
- **Revenue:** ~$13.2M
- **EBITDA:** ~$1.1M (~8-9% margin)
- **Locations:** Poughkeepsie (near capacity), Maspeth (unknown), LA (20% expansion, pre-booked), Miami
- **Entity:** Believed to be C Corp (needed confirmation)
- **Process:** NDA signed → financials received (rough P&L) → offer made at $5M → seller said lower than expected → LOI drafted ($5M TEV, 10-20% seller rollover, seller note) → stalled
- **Why stalled:** Investors said deal was too small, margins too thin for search fund economics. Capital structure wouldn't work.
- **Lesson:** Art storage deals at this size don't generate enough EBITDA to justify search fund overhead. Need $2M+ EBITDA minimum.
