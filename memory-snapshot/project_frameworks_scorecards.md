## Industry Scorecard (9 metrics, 30 points max)
- Industry Growth (10%) — CAGR
- Future Growth (10%)
- Business Quality/TAM (10%)
- Market Position (10%)
- Revenue Concentration (10%)
- Predictable Revenue (15%) — recurring vs. re-occurring
- Operating Efficiency (10%) — EBITDA margins
- Revenue Sustainability (10%)
- KS Fit (15%) — Kay's personal right-to-win

**Top scorers historically:** Medical billing & coding (28/30), Telemedicine (26/30), IT services & EHR (26/30), Art infrastructure (2.75/3.0 on separate scale)

## Initial Screen (for industries — added 3/20/2026)
Before running the full Industry Scorecard, industries pass an initial screen:
- Margins: 15%+
- Recurring Revenue: Yes
- Industry Growth: >GDP
- Growth TAM: $500M+ (investor requirement)

**Target TAM** is informational (like QSBS) — it determines sprint duration, not go/no-go. Per investor guidance: target count determines how long to stay in an industry, not whether to enter.

## Industry Scorecard vs Company Scorecard (clarified 3/20/2026)
- **Industry Scorecard** = evaluates whether an INDUSTRY is worth pursuing. Reference tool for ranking, NOT a decision gate. No automated kills — Kay decides everything.
- **Company Scorecard (Buy-Box Screen)** = evaluates whether a specific COMPANY fits the acquisition criteria. Uses the buy box below.
- These are different tools for different purposes. Buy box is for companies, NOT industries.

## Company Scoring (70/30 hybrid)
- 70% standardized hard gates (revenue, EBITDA, recurring %, employee count, geography, owner age, PE-free)
- 30% discretionary pattern override (Kay's judgment on fit, culture, growth story)

## Buy Box (Current, Mar 2026)
**Note:** Buy box applies to COMPANIES, not industries. Size is situational (not a hard fail).
- Revenue: $10-50M ($5-40M for software)
- EBITDA: $1.5-5M+ (floor $1.5M per PPM, prefer $3M+)
- Employees: 11-200
- Operating history: 10+ years (5+ for software)
- Revenue type: Durable recurring or convertible-to-recurring
- Model: B2B services or vertical SaaS, asset-light, high ROIC
- Owner: Retiring, fatigued, or founder-ready-to-exit. Not PE-backed, not VC-funded.
- Geography: Within 1hr of home if on-site; anywhere US if remote-manageable
- Must be: mission-critical, workflow-embedded, AI-enhanceable (not AI-disruptable)
- Exclude: B2C, retail, restaurants, construction, franchises, physician practices, seasonal, blue-collar culture

## Decision Framework (DEFINE-SOURCE-SCREEN-REFINE)
- **DEFINE:** Strategic boundary setting, 1-2 industries per 8-week cycle
- **SOURCE:** Direct (owner outbound, conferences) + Indirect (intermediaries, lenders, inbound). 75/25 split.
- **SCREEN:** First call → financial model → scorecard → go/no-go. 5 key diagnostic questions per sector.
- **REFINE:** Intelligence compounding, ICP tightening, scorecard recalibration

## Key Principles (from 16 months of learning)
1. **"Acquire momentum, don't manufacture it"** — Buy a business on the rise, get on the wheel and steer
2. **"Machines don't rely on motivation, they rely on cadence"** — Shift from searching to running an acquisition machine
3. **Quality over quantity** — Searchers who acquired contacted fewer owners/day (~4) vs. those who didn't (~9)
4. **River guides > cold outreach** — Industry insiders are the "silver bullet" (investor quote)
5. **Conference = highest value activity** — Kay's in-person strength is her #1 asset
6. **Two-week max per hypothesis** — Kill, advance, or table. Monday = decision gate.
7. **Large incumbents are signal, not targets** — Use them to find subscale specialists they underserve
8. **Need-to-have vs. nice-to-have** — Only pursue mission-critical, compliance-driven services
9. **PE as exit validation** — Need to acquire something a PE firm would buy at year 4-7
10. **1 interesting deal reviewed per week** = a solid system

## Growth Levers (for post-acquisition)
Only 4 reliable EBITDA growth levers: organic volume, price expansion, margin expansion, tuck-in acquisitions. Organic growth for first 2-3 years, then add-on acquisitions.

## Financial Targets
- Entry multiple: 4-7x EBITDA
- Target MOIC: 3-5x over 4-7 years
- $10M personal exit requires: $2M+ EBITDA at acquisition, grow to $8M EBITDA in 5 years, sell at 5x = $40M × 25% searcher share
- QSBS consideration: Insurance brokerage is EXCLUDED from QSBS tax treatment. Art storage qualifies.

**Why:** These frameworks represent 16 months of iteration. They should be the starting point for any niche evaluation, not reinvented each time.

**How to apply:** When scoring new niches, use the 9-metric scorecard. When evaluating deals, apply the 70/30 hybrid. When Kay is stuck on a decision, reference the 10 principles. The financial targets set the floor for what deals are worth pursuing.
