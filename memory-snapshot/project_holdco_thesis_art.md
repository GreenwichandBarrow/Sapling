As of 2026-03-31, Kay articulated a clear holdco thesis for the art/luxury ecosystem:

**Sequence (Buffett model — he didn't buy GEICO first):**
1. Buy art advisory firm first — cheap ($2-5M), gets inside the ecosystem, sees all referral flows
2. Then buy insurance brokerage — advisory clients already need art insurance, built-in distribution. Insurance = the float (recurring, non-discretionary, counter-cyclical)
3. Then bolt on storage, logistics, collection management — funded by insurance cash flow
4. Each acquisition adds clients to the insurance book. Flywheel compounds.

**Distribution thesis:**
- Advisory is NOT the business — it's the distribution channel
- Insurance is NOT a service line — it's the cash flow engine (Buffett's GEICO = the float)
- The holdco is the vehicle

**Family office angle (critical):**
- Family offices sit between patrons and advisors as the new distribution layer
- FOs managing hard assets (20% of HNW wealth allocated to art in 2025, up from 15%)
- FOs want ONE partner across advisory + insurance + storage + collection mgmt
- Capture one FO relationship = capture all their families' art needs
- FO market nearly doubling in North America (2,210 → 4,190 by 2030, Deloitte)
- Multi-family offices growing at ~8% CAGR

**Validation plan:**
- Family office conferences to test thesis (ACG April 21, Opal Newport July, Opal Southampton August)
- Art advisory validation calls (Margot Romano April 3, others)
- Key question: What % of advisory client intake comes through family offices vs direct?
