## The Core Insight

Kay's search has been looking in "advisory and specialty services for wealthy people" — niches with beautiful theses but empty target pools (3-40 targets), key person risk, and discretionary spending.

The pivot: search for **operationally critical B2B services that luxury businesses NEED to function**. Not services for wealthy individuals — services for the businesses that serve wealthy people.

Commercial cleaning for luxury boutiques is Kay's backstop example — boring, necessary, recurring, the boutique doesn't open without it.

## Key Filters That Kill Niches
1. Target pool < 20 (empty pool)
2. Key person risk (advisory/relationship businesses)
3. Discretionary spend (nice-to-have, not need-to-have)
4. Capital intensive (facilities, equipment, real estate)
5. Labor management reality (odd hours, high turnover, low-skilled)
6. PE already consolidated
7. Not recurring revenue (project-based)

## The Tension
Kay's thesis gravitates toward highly specialized, UHNW-adjacent, relationship-driven businesses. These are great to OWN but hard to BUY — small pools, founder-dependent, discretionary. The niches with big target pools (hundreds of companies) tend to be commoditized, PE-saturated, or involve operating realities Kay doesn't want.

Commercial cleaning is the backstop at 18 months (currently at 13 months).

## What's Needed
The niche-intel skill needs to find the intersection of:
- Operationally critical (need-to-have)
- Connected to luxury (Kay's right-to-win)
- 50+ target pool
- Recurring revenue
- Asset-light
- Operating reality Kay can live with
- Not already PE-consolidated
