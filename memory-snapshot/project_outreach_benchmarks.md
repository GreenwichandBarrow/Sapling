## Most Effective Outreach Methods (2025 investor survey, 54 responses)
1. Customized email — 24.1% (BUT declining: was 63% in 2022, 59% in 2023, 33% in 2024)
2. Phone — 22.2% (large uptick in 2025)
3. Conferences — 20.4% (continued emphasis from 2024)
4. River Guides — 11.1%
5. Automated email, LinkedIn, Direct Mail — smaller slices
- No significant trend between contact method and completing acquisition in 2024-2025

## Outreach Volume Benchmarks (2025)
- Average: 6.4 owners contacted/day (LOWEST EVER in survey history)
- Median: 4.3/day
- **72% of funds that acquired contacted 6 or fewer owners/day**
- Funds that acquired: averaged 4 owners/day
- Funds that didn't acquire: averaged 9 owners/day
- "More concentrated, low volume approach yielding more success"

## In-Person Seller Meeting Frequency (2025)
- Average: every 22 days
- Median: every 11 days
- **Funds that acquired: every 15.6 days**
- Funds that didn't acquire (2024): every 36.6 days
- "In-person meeting frequency is by far the best predictor of successfully completing an acquisition"
- Kay's target with weekly conferences: in-person seller meeting every 7 days (2x the successful rate)

## Written Offers (2025)
- Average: every 93.8 days (median 73)
- Written offers NOT a predictor of success in 2025 (was in 2023-2024)
- Getting harder to get in front of owners with volume of outreach increasing

## Search Strategy Focus (2025)
- 53.7% narrowly focused on single industry
- 33.3% industry focused
- 13% mix of industry and opportunistic
- Industry focused searchers: ~2x acquisition success rate (6/34 vs 2/23)
- Both searchers who described themselves as "narrowly focused on single industry" in 2022 and 2023 acquired the following year

## Time Spent Per Industry (2025)
- 36.5% spend 5-8 weeks on an industry
- 32.7% spend 8+ weeks
- 30.8% spend 1-4 weeks
- No noticeable trend between time spent and acquisition success
- "Unless exclusively focused, there may be an optimal amount not too short, not too long"

## Most Useful Sourcing Channels
- **LinkedIn:** Directly messaging sellers
- **River Guides/Industry Network:** Useful support at conferences, local industry consultants
- **Industry Specific Lists:** Association member lists validate credibility, exhibitor lists from Grata
- **Brokers/Bankers:** Local brokers, regional/boutique bankers (higher quality but competitive)

## AI Uses in Search (2025)
1. **Outreach and email** — writing emails, confirming contacts, organizing content
2. **Industry and market research** — market maps, quick research to "get smart quickly"
3. **Diligence** — early commercial DD, financial analysis, fill blind spots
4. **Idea generation** — list building, niche overviews, brainstorming
- Quote (13-18mo searcher): "I use AI in every part of my process. The heaviest use is in personalization and research."

## Key Metrics Shift (2025 investor survey)
Experienced searchers (24+ months) shift from tracking top-of-funnel (emails sent) to direct connections:

**Top metrics used by searchers (priority order):**
1. In-person meetings (face to face or Zoom)
2. Calls (first calls, second calls, calls longer than 15 min)
3. Meaningful conversations with a qualified seller
4. LOIs (submitted, signed)
5. Financials received
6. Emails (less commonly tracked: number sent, open rate)

**Key quotes from experienced searchers:**
- "Owner conversations, financial statements received from owners, company scorecards completed. Learning what makes a great small business is more important than raw emails sent." (24+ months)
- "Response rate from owners and meaningful conversation rate (responses that lead to real discussion about selling) and number of LOIs signed." (24+ months)
- "Early in the search I think the full funnel is important to track. After 4-6 months I think proprietary LOIs are most important." (7-12 months)

**Why:** Kay is at month ~18 of searching. The right metrics now are meaningful conversations, not emails sent. The target discovery skill should optimize for conversation quality, not volume. The weekly tracker should prominently feature meetings, calls, and conversations, not email counts.

## Sourcing Tools Rating (2025 investor survey, n=20 all, n=11 with 1+ LOI)
**All searchers (Must Have → Nice to Have → Don't Need):**
- Email Campaign: mostly Must Have (16/20), 3 say Don't Need
- Database: mostly Must Have/Nice to Have
- Broker: universal — everyone uses, regardless of stage or LOIs
- LinkedIn: mostly Must Have/Nice to Have
- Conference Attendance: mixed (Must Have for some, Don't Need for others)
- Personal Network: mostly Nice to Have
- Cold Calling: mostly Nice to Have
- City Tours: mostly Don't Need

**Searchers with 1+ LOI signed (n=11):**
- Conferences become LESS necessary (most who don't think conferences are needed are >12 months in)
- Brokers remain universal
- Email + Database + LinkedIn still core

**Key insight:** Conferences are highest value PRE-LOI (Kay's current stage). Once deals are flowing through brokers + email, conference ROI drops. So the heavy conference push now is strategically correct.

**How to apply:**
- Target discovery: quality over quantity. 10 well-researched owners > 100 spray-and-pray emails.
- Weekly tracker: lead with meetings and calls metrics, not email volume. Add response rate, meaningful conversations, source attribution.
- Conference prep: validates the heavy investment in in-person (#1 tracked metric). Will become less critical once LOIs are flowing.
- Brokers: never stop cultivating. Universal across all stages.
