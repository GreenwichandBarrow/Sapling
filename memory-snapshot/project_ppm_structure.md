## Fund Structure
- **Entity:** Greenwich & Barrow, LLC
- **Type:** Traditional Search Fund
- **Raise:** $550,000 through 9.7 units at $56,995K/unit
- **Search Term:** 24 months (launched Jan 2025)
- **Geography:** US nationwide, focus on major metropolitans

## Target Acquisition Criteria (from PPM)
- EBITDA: $1.5-5M
- Growing industry with anticipated tailwinds
- Healthy margins (15%+)
- Recurring revenue
- Min EBITDA $1.5M (Margin >15%)
- Privately held, long history (5+ years)
- No/low seasonality or cyclicality
- No customer concentration
- Simple model, manageable learning curve
- Scope for operating improvements
- Growth potential

## PPM Scorecard (Exhibit 3 — Opportunity Scorecard)
**Growth & Efficiency (40%):**
- Industry Growth: 15% — CAGR with 5-7yr staying power in fragmented/sizable market
- Operating Efficiency: 15% — EBITDA margins
- Overall Performance: 10% — LTM revenue growth, historic growth, resilience

**Revenue Quality (35%):**
- Predictable Revenue: 15% — recurring %, customer retention
- Revenue Concentration: 10% — % from top 5 customers
- Revenue Sustainability: 10% — CLV, CAC

**Strategic Assessment (25%):**
- Business Quality: 10% — scalability, infrastructure readiness
- Market Position: 5% — market share, competitive intensity
- People: 10% — willing seller, team/customer dynamics, Kay fit

## Investment Terms
- Up to 25% carried interest to Principal
- 8.33% vesting on acquisition
- 8.33% vesting equally over 4 years (acceleration on sale)
- 8.33% vesting based on performance
- Preferred return catch up
- 50% step-up on investor capital into acquisition equity
- Right of First Refusal for investors on acquisition equity
- Target 35% compound annual return on investor capital
- Transaction size: $10-30M, investor equity $3-18M (30-60% of capital)

## Initial Industries (PPM vintage — Jan 2025)
1. Commercial Cleaning Services ($75.3B, +1% CAGR, 15-20% EBITDA)
2. Women's Health Services ($41.3B, +5% CAGR, 15-25% EBITDA)

Additional industries listed for future screening: BPO, SaaS, Property Management, Green Certification, Sustainability, Landscaping, Self-Storage, Organizational Services

## Key Context
- PPM was written ~8 months before insurance thesis emerged
- Original industries (cleaning + women's health) both evolved: cleaning is now backstop at 18 months, women's health was killed (all startups, no mature targets)
- The PPM scorecard weights are different from the current industry scorecard (which evolved through use) — PPM has Growth 40% / Revenue Quality 35% / Strategic 25%, while current has 8 categories
- Budget $550K raised, ~$256K remaining as of March 2026
- PPM emphasizes proprietary sourcing (~80%) over intermediary
