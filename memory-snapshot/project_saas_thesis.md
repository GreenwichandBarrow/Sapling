SaaS is in the buy box when it's tightly tied to a vertical and has compliance + people aspects. Not general SaaS.

**Pattern Kay is drawn to:**
- Collection management software (art world — loved this space)
- Estate planning platforms (HNWI/family offices — people + platform)
- License renewal compliance (people + platform service, looked at previously)
- Estate management companies (already on tracker — could have a platform play)

**Thesis thread:** Vertical compliance SaaS serving regulated or high-value niches. Sticky, recurring revenue, regulatory moat, B2B. The "people + platform" model is the sweet spot — not pure software, but software-enabled services with compliance requirements.

**Why:** Fits Kay's buy box (recurring, B2B, compliance-driven, fragmented market). Also fits lifestyle (SaaS can be managed remotely). Previous niches tabled on the tracker (Collection Management Consultants scored, Estate Planning explored) may have adjacent SaaS targets worth revisiting.

**Next step:** Consider as a niche-intelligence candidate. Look for vertical SaaS platforms in niches Kay already knows (art, insurance, estate management, pest management compliance). The collection management software space in particular — Kay "really liked that space."
