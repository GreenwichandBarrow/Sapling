## Timeline

**Q1 2025 (Jan-Mar):** Foundation building. Infrastructure complete. 3 initial themes: Women's Health B2B, Vertical SaaS, HNWI B2B Services. 60 owner/expert conversations. Budget: $478K remaining (86% of $550K). Learning: over-researching lists was inefficient, shifted to testing 1-2 niches/week. Prioritized warm outreach.

**Q2 2025 (Apr-Jun):** Began in-person sourcing at events. "Field Weeks" in key cities. Advanced top 2-3 niches. Focus on recurring revenue models.

**Q3 2025 (Jul-Sep):** Major shift — relationships compounding. Narrowed to 3 deep verticals: Fine Art & Collectibles Services, Specialty Insurance Brokerage, Concierge Home Management. Budget: $359K (65%). Key learnings:
- START: Measuring depth of engagement over activity counts. Using conferences for privileged access.
- STOP: High-volume cold outreach as progress indicator. Email/call counts as KPIs.
- PAUSE: Bi-weekly niche exploration to focus on active verticals.
- Opportunity highlights: ACU (Fine Art, cold outreach → NDA → financials review), HG (Fine Art, cold → NDA → site visit → stepped back, seller transparency issues), PR (Insurance, river guide intro, two women at retirement age)

**Q4 2025 (Oct-Dec):** Difficult quarter. No LOI issued. Kay didn't send Q4 investor update. Dry period began mid-December.

**Q1 2026 (Jan-Mar):** Retooling. Migrated CRM (SalesFlare→Attio), task management (Todoist→Motion), recording (Fireflies→Granola+Attio), brought on Claude Code. Building AI-augmented workflows.

## What Worked
- In-person meetings and conferences → highest quality conversations
- River guide relationships → curated introductions, operator-level insights
- Cold outreach DID produce results (ACU and HG both came from cold)
- Focusing on overlapping UHNW service needs → economies of research scale

## What Didn't Work
- High-volume cold email → didn't translate to meaningful conversations
- Overseas VA for list building and cold email → inaccurate, not personalized
- Specialty insurance brokerage outreach → zero responses across email, snail mail, LinkedIn DMs
- Email counts/call logs/response rates as KPIs
- Constant method changes in tracking → can't gauge what's working

## Kay's Self-Identified Bottlenecks
1. **Time** — biggest bottleneck, solo searcher
2. **Tracking consistency** — strong at vision/strategy, struggles with repetitive weekly tracking. Always editing methods, never tracking the same thing long enough to see trends
3. **Cold call reluctance** — knows it's holding back outreach
4. **Niche selection** — overwhelming number of options in luxury ecosystem
5. **Industry expert → owner conversion** — experts didn't produce enough owner intros, need more intermediaries

Goal: 1 interesting deal reviewed per week

## Outreach Philosophy (Current Thinking)
- Met two successful acquirers: one sent only 4-5 emails/week to owners, another focused all channels on 10 businesses per industry
- Shifted to depth over volume: **10 deeply researched companies per niche per month** — not 50 emails/week
- Depth of connection with a company is better than reaching more companies
- Each target gets full research brief, multiple touchpoints (email, conference, warm intro), not just a cold blast
- Searcher survey validated: acquirers contacted ~4 owners/day vs non-acquirers ~9/day

**How to apply:** Design workflows that handle tracking automatically (Kay won't do it manually), lean into high-touch/quality over volume, make conferences the centerpiece activity, automate everything that isn't relationship-building.
