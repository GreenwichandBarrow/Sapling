Kay wants a Plan B in her pocket alongside Plan A (search fund acquisition → operate → exit).

**Energizers:** Coffee chats, 1-on-1 relationships, WFH, building systems, running small distributed teams, going deep, autonomy.
**Drains:** Office time, cold calling, repetitive tracking, large groups, bureaucracy.

**Ranked by fit:**

1. **Productize the OS** — The AI-native search fund operating system we've built is genuinely novel. Sell to searchers, small PE firms, independent sponsors. Subscription revenue, small team, WFH, every customer is a coffee chat. Uses ALL energizers. Could run alongside Plan A.

2. **Luxury roll-up** — Buy smaller ($1-2M) in domains she knows (jewelry, hospitality, estate mgmt, art advisory). Bolt on 2-3, exit the platform. Lower risk per deal, deep domain expertise. Slower path.

3. **Independent sponsor** — Same work, different structure. No fund clock. Raise capital deal-by-deal. Investor relationships survive. Not really a Plan B, more a structural pivot of Plan A.

4. **Operating partner / Fractional COO to PE** — $250-400K + carry. Good money but working inside someone else's system. Would drain Kay within a year. Drops down the list.

**Key insight:** Plan A and B1 (productize OS) aren't mutually exclusive. OS business could fund lifestyle while searching and eventually become its own exit.
