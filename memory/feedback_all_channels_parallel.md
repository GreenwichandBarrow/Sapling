Kay runs all deal-sourcing channels simultaneously: JJ cold call, cold email outreach (Kay Email / DealsX), cold LinkedIn DM, deal-aggregator (broker platforms + industry specialists + association boards + email inbound), DealsX, river guides, conferences, intermediary outreach, warm-intro sprint. **All active. All the time. For every active niche.**

Do not recommend "drop channel X because it isn't yielding." Do not recommend "redirect energy from channel X to channel Y." Do not frame a zero-yield window as a channel-strategy verdict. Every recommendation about a sourcing channel must instead be about **improving source quality within that channel**: fix scanning bugs, add missing sources, unblock registrations, remove confirmed-dead URLs, verify filter criteria.

**Why:** 2026-04-21 — after investigating a 6-day zero-yield pattern in deal-aggregator, I twice recommended dropping public broker platforms for niches #1 and #2 and redirecting to proprietary outreach. Kay corrected: proprietary outreach is already running. The point of deal-aggregator is to be the *parallel* channel. If sources underperform, the answer is never to stop using them — the answer is to make the sources better, give them an appropriate-length test window, and re-assess source quality (not channel strategy) on cadence. Kay set this rule explicitly: "we are not going to stop using the deal aggregator for any niches. we are going to just make the sourcing for the deal aggregator better."

**How to apply:** Any time I'm tempted to write "drop X" or "redirect from X to Y" or "structurally off-mix" about a channel — stop. The framing is wrong. Rewrite as: "Source X isn't producing; options for improvement are (a) fix scan path, (b) add new URL, (c) unblock registration, (d) verify buy-box filter, (e) remove confirmed-dead entries." Present those as the decisions. The channel itself stays live. Same principle applies to outreach methods (cold call, cold email, LinkedIn), conference attendance, and warm-intro sprints.
