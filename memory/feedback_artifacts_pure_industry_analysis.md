**Rule:** Output artifacts (niche one-pagers, scorecards, Industry Research Tracker entries, Thumbs Up/Down decks, LOIs, call preps for external audiences) are pure industry/target analysis. Do not put personal context, team specifics, individual team-member attributes, or Kay's personal structural framings (e.g., Alain Wertheimer / Chanel / Wedgwood analogies) into these artifacts.

**Why:** Kay corrected this explicitly on 2026-04-19 during specialty coffee equipment service one-pager work. She and I discussed in strategic conversation that her Milanese analyst (soon Chief of Staff) provides a rare right-to-win against Italian-HQ coffee OEMs, and she drew the Chanel-Paris-HQ decentralized-US-distribution parallel to describe the structure. I pulled that context into the one-pager. She pushed back: that context is for our strategic perspective, not for the subagent to write into the artifact. The one-pager is pure industry niche analysis.

**How to apply:**
- Strategic conversation (Kay ↔ Claude) = full context allowed. Personal network, team specifics, aspirational framings (Chanel, Alain, Wedgwood), right-to-win specifics (Milanese analyst, hospitality rolodex, family connections). These inform how I orchestrate and what I surface to Kay.
- Output artifacts = pure industry/target analysis. Generic acquirer-checklist framing ("what any acquirer needs") instead of "Kay has X." No named team members. No personal-network brand names unless they are pure industry entities being analyzed, not acquirer-network assets. No personal-aspirational framings.
- **Scorecard rationale:** If G&B's scorecard has a "KS Fit" dimension, score it on industry fit to G&B's stated thesis (luxury-hospitality adjacency, messy-business-great-segment profile) — not on team-member-specific advantages.
- **Right-to-win sections in artifacts** should be generic acquirer checklists listing factors any acquirer in the space should evaluate — not a statement of what this specific acquirer has.
- **Structural analogies** are allowed in artifacts only when they are purely industry-recognizable comparators (e.g., Ferrari authorized service networks). Personal or aspirational analogies (Chanel, Alain, Wedgwood) stay out of artifacts even when Kay herself raised them in conversation.

**Litmus test:** Would this artifact read identically if a different searcher were evaluating the niche? If no — if any sentence changes based on who the acquirer is — strip that content.
