Post-conference (and any deal-sourcing) follow-up has exactly THREE audience buckets, not four:

1. **Intermediary** — anyone who advises business owners and can refer deal-flow. Includes M&A advisors, corporate advisors, wealth managers, exit planners, CPAs, attorneys, business brokers, bankers. Gets the buy-box reference paragraph.
2. **Owner** — potential sellers. Warm relationship-building only. No buy-box, no ask.
3. **Peer / ecosystem** — other searchers, service providers, fellow LPs, family offices. Simple stay-in-touch.

**Never split "advisor" into its own fourth bucket.** Advisors advise businesses, which means they have direct owner connections, which means they are functionally intermediaries for G&B's purposes.

**Why:** Separating "advisor" from "intermediary" is a conceptual error — it implies two different outreach patterns when the pattern is the same (give them the buy-box so they can refer). It also confuses the template logic (two buckets with identical bodies). Kay flagged this 2026-04-23 when reviewing the conference-engagement skill design post-XPX.

**How to apply:**
- In any skill, script, template, or briefing that classifies post-meeting contacts: use the 3-bucket taxonomy.
- When a contact could be "advisor or intermediary" — they're always intermediary.
- If a contact is genuinely not a potential referrer (e.g., an industry analyst at a trade pub), classify as peer/ecosystem, not "advisor."
- The only meaningful split inside the intermediary bucket is by their specific role (M&A advisor vs wealth manager vs broker) if context demands — but the outreach body is the same.
