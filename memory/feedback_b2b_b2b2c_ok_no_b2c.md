Niche filter for DealsX and weekly tracker: B2B is ideal, B2B2C is acceptable, pure B2C is rejected.

**Examples:**
- ✓ B2B: software sold to clinics, agencies, manufacturers, brokers
- ✓ B2B2C: software sold to IVF clinics that serve patients; software sold to home care agencies that serve families; software sold to retailers that serve consumers
- ✗ B2C: caregiver apps sold direct to families; fitness apps sold direct to consumers; personal finance apps sold direct to individuals

**Why:** Kay's April 12 clarification refining the earlier "DealsX must be B2B" rule. B2B2C models still have the business-buyer economics (recurring contracts, expansion revenue, reference selling) that make search-fund-style acquisitions work, even if end-users are consumers.

**How to apply:** When classifying a niche or target, identify the paying customer. If the payer is a business, it passes — regardless of whether end-users are businesses or consumers. If the payer is an individual consumer, reject.
