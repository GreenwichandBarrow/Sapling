name: Broker email style
description: How to write emails to business brokers — short, offer NDA, open with niceties
type: feedback
---

Broker emails should be concise. Less is more. They get hundreds of these.

1. **Open with niceties** — "Hope this finds you well"
2. **How you found the deal** — mention the source/referral as "a friend" or by name
3. **One sentence on what you do** — "actively looking to acquire a services business" + relevant ecosystem context
4. **Offer to sign an NDA** — that's the natural next step after a teaser
5. **Done.** — no fund description, no thesis, no sector expertise. Save that for the call.

**Calibrated example (Kay's own words):**
> Hi Eric,
>
> Hope this finds you well. I came across the Project Restoration teaser through a friend, Stanley Rodos at Crate Capital. I am actively looking to acquire a services business and have been actively exploring opportunities within the art ecosystem. I'd be happy to sign an NDA to learn more and discuss whether this might be a fit.
>
> Very best,
> Kay

**Why:** Brokers are gatekeepers managing a process. They want to know you're a real buyer who won't waste their time. An NDA offer signals that immediately.

**How to apply:** Any email to a broker about a deal teaser should follow this pattern: nicety → how you found the deal → one line on what you do → NDA offer → sign-off. 3-4 sentences max.
