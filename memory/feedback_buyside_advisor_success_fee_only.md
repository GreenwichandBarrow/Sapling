## Rule

When Kay evaluates engaging a buy-side M&A advisor (or sell-side firm offering buy-side services), her acceptable fee structure is:

- **YES:** Pure success fee (% of deal closed, Lehman scale or flat %)
- **NO:** Retainer (monthly, quarterly, or annual)
- **NO:** Engagement / setup / onboarding fee
- **NO:** Hybrid (retainer + success fee)
- **NO:** Minimum monthly commitment

If a firm's only model is anything other than pure success fee, **walk away**. Don't push back, don't try to negotiate the retainer down, just decline. There are enough success-fee-only firms (boutique + search-fund-friendly) to fill the channel without paying upfront.

## Why

Per Kay 2026-04-30 after evaluating Calder Capital signup: "I'm OK with a success fee but nothing else." Aligns with G&B's broader cost discipline (`feedback_saas_monthly_first.md`, `feedback_budget_not_kays_job.md`) and avoids the trap of paying ongoing fees to advisors who may or may not deliver. Search funds historically struggle with retainer-based buy-side advisors because the equity-on-deal model means cash-out before close is painful for the cap table.

## How to apply

- **Before any buy-side advisor first call:** if the firm's website lists fee structure publicly and it includes retainer, FLAG to Kay before she takes the call so she's not wasting time.
- **If a buy-side advisor proposes engagement and the structure is opaque:** the first thing to ask in the call is fee structure. If retainer is part of it, polite decline + thank them for their time.
- **For evaluating new buy-side advisors going forward:** apply this filter at intake, same way `feedback_no_lending.md` and `feedback_no_california.md` apply at niche/target intake.
- **Acceptable success-fee structures:** Lehman scale (5-4-3-2-1% on first $1M, then $2M, etc.), flat % (commonly 1-3% of total deal value at closing), or hybrid success-fee-only (e.g., higher % below threshold, lower % above).
