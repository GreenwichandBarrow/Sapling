# G&B Charter Clarifications (Apr 28 2026)

Three rules surfaced during the buyer-fingerprint build session. Save these so the same misframings don't repeat.

## Rule 1 — "Founder-replacement" (GOOD) vs "founder-personality-dependent" (HARD-NO)

Don't conflate these. The Charter favors **retiring-owner-with-ready-ops-team** acquisitions — that IS the search-fund model, where Kay replaces the operational ROLE. The hard-no is **founder-personality-dependent** businesses where the founder IS the brand / IS the salesperson / IS the customer relationship anchor.

**How to apply:** When describing what Kay rules out, never write "rules out founder-replacement" — that's exactly the search-fund model. Write "rules out founder-personality-dependent businesses" or "rules out businesses where the founder IS the brand."

## Rule 2 — Wertheimer / luxury-aspirational test is JEWEL-stage specific

The Wertheimer archetype is the Charter identity, but the **luxury-aspirational test does NOT apply universally** to every acquisition. The 4-stage architecture (Bridge / Engine / Community / Jewel) means each stage has a different brand-alignment bar. Pest management passes Brand alignment via the **Community-stage bagel-shop test**, not via luxury-aspirational. Wertheimer-luxury applies most strongly to Jewel candidates only.

**How to apply:** Brand alignment is two-part. (a) UNIVERSAL: bagel-shop test (do owners make the world better? would Kay want her kids to know them?) — applies to every acquisition. (b) STAGE-RELATIVE PRIDE: Jewel = luxury-aspirational + 4 female-empowered criteria; Community = bagel-shop dominates; Engine = economic engine, brand alignment is "doesn't embarrass the family"; Bridge = clean professional. Don't apply Wertheimer-luxury to Bridge / Engine / Community niches — that's a category error.

## Rule 3 — Business brand must be independent of operator (Deal 1 too)

Even on Deal 1, where Kay is CEO operationally, **she is NOT "the face."** The brand belongs to the company structure (process, team, customer-facing operations), not to a person. Earlier in the same Apr 28 session I corrected the opposite-direction error (don't conflate Wertheimer-allocator with Deal 1 operating mode — Kay IS the CEO for Deal 1) — but that doesn't make her the brand-face. Two distinct rules:

- **Operator role:** Deal 1 = Kay is CEO (search-fund traditional). Family HoldCo (Deals 2-4) = Kay is allocator. (See `feedback_kay_ceo_deal_1_not_allocator.md`.)
- **Brand role:** Both Deal 1 and Family HoldCo eras = the BUSINESS has its own brand identity, independent of any individual. Kay-as-CEO does NOT mean Kay-as-the-face.

**How to apply:** When evaluating Brand alignment, score "does the business have brand strength independent of operator?" Founder-personality-dependent businesses fail. Kay-is-the-face businesses also fail. Even Deal 1 niches must pass this test.

## Rule 4 — Filter vs output: framework asks the QUESTION, never imports the answer

**This rule was violated and re-corrected THREE times in the same Apr 28 session. Take it seriously.**

The buyer fingerprint (or any G&B scoring framework) asks the question: *"What kind of business is Kay the right buyer for?"* The niches that pass the framework are the ANSWER (output) — they live in the Industry Research Tracker, NOT in the framework itself.

**When tempted to write specific industry labels into a framework dimension — DON'T.** Even when the master Charter document itself names candidates inline (e.g., "Bridge — Business Services / Vertical SaaS, candidates: Specialty Healthcare SaaS / female-empowered jewel niche / pest control luxury markets"), the candidates are part of the Charter's own narrative description, NOT meant to be imported into derivative frameworks like the buyer fingerprint or scoring rubric.

**Pattern the violation took (Apr 28):**
1. First draft: I wrote "Bridge = Vertical SaaS" inside the Stage fit dimension. Kay corrected.
2. After SaaS macro-retraction: I wrote "Bridge = Business Services / Vertical SaaS umbrella" because that's how her master Charter copy reads. Kay corrected again.
3. Third try: still slipped industry references back in. Kay corrected a third time: *"i dont think our goal in this fingerprint is to outline the industries in the fingerprint - its to ask the questions what business are you the right buyer for? And then that will answer the indsutry niche question"*

**How to apply (airtight version):**
- When drafting any G&B scoring framework dimension, define every test FUNCTIONALLY only. Use characteristics, not industry names.
- "Bridge = exit-bound 5-7yr hold, $2.5M+ EBITDA, asset-light, scalable, recurring rev, defensible, fragmented, LP-exit-compatible, strong management OR 1-2yr promote-from-within." That's correct.
- "Bridge = Business Services / Vertical SaaS / Specialty Healthcare SaaS." That's wrong — those are answers to the question the framework asks, not part of the question itself.
- Same applies to Brand alignment, Stage fit, Network leverage, every other dimension. The fingerprint is the question; niches are the answer.
- If the master Charter document mentions specific industries inline, keep them in the Charter file as historical context but DO NOT propagate them into derivative frameworks.

**Litmus test:** if the framework can be applied to a niche Kay has never considered before, it's working. If it pre-lists the niches that pass, it's broken.
