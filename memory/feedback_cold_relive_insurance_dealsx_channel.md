When pipeline-manager or Active-Deals-walk surfaces "cold-but-live" insurance brokers (Contacted but no reply in 30-60d), the re-engagement attempt belongs to **DealsX**, not Kay's direct Gmail channel.

**Why:** DealsX runs Kay's high-volume cold + warm-restart infrastructure for the insurance vertical (and is the channel-of-record for any insurance niche — DealsX Email per Industry Research Tracker). Kay's direct Gmail is reserved for warm-tier conversations (named-intro, peer-conversation, owner-direct, intermediary-relationship). Routing cold-restarts through Kay direct creates a double-touch problem (DealsX warming sequence + Kay direct = inbox spam + sender-rep risk) AND duplicates work Sam's team already does at scale.

**How to apply:**
- When a re-engagement candidate surfaces from any source (relationship-manager, Active Deals walk, target-discovery refill), **check the Industry Research Tracker channel for the niche FIRST**. If channel = DealsX Email → re-engagement is DealsX's job, do NOT surface as a Kay-Email nudge candidate.
- Apply specifically to insurance vertical (Specialty Insurance Brokerage and any related sub-niches) — that's the confirmed DealsX channel as of 2026-05-01.
- Generalize to any future DealsX-channel niche.
- Active-deal cleanup (archive vs nudge) is a separate decision — archive recommendations are still Kay's call. Only the *nudge action* itself routes to DealsX.
- A cold-but-live broker can still be flagged in Kay's briefing as a data point ("X brokers in DealsX queue need re-warming"), but never as "RECOMMEND: draft warm nudge to X."
