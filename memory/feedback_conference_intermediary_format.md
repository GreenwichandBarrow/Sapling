Conference pipeline is grass-roots intermediary networking: broker breakfasts, ACG chapter events, XPX breakfasts, AM&AA, accounting/law firm client breakfasts, coffee chats with deal-makers. NOT big industry conferences (SaaStr, HIMSS, major ETA flagships).

**Why:** Greg Donus / Smart Tours story (Helen Guo SMB Deal Hunter podcast) — found his $25M acquisition via a 7am Long Island broker breakfast → insurance broker → BDO connector → accountant → deal. Kay realized her prior conference search was looking in owner rooms; river guides live in intermediary rooms. Grass-roots M&A networking is also low-cost, morning-heavy (fits her kid schedule), and lets relationships compound over months.

**How to apply:** When running conference-discovery or river-guide-builder, prioritize intermediary events (brokers, CPAs, M&A advisors, M&A attorneys, wealth managers). Vertical industry conferences are secondary — 1-2/month max. Any big flagship conference needs explicit Kay ask; do not auto-surface.
