Email drafts should include a day-of-week-aware closing line above the sign-off when send-day is Friday afternoon or weekend.

**Why:** Calibration signal 2026-04-25 from James Emden reply edit. Kay added "have a great weekend!" before the "Very best, Kay" sign-off because the email was drafted Saturday afternoon for likely Saturday or Monday-AM send. The temporal warmth read more human than a flat "Looking forward to chatting."

**How to apply:**
- **Friday afternoon → Sunday send-day:** include "have a great weekend!" or similar before sign-off
- **Sunday evening / Monday AM:** include "hope you have a great week ahead" or similar
- **Tuesday-Thursday standard:** no temporal close needed; the nicety opener already covers it
- Use exclamation point for warmth on the temporal close — it's relational, not transactional
- Don't force it if the email's tone is more formal/transactional (e.g., declines, vendor notes)

**Relationship to other rules:**
- Per `feedback_no_sunday_emails`, weekend drafts schedule for Monday AM. The temporal close should reflect the SEND day, not the DRAFT day. So a Saturday draft for Monday-AM send → "hope you have a great week ahead," not "have a great weekend." Confirm send-day before picking the close.
- Per `feedback_email_niceties`, the warm opener and temporal close are complementary — opener anchors empathy, temporal close anchors timing.
