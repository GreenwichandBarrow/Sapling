## The four-gate screen (apply pre-LOI on every deal)

A target deal must clear ALL four gates simultaneously to proceed past initial screen:

1. **$300K Kay salary supportable** — post-acquisition operating CEO comp at her hard floor (per `user_kay_life_goals.md`). Below this is a non-starter regardless of other dimensions.
2. **15%+ EBITDA margin** — signals pricing power, operational leverage, defensible position. Sub-15% margins in service businesses indicate wage-pressured / commoditized models that don't match the operating thesis.
3. **35% investor IRR clearable** — Anacapa-style hurdle (per `project_g_b_doctrine_kristin_lessons.md`). Variables that affect this: entry multiple, deal size, industry growth headroom. All three must support the math.
4. **Senior debt service + working capital affordable** — typical 5x EBITDA deal with 50-60% debt at 8-10% interest. Working capital needs vary by business type (manufacturing has more inventory drag; pure services less).

## Why $2M EBITDA is the practical floor

Below $2M EBITDA, the math breaks even before considering screen criteria:

| EBITDA | Salary | IRR | Net |
|--------|--------|-----|-----|
| $1M | Affordable but breaks IRR (~21%) | Fails 35% hurdle | **REJECT** |
| $2M | Affordable, IRR tight (~30%) | Borderline | **EVALUATE** |
| $3M | Comfortable, IRR clears (~33%) | Pass | **PROCEED** |
| $5M+ | Comfortable, IRR exceeds (35%+) | Pass | **PROCEED** |

The $300K salary is technically affordable at $1M EBITDA, but combined with the 35% IRR hurdle, sub-$2M deals can't generate enough free cash to clear both simultaneously. **$2M EBITDA is the minimum-viable-deal threshold.**

## Buy-box ranges (three contexts)

Different settings for different surfaces — same underlying buy-box:

| Context | EBITDA | Revenue | Margin floor |

| **Public-facing one-liner** | "$2-10M EBITDA, lower middle market, service-driven" | (implicit) | (implicit) |

No max margin in external copy. Higher margin = better, no upper limit. The 60% bracket used in earlier math was for filter-corner calculations only, not for external messaging.

## How to apply

- **At intake (CIM, broker email, target sheet entry):** apply the four-gate screen before deeper diligence. If any gate fails, decline politely and move on.
- **CFO agent owns the IRR + debt-service math.** Surface the calculation when borderline, not just when fit is borderline.
- **Templates Doc + Axial settings synced 2026-04-30** to the new buy-box. Other matching systems (BizBuySell profile, future broker registrations) should match.
- **Deal-screen language for surfaces:** "$2-10M EBITDA, 15%+ margins, service-driven, founder- or family-owned, US-based, lower middle market." Five anchors.
