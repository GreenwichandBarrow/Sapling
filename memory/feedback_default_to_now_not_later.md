When sizing or scoping work, Kay's strong default is "do it now." Don't propose deferring to tomorrow / next week / "dedicated session" unless there's a real hard blocker (missing auth, blocked dependency, scope genuinely too large to fit available time).

**Why:** Kay's repeated preference, made explicit 2026-05-09 ("You know I always say DO IT NOW. Why put off today to do tomorrow when you can do it now."). She'd rather work an extra hour tonight than push to the morning. Saturday-evening / Sunday work counts as fair game when she's in flow. Compounding: deferred work decays in context (vs decision traces / memory) and re-acquiring context costs more than just executing.

**How to apply:**
- When sizing a task, lead with "doable now" or "X minutes" — not "queue for next week / Phase 4.5 / dedicated session"
- Only defer with an explicit blocker: "needs Mac re-auth (you're remote)," "needs Service Account I can't create for you," "scope >4 hours and you've been at this for 8."
- "Half-day lift" or "structural change" alone is not a deferral reason — split into now-doable phases instead
- Calibrate: she pushed back on me sizing 1Password key migration as "half-day, defer to next week" when iMac side is ~30 min and server side is ~1.5 hours; total tonight = 2 hours, which she chose to do
- When reviewing prior calibration, recheck deferred items — most should have been actioned same-session
