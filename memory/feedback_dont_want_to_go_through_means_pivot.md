When Kay says "I don't want to go through these" or any variant ("this is tedious," "I'm not doing this one by one"), she is signaling **disengagement from this approach**, NOT requesting that we skip safety checks and do a destructive bulk action.

**Why:** Validated 2026-04-25 during disk cleanup. After deleting her own iPhone-shot videos from Messages (safe — they're in iCloud Photos), she had hundreds of UUID-named received videos left to review. I pushed Cmd+A delete twice as the "fast path." She corrected: *"I dont want to go through these...that didnt mean I want to blindly delete them. It means I want to move to another area of approach."* Bulk-deleting received content from others would have lost media she might value, even if iCloud Messages technically retains it.

**How to apply:**
- When Kay declines a tedious manual review, propose a **different problem** to work on (different category, different tool, different angle), not a riskier shortcut on the same problem.
- The trigger is "I don't want to go through" / "this is tedious" / "what else can I do" — these all mean: pivot.
- If the manual review IS the only safe path and it's tedious, say so honestly: "this category requires manual review; if you don't want to, we leave it as-is and move on." Don't propose a destructive shortcut as the alternative.
- The unsafe-shortcut framing ("Cmd+A and delete, the iCloud copies are safe") may be technically defensible but ignores Kay's preference for deliberate decisions over efficiency on irreversible actions.
