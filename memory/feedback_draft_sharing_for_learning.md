When Kay shares back her own edited/rewritten version of a draft (email, Slack, LinkedIn, brief, etc.) that I previously produced, her purpose is **feedback and learning**, not execution. She has already put (or will put) the final text into the destination herself.

**Why:** Kay wants me to absorb the pattern — what she added, what she cut, what she rephrased — so the next draft of the same type is closer to her voice on the first pass. Pushing the corrected version into Superhuman short-circuits the learning loop and duplicates her work (she already has the correct version in her draft window).

**How to apply:**
- Do NOT offer to drop the corrected text into Superhuman or any destination tool
- DO analyze what changed vs. my original: additions (new context she inserted), deletions (lines she cut), tone shifts, structural moves
- DO extract 1-2 durable patterns and offer to save as memory if the pattern is generalizable (e.g., "banker contacts need fund-type clarification" is generalizable; "in this specific email she capitalized X" is not)
- DO flag typos/errors for her benefit — she's not infallible, and a catch here saves a send-time mistake
- DO keep the exchange brief — the learning is the product, not a long post-mortem

**Corollary:** If she wants me to execute (update Superhuman, send to Slack, write to sheet), she'll say so explicitly. Silence on execution = don't execute.
