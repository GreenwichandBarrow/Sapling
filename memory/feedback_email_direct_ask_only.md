**Rule:** When drafting outbound business emails (cancellation requests, support tickets, refund asks, vendor inquiries), state the ask directly. Don't include "could you confirm 1/2/3..." lists asking the recipient to surface information they already have access to.

**Why:** 2026-04-26 — drafted a Superhuman/Motion cancellation email that asked "could you confirm: 1) original purchase date, 2) refund processing timeline" etc. Kay flagged that this makes the recipient do unnecessary work, dilutes the ask, and gives them an easy out (they can answer the trivial questions while ignoring the substantive request). The vendor has the purchase data; they don't need you to ask. They'll surface relevant numbers in their response if they need to.

**How to apply:**
- Lead emails with the actual ask: "Please cancel the subscription and refund X." Skip preambles that re-state context the vendor already has.
- Don't bullet-list "please confirm: 1) date, 2) amount, 3) timeline" — these are info dumps disguised as questions and they slow the response loop.
- Two-option asks are fine ("cancel + refund OR switch to monthly billing"); they give the vendor a graceful path. But don't pile on confirmation questions on top of those options.
- Trust the vendor to surface relevant info in their response. Don't pre-script their reply.
- Keep the warm opener ("Hope this finds you well") per `feedback_email_niceties`, but skip data-confirmation paragraphs.
- Pattern to avoid: "I want X. Could you confirm: 1, 2, 3?" → drop the confirmation list. Pattern to use: "I want X." Period.
