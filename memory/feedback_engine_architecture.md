G&B runs on a four-role engine architecture. Kay's calendar is the scarcest resource in the company, and the engine exists to keep her in front of owners and intermediaries as close to 100% of working hours as possible.

**The architecture:**
- **Kay** — in front of business owners and intermediaries. That's it. Conferences, owner calls, intermediary meetings, advisor calls, deal-close relationships. This is the ONLY high-leverage activity.
- **DealsX** — email + LinkedIn DM outreach channel. Runs in the background.
- **JJ** — cold calls (10am-2pm ET Mon-Fri). Runs in the background.
- **Claude (Chief of Staff)** — niche identification/scoring, deal aggregation, email drafting, meeting prep, conference pipeline, relationship cadences, tracker management, investor updates, every administrative task. Runs in the background.

**Why:** Kay is a relationship-driven deal finder (ENTJ-A Commander, Wedgwood archetype, conviction-through-depth). The edge G&B has over searchers/PE is legacy-motivated sellers picking Kay because they met her and trusted her — that only happens in person. Every hour Kay spends on admin, drafting, research, or triage is an hour not spent generating the single thing that closes deals: personal relationships with sellers and the people who refer them.

**How to apply:**
- Default assumption: if a task is not "Kay in front of an owner or intermediary," the engine owns it — not Kay.
- When a task surfaces, ask: "Can DealsX, JJ, or Claude handle this end-to-end?" If yes, do it without asking. If no, identify what single decision Kay needs to make and present only that (RECOMMEND + YES/NO/DISCUSS).
- Never expand Kay's admin surface area. If I catch myself asking her to review N items, compress to 1 recommendation or batch to a weekly review.
- If I notice the engine leaking work onto Kay (she's doing her own scheduling, her own prep, her own drafting), flag it as a skill gap and propose the subagent/skill that absorbs it.
- Conference attendance is engine OUTPUT (Kay's time), not engine work — protect it fiercely per `feedback_in_person_conferences_highest_roi`.
- Measurement: Kay's calendar should trend toward 80%+ external meetings (owners/intermediaries/advisors). Internal admin time is engine failure.
