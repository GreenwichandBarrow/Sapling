When triaging an inbound from someone identifying as an "M&A advisor" or "boutique M&A firm," **check what tool they're offering before evaluating the warmth of the contact**. Free valuations, Value Range Analyses (VRAs), pre-sale audits, exit-readiness assessments, and free benchmarking reports are sell-side prospecting tools — the advisor uses them to recruit owners onto their listing pipeline. They're NOT bringing buyers deal flow; they're working the seller side and exposing those deals to a wide buyer pool (the same pattern as Calder Capital, Transworld, BizBuySell brokers).

A buy-side intermediary would offer the OPPOSITE: deal pipeline access, target lists, sector intros, deal-flow shares. They'd ask about your buy-box, target sectors, and deal size — not offer to value your company.

**Why:** May 1 2026 — Bob Williamson (Cetane Associates, Pest & Lawn director) followed up after a warm NJPMA workshop intro by offering a free Value Range Analysis. The warmth pattern was strong (in-person lunch, follow-up next day, niche-adjacent — pest M&A advisory ties directly to JJ's active cold-call sprint). Surface signals all read "warm intermediary." Layer mismatch made it a no-engagement decision: Cetane is sell-side, the VRA is sell-side prospecting, engaging would either consume time politely or signal G&B as a sell-side referral source. Cetane is the second sell-side advisor encountered in two weeks (after Calder Capital fee-structure work). Pattern is recurring; warrants a durable rule.

**How to apply:**
- Tool aim = layer identifier. Tool aimed at OWNERS (valuation, audit, exit prep) → sell-side. Tool aimed at BUYERS (pipeline, target lists) → buy-side.
- Don't be fooled by professionalism + warmth + niche-adjacency. All three were strong with Cetane and the layer was still wrong.
- Surface action: maintain politeness from any existing in-person warmth (don't burn the bridge), but no follow-up outreach effort. No 20-min intro call ask. No reply if a reply isn't already required.
- Cross-reference: this is the same logic as `feedback_broker_competition.md` (broker deals go to 3000+ buyers, G&B rarely wins) — sell-side advisor relationships have the same competitive-flooding problem.
