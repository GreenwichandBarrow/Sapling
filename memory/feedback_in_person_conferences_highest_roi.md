One in-person conference per week is a high-conviction deal-sourcing strategy for G&B, and should be defended against reallocation to other channels.

**Why:** In the first two weeks of running this cadence (starting week of 2026-04-13), the second conference produced a live deal pitch — a founder-led, widow-owned, absentee-run NY Metro aerospace/defense business with strong contracts and a legacy-over-multiples seller. That conversion rate (2 conferences → 1 real deal) beats every digital sourcing channel G&B runs. Corporate advisors, wealth managers, and intermediaries pitch deals to people they meet in person; they don't pitch deals to inbox cold emails.

**How to apply:**
- Protect the 1-conference-per-week cadence. Do not let calendar pressure or "we're too busy" talk Kay out of attending.
- When proposing conferences, weight in-person meaningfully higher than virtual or digital-equivalent sourcing.
- When a deal surfaces at a conference, treat the pipeline (advisor follow-up, NDA, screening calls) as same-day priority — the warm in-person intro is the unlock, and stale warm intros cool fast.
- Use this pattern in trade-offs: if Kay is deciding between a conference and a lower-value deliverable, the conference wins.
- Revisit cadence upward (2/week) only if sustained capacity exists AND Kay is getting throughput on the inbound those conferences generate.
