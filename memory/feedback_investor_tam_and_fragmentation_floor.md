Two hard investor-driven floors apply to every G&B niche evaluation, in addition to the 3-step messy-business-great-segment filter:

**Floor 1: Growth TAM ≥ $500M** (US market or G&B's addressable scope, not global vanity TAM). This is in `project_frameworks_scorecards` as an investor requirement and per Kay's investor commitments. Niches with smaller TAMs cannot scale to a credible exit story for the investor base.

**Floor 2: Thousands of VIABLE ACQUISITION TARGETS** (not just thousands of total operators). The precise metric is:
- Total operator count is a misleading proxy — most operators in any category are sub-scale (sole proprietors, <$1M revenue)
- The actual number that matters: **companies in G&B's buy box ($5-50M revenue, $1.5-5M+ EBITDA), owner-operated (not PE-owned, not large-corporate-subsidiary), geographically distributed**
- Roll-up math depends on:
  - Many shots on goal (only 1-3% of contacted owners say yes; need a deep funnel of QUALIFIED targets)
  - Win rate doesn't have to be high (low conversion is fine if QUALIFIED pool is deep)
  - Geographic flexibility (if NYC's 5 buy-box-fit operators all reject, you can pivot to Boston without restarting)
  - Selection quality (can wait for the right owner-fit instead of taking what comes)
  - Cadence (1-2 tuck-ins/year requires a constant pipeline of QUALIFIED available targets)

**Calibration heuristic:** A category with ~10,000+ buy-box-fit owner-operated targets (like HVAC, landscape) is roll-up-viable. A category with ~100-500 buy-box-fit targets (like specialty coffee equipment service, art storage) yields one anchor + possibly 1-2 add-ons but cannot sustain a 5-7 acquisition roll-up engine over 5 years.

Niches with hundreds of viable targets may be GREAT one-and-done acquisitions or tuck-ins inside a larger HoldCo, but they are NOT investor-viable as standalone roll-up anchors.

## Where Specialty Niches Fit (Not Anchors, Possibly Tuck-Ins)

Specialty niches that fail TAM/fragmentation floors are NOT G&B anchors. They might still be:
- **Tuck-in service lines** inside a broader anchor's HoldCo (e.g., add specialty espresso service inside a broader hospitality services platform)
- **Second-platform moves** after the anchor proves out (years 5-7)
- **Investor curiosities** Kay tracks personally but doesn't deploy capital toward
- **Customer-set proxies** (specialty coffee customers = boutique hospitality, useful for understanding broader hospitality services)

But they CANNOT be the search-fund anchor by themselves. The investor mandate requires scale.

## How To Apply

**For every new niche evaluated:**

Add to the 3-step filter as Step 0 (must pass before continuing):

**Step 0 — Investor floors (BLOCKING)**
- Growth TAM in US ≥ $500M? If no, kill or reclassify as tuck-in candidate.
- Thousands of operators (≥ ~3,000)? If no, kill or reclassify as tuck-in candidate.
- If both pass, proceed to Step 1-3 of the messy-business filter.

**For the niche set we've been surfacing:**

The art-anchored and luxury-specialty niches we've discussed (specialty coffee equipment service, wine cellaring, auction logistics, specialty appraisal, family office bookkeeping) are interesting and pass the messy-business filter, but **most fail the investor floors as standalone anchors.** Reframe them as potential tuck-in service lines or customer-set proxies inside a larger anchor.

**The candidate anchor niches that pass ALL filters:**
- Pest management
- HVAC services
- Pool / spa services
- Landscape services
- Commercial / specialty cleaning
- Restoration services
- Commercial security monitoring

These are large, deeply fragmented services categories. Within each, **G&B can apply the luxury-adjacent / specialty positioning** as the operating thesis — but the BASE category has to clear the investor floors.

**The luxury-anchored vision still works** — but as a positioning layer ON TOP of a large fragmented base, not as the base itself.
