The "SaaS Filter" (Jake + Adam's combined criteria) is a hard pass/fail gate for any software niche:

**Jake Stoller (Riverside) 7 dimensions:** system-of-record, compliance-driven, high cost of failure, domain expertise, long sales cycles, reference-driven customer base, data moats.

**Adam (Datacor/PeaceCare) business-structure pattern:** high recurring revenue, compliance stickiness, non-technical buyers, 7-10yr replacement cycles, fragmented customer base, modular expansion.

**Application scope — SaaS niches only:**
- Applies: any niche tagged with a DealsX Industry bucket (Specialty Healthcare PM/EHR, EH&S Software, etc.) or otherwise classified as vertical SaaS.
- Does NOT apply: services (Premium Pest Management, Private Art Advisory, Estate Management), brokerage (Specialty Insurance), distribution, or other non-SaaS niches. Those continue running through the existing G&B industry scorecard + buy-box criteria.

**Pipeline placement:**
- Niche-intelligence Step 2 (Identification) must check the SaaS Filter before proceeding to Step 3 (one-pager) for any SaaS-classified niche.
- Niches that fail the filter are tabled with reasoning; no one-pager/scorecard created.
- Tracker has a "SaaS Filter" column: pass/fail for SaaS niches, blank for non-SaaS.

**Why:** April 12 2026 — G&B industry scorecard penalizes Porter's + Size/Fragmentation, which are structural features of mature vertical SaaS categories (consolidators exist). Jake + Adam REWARD the same structure the G&B scorecard penalizes. Two tools, two purposes: filter qualifies niches, scorecard ranks qualified niches. A 2.25 scorecard on a filter-pass SaaS niche is more interesting than a 2.57 scorecard on a filter-fail SaaS niche.

**How to apply:** (1) Tag each niche as SaaS or non-SaaS. (2) For SaaS niches, run Jake + Adam filter first. (3) Only scorecard niches that pass. (4) Record Filter result on tracker as a visible column. Non-SaaS niches bypass this and skip straight to the G&B scorecard path.
