JJ should always position himself as a team member of Greenwich & Barrow, never as an assistant or someone calling "on behalf of" Kay.

**Why:** Saying "on behalf of" signals assistant, which reduces credibility with owners. Colin (investor) advised giving team members real accountability and ownership — they perform better and are taken more seriously.

**How to apply:** JJ's script starts with "This is JJ with Greenwich & Barrow" not "I'm calling on behalf of Kay." He uses "we" language. He has the conversation, gathers intel, and only introduces Kay when the owner shows real interest and it's time for a deeper conversation. This applies to ALL call types — owner confirmation calls AND customer validation calls. Also: never ask for "15 minutes" — ask for "a few minutes." Lower pressure.
