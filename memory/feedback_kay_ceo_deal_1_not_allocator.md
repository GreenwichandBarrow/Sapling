# Kay is CEO for Deal 1 (Apr 28 2026)

**Rule (refined Apr 28 same session, third pass):** Kay is the CEO of G&B Deal 1 from acquisition. Succession path is OPEN — promote-from-within, external CEO hire, or extended Kay tenure all in scope, **determined by what the business actually needs, not by a pre-locked timeline.** The master Charter mentions 1-2yr operator-seat exit as ONE possibility; Kay (Apr 28) explicitly rejected making it prescriptive: *"I am open to both options. I just want to buy and run a business."*

**Why:** Search-fund traditional model has the searcher as initial operator-CEO, then succession plays out per the business reality. Don't over-engineer the timeline. The Charter's 1-2yr language describes one possible path; the actual succession depends on what management is in place, what gaps exist, what the business needs from Kay over time, and how long she wants to operate hands-on vs. shift to chairman/allocator.

**Family HoldCo era (Deals 2-4) is different:** allocator-mode applies from day one with hired CEOs. That distinction holds.

**How to apply:**
- When evaluating a niche for Deal 1, score for **viable management path** (strong existing management, promotable internal candidates, OR amenable to external CEO hire) — but don't impose a 1-2yr exit deadline. Kay is open to running the business as long as it needs.
- When drafting deliverables, frame Kay's operator role flexibly: "CEO from day one; succession determined by business reality."
- First-year hires planning: CFO/COS (analyst pre-slated) + VP Sales (fresh build). Successor for the operator seat is identified over time as the business reveals what it needs.
- For Family HoldCo deals (2-4), allocator-mode applies from day one with hired CEOs.
- DO NOT lock in a "Year 1-2 operator → Year 2+ allocator" phased timeline. That over-prescribes.
- **DO NOT bake socrates / aspirational / Charter framings into current-state operational docs.** Buy-boxes, outreach templates, scorecards, briefs, and other operational deliverables describe TODAY's G&B Deal 1 reality.
