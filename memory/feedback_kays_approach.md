## How Kay Makes Decisions

1. **Conviction through depth, not breadth.** Kay builds conviction by going deep on one thesis until she either has full confidence or kills it with data. The specialty insurance deck is the example — she didn't skim 20 insurance sub-niches, she built a comprehensive deck on one and used it to test conviction with investors and advisors.

2. **Parallel tracks, first to convert wins.** The Chanel interview model: run 3 tracks simultaneously, 10 targets each. Whichever produces a signed LOI first wins. This is how she got the Chanel job (9-month process). Applied to the search: insurance, estate management, and one other niche running simultaneously.

3. **Lived experience over research.** Kay values knowing things from being INSIDE them. She knows commercial cleaning is a viable niche because she saw it from inside Chanel — not from a market report. When the system presents research she already knows is wrong (or incomplete), she gets frustrated. The system should mine HER data (calls, emails, OneNote, transcripts) first, then supplement with web research.

4. **Gets frustrated when the system doesn't find what she knows exists.** Kay has 13 months of conversations, emails, call notes, and handwritten notebooks. She knows there are leads and insights buried in that data. When the system searches the web instead of her own data, or misses something she mentioned in a call 4 months ago, it feels like wasted effort.

5. **Strong opinions formed over 13 months.** The system should leverage Kay's accumulated knowledge, not start from scratch. She has already evaluated 150+ niches, had hundreds of conversations, and built deep expertise in insurance, art, and luxury ecosystems. Ask her what she thinks before presenting research that contradicts her experience.

6. **She doesn't quit.** Chanel took 9 months. The search is at 13 months. She has 9 months of runway left. There is no scenario where she walks away — the only question is WHAT she acquires, not WHETHER.

7. **She's at 13 months with 9 months of runway.** This isn't theoretical. She needs to find the right niche NOW, start outreach, and have an LOI signed within 6 months. Commercial cleaning is the backstop at 18 months. The system should treat every day as precious.

## How to Apply

- Mine Kay's data FIRST (calls, emails, Fireflies transcripts, OneNote, vault), then supplement with web research
- When presenting niches or leads, check if Kay has already evaluated them (search vault, emails, call notes)
- Don't start from scratch on any topic she's discussed before — find the prior context and build on it
- Present her own words back to her when relevant ("You told August Felker in November that...")
- When she says something exists in her data, believe her and find it
- Prioritize depth on insurance (the conviction thesis) over breadth across new niches
