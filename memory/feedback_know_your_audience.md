Always know your audience before creating content. Understand the platform, the reader, and the context before drafting anything.

**Why:** Kay caught a buyer criteria draft for Empire Flippers (digital business marketplace) that described wanting non-digital B2B service businesses. The content was accurate to the thesis but wrong for the audience. It would have signaled "I don't belong here."

**How to apply:**
- Before drafting any external-facing content (buyer profiles, outreach emails, platform registrations, conference bios), research who will read it and what they expect
- Tailor language, criteria, and framing to the platform's niche (e.g., SaaS language for SaaS marketplaces, insurance language for insurance conferences)
- If the thesis doesn't fit the audience, say so — don't force generic content into a mismatched context
- This applies everywhere: broker emails, investor updates, LinkedIn posts, conference materials, platform profiles
