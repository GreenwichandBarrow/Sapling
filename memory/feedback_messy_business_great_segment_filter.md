The G&B HoldCo operating filter, codified 2026-04-19. Apply this two-step check to every new niche, target, or strategic decision.

## The Filter (Updated 2026-04-19 — added Step 3)

**Step 1: Is the SEGMENT structurally good?**
- Recurring or compounding demand
- Demographic / wealth tailwind (HNW growth, boomer wealth transfer, art market growth, etc.)
- Defensible (relationship-based, regulated, switching costs)
- Non-discretionary spending drivers (insurance, estate, tax, regulatory, collector cycles)

**Step 2: Are individual BUSINESSES messy enough to be acquirable cheaply?**
- Fragmented mom-and-pops
- No software, no systems, no professional management
- Owner-operated, owner-fatigued, no clear succession
- Multi-revenue-stream "mess" that can be segmented at acquisition (the Acumen pattern — separate storage from transport from insurance)
- Cost-heavy operations not demand-constrained
- 8-15% margins typical (high enough to survive, low enough to make sellers humble on multiple)

**Step 3: Is the SERVICE itself AI-resistant?** (Added 2026-04-19 per Kay's appraisal-rejection)
- **AI ATTACKS COSTS** in physical-workflow service businesses (Saltoun thesis) — this is what we want
- **AI SUBSTITUTES THE WORK** in knowledge-work service businesses — this is what we avoid
- Physical-workflow = on-site work, climate-controlled storage, transport, installation, security response, pest treatment. Cannot be virtualized.
- Knowledge work = appraisal, provenance verification, basic bookkeeping, contract review, legal research, image-pattern matching, compliance forms. AI eats pricing within 2-3 years.
- Regulated services that REQUIRE human credentials/signatures (insurance brokerage, USPAP-style certified work) are partially AI-resistant but still under price pressure on the volume tier
- Hybrid services (some knowledge work, some physical) need a clean lens applied — what % of value is in the AI-substitutable layer?

**If YES to all three → AI + capital is the lever.** AI fixes the operational mess at low marginal cost. Capital funds the consolidation + SaaS overlay + tech upgrades. **The work itself doesn't get eaten.**

**If Step 3 fails, kill the niche regardless of Step 1+2 strength.** Knowledge-work services that look attractive (recurring, fragmented, sticky) get repriced by AI substitution within the hold period — your acquisition multiple is paid before the AI tide compresses the underlying revenue.

## Why This Filter Matters

**Don't confuse "bad business" with "messy business."**
- **Bad business** = no path to value. Commodity, race-to-bottom, dying segment, structural margin compression.
- **Messy business** = operationally disorganized inside a structurally sound segment. Fixable.

The Andrew Saltoun lesson: he bought what he called a "terrible" prosthetics business. But the SEGMENT was structurally great (insurance reimbursement + demographic tailwind + sticky patient relationships + recurring need). The business was messy, not bad. His will + work fixed the mess. Inside a sound segment, that work compounds. Inside a bad segment, the same work goes to zero.

## Why The Order Matters

**Run Step 1 BEFORE Step 2.** A messy business in a bad segment is just a bad business. Even with AI + capital + perfect execution, the segment ceiling caps the upside.

A good business in a good segment is also wrong for G&B — it's expensive and there's nothing to fix. We don't have the LBO premium to pay for already-clean businesses.

The G&B sweet spot is the intersection: **messy + great**.

## Examples (post-2026-04-19 reframe)

**Messy in great segment (PURSUE):**
- Art storage & logistics (Acumen profile) — demand abundant, cost-heavy, fragmented mom-and-pops, AI-attackable
- Wine cellaring & logistics — twin pattern to art storage, same customer base
- Specialty appraisal services — solo practitioners, manual workflows, permanent insurance/estate/tax demand
- Estate liquidation services — boomer wealth transfer secular tailwind, fragmented chaotic operators
- Family office outsourced bookkeeping — manual processes, recurring revenue, growing HNW market
- HNW residential security monitoring — 24/7 staffing cost problem, fragmented, recurring revenue
- Auction logistics support — fragmented sub-contractors to major auction houses

**Bad business (REJECT even if messy):**
- Standalone art transport without storage — commoditized, race to bottom
- Generic moving / relocation — race to bottom
- Small art galleries themselves — illiquid inventory, secular decline of physical retail
- Generic shuttle freight — saturated, race to bottom (Acumen outsources this themselves for a reason)

**Step 3 fails — knowledge work being eaten by AI (REJECT even if Step 1+2 pass):**
- Specialty Appraisal services — image recognition + comps database + AI report generation already commoditizing volume work (Mearto, ValueMyStuff, Heritage Auctions AI tools)
- Provenance verification — AI authentication + database matching compresses billing
- Basic bookkeeping for family offices — AI invoice processing + categorization eats the bottom of the market
- Yacht crew matching — AI candidate matching eats the high-margin layer; background checks + document management persist as services
- Generic compliance forms / paralegal-style work — AI substitution well underway

**Good business in any segment (TOO EXPENSIVE):**
- Established consolidators (Iron Mountain, Crozier post-acquisition, UOVO) — already cleaned up, multiples too high
- Vertical SaaS at scale (Voxme is on the right side because still founder-led + small)

## How To Apply

- **For every new niche idea:** Run Step 1 first. If segment is questionable, kill before doing the niche-intelligence one-pager. Saves analyst time.
- **For every acquisition target:** Run Step 1 on the segment, then Step 2 on the company. If the target is "good" inside a "messy" peer set, ask whether you're paying for the cleanup work that makes the rest of the segment cheaper. Sometimes the messy peer is the better acquisition than the polished anchor.
- **For tracker review:** Re-score existing niches against this filter. Ones that pass both steps stay; ones that fail Step 1 get killed regardless of operational beauty.
- **For DealsX outreach:** Sam's list should over-index on segment quality. Within a great segment, target the messiest operators, not the cleanest.
