Morning briefings must be categorized by action type, not by data source. Kay wants to see what she needs to review/approve vs what's ready to go.

**Why:** Kay processes information by action. "What do I need to review?" is a faster decision loop than "here's what happened in email, here's what happened in pipeline."

**How to apply:** Every morning briefing follows this structure:

```
Pipeline shifts to review/approve:
1. ...

Pipeline summary:
Active Deals: {total} (breakdown by stage)
Intermediary: {total} (breakdown)
Niches: {n} Active-Diligence, {n} Active-Outreach

Motion action steps to review/approve:
1. ...

Superhuman email drafts to review/approve:
1. ...

Other items / today's agenda:
1. ...
```

These 5 sections appear in this exact order every single day. Consistent format, no variation. Each item is numbered. Each has a clear action (review, approve, decide). No informational items without an ask.
