When Kay needs a ranking of active niches under an acquisition-timeline constraint, the analysis must use **traditional ETA searcher criteria applied to external market data**, not internal vault outputs like river-guide-builder scores, scorecard readings, or "warm network depth."

**Why:** On 2026-04-21, during a strategic pivot conversation after the Guillermo biweekly, two rankings were run back-to-back. The first pass leaned on warm-network depth + tri-state target density + luxury differentiation fit + PE competition, drawing from river-guide-builder outputs and vault scorecards. It ranked Private Art Advisory primary, Art Storage backup. Kay pushed back: "full of holes and fluff." The second pass applied hard traditional-searcher criteria on external data only (IBISWorld, MarshBerry, Capstone, Stanford search-fund study, industry M&A reports) and ranked Specialty Insurance (HNW) primary, Premium Pest backup — with Private Art Advisory and Art Storage disqualified on cyclicality and CAPEX/PE-crowd-out grounds. Kay confirmed: "This list resonates with me much better than the first pass. I agree." The searcher-criteria method produced a defensible, materially different answer that she accepted as valid.

**How to apply:**

1. **Use only the 9 traditional searcher dimensions** when ranking niches under acquisition-timeline constraints:
   - US premium-segment market growth (not overall category)
   - Fragmentation of in-buy-box targets ($2-15M revenue)
   - Typical EBITDA margins (comparable vertical benchmarks)
   - Recurring revenue characteristics
   - Acquisition multiple environment (entry and exit)
   - PE/strategic consolidation intensity (inverted — less PE = better score)
   - Cyclicality / recession resilience (2008-09, COVID, 2022 inflation evidence)
   - Labor / regulatory gate to operating post-close
   - Owner demographic retirement wave

2. **Apply a luxury-fit multiplier (0.9 / 1.0 / 1.1 / 1.2) as a FINAL weight** — not as a primary scoring dimension. It only matters where luxury credibility is a material factor in owner psychology or customer conversion.

3. **Never conflate "warm network" with "niche attractiveness."** Warm network depth is about the person running the search, not the niche. It goes to execution feasibility, not niche ranking.

4. **Source every claim externally.** IBISWorld, Mintel, Statista, SCA, MarshBerry, Capstone, BizBuySell, Stanford search-fund study, BPC survey, 10-K filings, industry trade publications. Flag "inferred" or "unknown" rather than fabricate.

5. **When internal data is thin, do not paper over with inference.** If river-guide tabs are empty or Apollo enrichment is incomplete, flag the gap loudly and force the analysis through external data. Don't let vault-data-quality weaknesses drive the ranking in a direction the underlying market fundamentals don't support.

6. **Tone discipline:** write like a skeptical acquirer, not a consultant. No hedging phrases ("potentially," "may offer," "could be interesting"). Every verdict is a call.
