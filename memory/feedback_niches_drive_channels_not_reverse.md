When discussing new niches, **do NOT bias selection toward niches that "fit JJ's cold-call profile" (or any specific channel).** Niche selection runs on strategic merit only — the messy-business-great-segment + AI-resistance + recurring-revenue + thesis-fit filters.

Channel placement (JJ-Call-Only / Kay Email / DealsX Email / Intermediary outreach) is a downstream operational decision made AFTER the niche is approved.

## The Rule

**Strategic order is:**
1. Does this niche pass the 3-step filter (Step 1 segment, Step 2 mess, Step 3 AI-resistance)?
2. Does it fit G&B's HoldCo thesis (services-to-luxury, B2B institutional layer, customer overlap)?
3. Does it pass the recurring-revenue strictness test (Kay's definition — same client monthly/annual YoY)?
4. **Only then:** which outreach channel reaches the niche's targets best?

**Anti-pattern (do not do this):**
- "What niche fits JJ's cold-call profile?"
- "What niche could Sam call on next?"
- "What niche keeps Kay's email cadence active?"

These are channel-first questions disguised as niche questions. They produce niche selections that serve operational convenience rather than strategic merit. The result is bad niches that look like good operational fits.

**Correct pattern:**
- "Which new niches deserve to be on the tracker as Active-Outreach?"
- "Of the active niches, which targets fit JJ-Call vs Sam DealsX vs Kay Email best?"
- "Is JJ's role still the right design given the niche set we've chosen?" (May lead to JJ doing different work — follow-up calls, intermediary calls, warm-intro support — instead of cold-call on a forced niche.)

## Why This Matters

Surfaced 2026-04-19. Claude proposed Estate Liquidation, Wine Cellaring, Specialty Appraisal, and HNW Residential Security as candidates for JJ's post-5/7 niche (Pest → Sam DealsX transition). Kay corrected: "I'm not building niches to cater to JJ. Let's discuss strategically first, then decide where it lives."

The strategic discussion of any niche should run independently of who would call on it. Otherwise, niches get selected for cold-call ergonomics rather than for thesis fit, and the HoldCo strategy degrades.

## How To Apply

**When proposing new niches:**
- Frame the discussion on segment quality, operational mess, AI-resistance, recurring revenue, customer overlap with the HoldCo
- Do NOT mention "owner answers phone" or "good for cold calls" as a primary justification
- Channel-fit notes are okay as a footnote AFTER the strategic case is made

**When evaluating new niches:**
- Apply the 3-step filter strictly
- Ask whether this strengthens the customer-centric HoldCo thesis (HNW collector / family office / B2B institutional layer)
- Ask whether the niche is recurring per Kay's strict definition

**When deciding channel placement (after niche is approved):**
- Sam DealsX = scaled email outreach to large universes (typically broader, less intimate)
- JJ-Call = phone outreach where owner-answer rate matters and target universe is moderate
- Kay Email = relationship-driven, smaller universe, higher-touch
- Intermediary = niches where deal flow comes from brokers / lawyers / accountants

**When JJ's role doesn't fit any approved niche cleanly:**
- It's okay for JJ's role to evolve — follow-up calls, warm intro support, intermediary calls, due diligence calls on active deals
- Don't force-create a niche to keep JJ in cold-call mode
- His role serves the strategy, not vice versa
