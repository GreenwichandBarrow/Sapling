When proposing outreach from Kay (email, LinkedIn DM, WhatsApp, cold call) to any contact — M&A intermediary, industry expert, potential advisor, target-adjacent person — the first check is whether Kay personally knows them. If she does not, do NOT draft send-ready outreach, do NOT recommend sending, do NOT include in tomorrow-pins as a send-today item.

**Why:** On 2026-04-21, Claude proposed a 6-intermediary outreach sequence for deal-flow validation (3 insurance M&A advisors, 3 pest M&A advisors) with send-ready drafts, Apollo verification plans, and recommended Monday send dates. Kay corrected: "8x these are not people I know. Do not draft or send outreach." Cold outreach from Kay to strangers is categorically not her approach — she builds relationships through warm paths, lived connections, and advisor introductions. Per `feedback_kays_approach.md`: Kay builds conviction through depth and values lived experience. Per `feedback_warm_intro_before_sheet.md`: warm intro paths are a qualification gate. Per the founding G&B approach: outreach from Kay personally carries weight precisely because it's never spam — breaking that discipline degrades her entire sender reputation and credibility.

**How to apply:**

1. **Default rule:** before drafting ANY outreach from Kay, check whether she knows the person. Methods: grep vault for entity / past call / past email; check Attio; ask Kay directly if uncertain.

2. **If Kay does not know them:** do NOT produce a send-ready draft. Acceptable outputs are:
   - A research note identifying the person, their relevance, and why they would be valuable to know
   - A warm-intro-path analysis: "Here are 3 people in your network who might introduce you to X"
   - An ASK back to Kay: "You don't know X directly. Want me to investigate warm-intro paths, or defer?"

3. **If a warm-intro path exists:** draft the intro-request to the mutual connection first, not the cold email to the stranger.

4. **Research-call outreach is still outreach.** "Paid 30-min expert consultation" framing doesn't bypass this rule. If Kay doesn't know them, it's still cold outreach from a name-brand family-office — which either burns reputation on the recipient side or invites transactional responses that waste her time.

5. **Channels where this applies:** Superhuman email drafts, LinkedIn InMail drafts, WhatsApp pings, cold calls routed through JJ (though JJ-to-owner calls are separate and scoped through JJ workflows), any external-facing outreach whatsoever.

6. **Exceptions (rare):** (a) published RFI / inquiry inboxes where the cold inquiry is the designed channel (e.g., a broker's "contact us for listings" page); (b) conference follow-up after an in-person meet; (c) responding to someone who contacted Kay first. Even in these cases, default to surfacing the contact opportunity to Kay rather than auto-drafting.
