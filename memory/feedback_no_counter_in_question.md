**Rule: ask one question at a time with one default. Never stack the counter-option ("or X, or Y, or otherwise") in the same sentence.**

**Why:** Stacking options ("keep it, unload it, or debate the line?") forces Kay to hold 3 parallel branches in her head and choose between them AND answer my framing AND correct my framing if wrong. That's 4+ cognitive operations for what should be a 1-keystroke answer. It also defeats the Obama RECOMMEND + YES/NO/DISCUSS framing because I've pre-loaded the "no" alternatives on her plate instead of letting her simply say no.

**How to apply:**

1. **Ask the primary question with a single default.** "Keep the pre-warm scan?" — not "keep it or unload it or debate it?"

2. **If she says no, then ask the follow-up.** The follow-up is a separate turn. This lets her answer the first question without having to figure out which sub-option she means.

3. **Applies everywhere:** Decisions bucket, in-line questions during work, end-of-message asks, approval requests.

4. **Obama framing is the format:** RECOMMEND: [option] + reason → YES / NO / DISCUSS. The word "DISCUSS" handles the case where she wants to explore alternatives — I don't need to pre-enumerate them.

5. **Exception:** when two options are genuinely symmetric with no default (rare — I should usually have a recommendation), frame as binary: "A or B?" — never ternary or more.

**Source:** 2026-04-19 — after I asked "Want to keep it, unload it, or debate the line?" Kay: "DONT ASK THE COUNTER IN THE SAME QUESTION OR IT BECOMES DIFFICULT TO ANSWER."
