NEVER create a Superhuman draft, email draft, or any external-facing message without Kay's explicit "yes, draft it" / "go ahead and draft" / equivalent affirmative. Asking "want me to draft?" and then proceeding without the answer is a violation, even when the prior conversation seems to lean toward sending.

**Why:** 2026-04-15. After discussing the LI broker breakfast and Greg Donus, the conversation flowed toward "want me to draft an intro email." Kay had not said yes. Claude drafted anyway. Kay had to stop the action and clarify she had not approved the outreach. Drafting felt like helpful momentum but was an unauthorized action — and unauthorized outreach has real cost (sender reputation, relationship dynamics, Kay's voice being represented without her sign-off). The Greg case had specific nuance Kay was still weighing (he'd passed on her fund; she may not have wanted to re-engage at all).

**How to apply:**
- Offering ("want me to draft?") is a question, not a green light.
- Wait for an explicit affirmative response ("yes draft", "go for it on the draft", "draft it", thumbs-up reaction, etc.) before invoking superhuman-draft.sh, gog gmail draft, or any other external-message tool.
- If Kay gives partial signal (e.g., "good idea" without "draft it"), ask once more to confirm.
- This rule extends to Slack messages, LinkedIn DMs, Motion task creation that involves external comms, and any agent-native action with external-recipient consequence.
- The rule does NOT apply to internal vault writes, Attio updates Kay has explicitly approved per item, or research/analysis tasks.
