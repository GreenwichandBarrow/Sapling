In first-touch cold outreach (conference 1:1 requests, intermediary intro emails, owner outreach, broker pings, anyone Kay has not previously corresponded with), **anchor any observation on the FIRM, not the individual.**

**Forbidden in first-touch (reads as creepy):**
- "Congratulations on the recent move to Director" (using LinkedIn promotion date)
- "Saw you joined {firm} in March" (using start date)
- "Noticed you've been at {firm} for 6 years" (using tenure)
- "Saw on LinkedIn that you went to {university}" (using education)
- "Saw your recent post about X" (unless directly relevant to the ask AND the post is from a professional context, not personal)
- Any phrasing that implies Kay went deep on someone she does not know.

**Allowed in first-touch (anchors on the firm):**
- "Your {firm}'s {sector} practice covers {area}, which lines up with the lower-middle-market space I am working in as a buyer."
- "{firm}'s buy-side and sell-side practice in the lower middle market is right in the space I am working in as a buyer."
- "{firm} {division/group} is on my radar as I build out {category} relationships."

**Why:** Cold outreach lives on a thin signal-to-creepy line. Generic firm-level observations are normal due diligence ("I checked their site"). Personal LinkedIn data feels like stalking — even if technically public, it signals to the recipient that you went WAY past normal pre-meeting research depth. The asymmetry: you know specific things about them; they know nothing about you. That tilts the relationship wrong from the first interaction.

**Reverse direction OK:** Once the relationship is warm (post-first-meeting, post-substantive-reply, post-introduction-by-mutual), personal observations are fine and even good — "saw you posted about X, totally agree with your take." First-touch only is the restriction.

**Source:** 2026-05-11 Kay correction on a Lizabeth Roth draft that included "congratulations on the recent move to Director" — Kay flagged as "too creepy" because that's LinkedIn promotion data on someone she hasn't met. The fix: remove the personal note, keep the firm-level observation about Copper Run's LMM practice.
