## Rule (updated 2026-04-30)

When introducing G&B to intermediaries (brokers, IBs, lenders, lawyers, CPAs, family offices, corporate advisors, association heads):

1. **No structural labels in the body.** Drop "search fund," "search vehicle," "holding company," "holding company in formation," and "ETA" terminology. Don't replace with another structural label — just describe what Kay is doing in plain verbs.
2. **Lead with personal voice + curiosity about THEM.** The body should sound like Kay building a relationship with a specific person, not a corporate buyer pitching at a category. Cookie-cutter / form-letter feel is the failure mode.
3. **Buy-box info goes to a footer reference list, not the body.** A small "Buyer profile (for reference)" block after the sign-off is fine. Buy-box bullets in the body are out — they make the email read as a transactional pitch.
4. **Geography stays out of intermediary outreach emails entirely** (per Kay 2026-04-30). NY-Metro / NE-preferred lives in the footer reference if at all, never in the body.
5. **ONE-AND-DONE cadence.** No follow-up sequences. If they don't respond, move on.

## Why

- "Search fund" triggers negative associations in the lower-middle-market intermediary community (perceived as junior, transient, financial-engineering-focused) — Kay 2026-04-29.
- "Holding company in formation" is technically inaccurate (a holding company holds multiple operating businesses; G&B Deal 1 is a single-asset acquisition) and dilutes the Stage-4 Family-HoldCo branding reserved in `project_gb_charter.md` — Kay 2026-04-30.
- Cookie-cutter pitch emails get ignored. Brokers see hundreds of generic buyer pitches; the ones that get replies sound like someone reaching out as a person, not a fund — Kay 2026-04-30.
- Geography in the body is over-specifying for first contact; brokers will surface it on the call if it matters.

## How to apply

**Body structure for intermediary first-reach:**

1. Warm nicety opener ("Hope this finds you well")
2. Warm-intro variant if applicable, otherwise "I came across [Firm] through [source]"
3. Brief who-Kay-is in plain verbs (1-2 sentences max). Example: *"I'm Kay Schneider, founder of Greenwich & Barrow. I'm looking to acquire and run one business for the long term, and I've been spending time getting to know brokers in our space."*
4. Curiosity-led ask: "I'd value 20 minutes to hear what you've been seeing..." — frames the call as Kay learning from THEM, not pitching AT them
5. Pointer to the footer for buy-box reference
6. Sign-off

**Footer (after sign-off):**

> Buyer profile (for reference)
>
> - Founder-led or family-owned, US-based
> - Lower middle market ($1M to $10M EBITDA)
> - Service-driven businesses, recurring revenue or franchise-protected operations
> - Long-term, single-asset orientation (not a roll-up, not a carve-out)
> - Backed by 12 institutional and family-office investors

**Drop entirely from G&B copy:**
- "Committed equity" / "$2.8M committed equity" (false — equity is committed-on-deal, not held in escrow)
- "24-month acquisition window" (sounds urgent / pressure-driven)
- Any "fund" / "search fund" / "holding company" structural label
- Geography in the body of broker emails

**Owner outreach is different.** Owners don't have the search-fund baggage and `feedback_outreach_about_them.md`, `feedback_no_revenue_in_outreach.md`, etc. still apply. This rule is intermediaries-only.
