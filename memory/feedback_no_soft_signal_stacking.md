When drafting an external message (email, Slack, text), audit the message for soft signals before sending. **Stacked soft signals = low conviction.** They tell the recipient "I don't really expect a reply" — exactly the opposite of what Kay wants from her outbound.

**Soft signals (any of these, used in moderation are fine):**
- "Let me know..."
- "No rush..."
- "Happy to chat..."
- "Whenever works..."
- "If you're open..."
- "Should you have time..."
- "If it makes sense..."

**Why:** Day-5 voice trace (2026-05-04) caught a draft with three of these in a four-paragraph email. Reads as the writer hedging because they don't believe the recipient will engage. Owners and intermediaries are time-poor and read tone fast — stacked soft signals telegraph low conviction and earn lower reply rates. The Hangman email (Kay's calibrated voice baseline) uses ZERO soft-signal stacking.

**How to apply:**

1. **One soft signal max per message.** If the draft has more than one, kill the extras.

2. **Every email needs a forward-motion ask.** Replace exit-door-only CTAs ("let me know if you want to chat") with one of:
   - **Specific time window:** "Are you free Tue 2pm or Wed 11am ET?"
   - **Specific question:** "What's the typical owner profile in this segment?"
   - **Specific deliverable:** "I'll send a one-pager Monday — open to feedback before I do?"
   - **Concrete next step:** "I'll loop in [scheduler] to find time this week."

3. **Audit before drafting hits Kay's review.** If the email closes with "let me know if you'd like to chat" and nothing else, rewrite the close. Soft-only closes are calibration debt — Kay will edit them out, so don't ship them.

**Specifically for intermediary outreach:** Brokers and IBs answer to forward-motion. "I'd like to introduce G&B and learn how you source — open to a 20-min call Tue or Thu next week?" beats "happy to chat anytime if there's interest" every time.

**Specifically for owner outreach:** Owners decide on conviction. The opening line and the close both matter. Don't bookend curiosity with hedge.
