NYC-based construction, renovation, design/build, general contractor, and construction-adjacent businesses are a HARD EXCLUDE from G&B's pipeline. The general buy-box already excludes "construction" globally per `project_frameworks_scorecards`, but NYC construction specifically is an additional emphasis.

**Why:** Surfaced 4/19 — a 4/16 Synergy BB deal "NYC Renovation Design/Build" was included in the morning brief and Kay had to manually kill it ("RENOVATION / BUILD NYC IS NO GO. WE SAID NO CONSTRUCTION IN NYC"). The screening filter missed it. NYC construction in particular has regulatory complexity (DOB, union labor, NYCDEP), high PE roll-up density, and project-revenue volatility — none of which fit G&B's recurring-revenue, asset-light, owner-operated thesis.

**How to apply:**
- At deal-aggregator stage: any business categorized as construction, renovation, design/build, general contracting, build-out services, or major capital project services is filtered out before surfacing to Kay. NYC location amplifies the rejection.
- This is not a soft "flag for review" — it is a hard kill at intake. Do not surface NYC construction deals to the morning brief, the active-deal fast-path, or the intermediary inbound queue.
- The buy-box already says "construction" is excluded — this rule reinforces it and adds NYC-specific emphasis. If a deal is mis-categorized at intake (e.g., "facility services" but actually construction), the screening should still catch it on second pass.
- If Kay manually surfaces a construction-adjacent deal as worth a look (e.g., a maintenance services business that has some build-out revenue), that's her override — but the default direction is NO.
- Surface a screening-miss flag to calibration-workflow when this rule is invoked manually by Kay (as 4/19 — the brief should not have included NYC Renovation Design/Build in the first place).

**Connected:** `project_frameworks_scorecards.md` (line 49: buy-box exclude list includes "construction"). 4/19 brief item #10 was the screening-miss trigger.
