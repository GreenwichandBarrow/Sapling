Cold outreach leads with curiosity about the owner, what they've built, and their space. Not about Kay, not about G&B.

**Why:** Kay's approach is genuine curiosity. The email should make the owner feel seen and respected for what they've accomplished. Mentioning G&B or Kay's background shifts the focus away from them. The owner should feel like someone took the time to understand their world.

**How to apply:**
- Open with what you learned about THEIR business, THEIR journey, THEIR market
- Ask questions that show you've done your homework about their specific situation
- Don't mention Greenwich & Barrow unless absolutely necessary
- Don't describe what Kay does or her background — that's what LinkedIn is for (they'll check)
- The email is a mirror reflecting their accomplishment, with a genuine question attached
