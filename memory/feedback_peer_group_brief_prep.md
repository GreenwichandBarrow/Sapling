For any brief involving Kay's own High/Low/Lesson, personal reflection, or peer-group participation (WSN, ETA peer calls, searcher roundtables, investor-update reflection sections), three rules apply.

**Why:** Validated across the 4/21 WSN Month 2 brief prep — Claude's first-pass drafts were rejected 6+ times for three recurring failure modes: strategic framing presented as emotional content, disclosure too raw for the room, and leakage of G&B continuation-vehicle architecture into traditional-searcher contexts. Traces: `brain/traces/2026-04-21-peer-group-hll-methodology.md` and `brain/traces/2026-04-21-traditional-search-peer-disclosure.md`.

**How to apply:**

**1. Ask before drafting.** For each reflective section (High, Low, Lesson, personal-state prompts), ask ONE open question and wait for Kay to dictate. Do not infer from session state — felt content is in Kay's head, not the log. Examples: "What's a moment you've replayed?" / "What sat on your chest this month?" / "What would you tell a searcher six months behind you?" Then reshape Kay's phrasing into speakable form. Preserve her words.

**2. Calibrate disclosure to meeting cadence + relative position.** Month-two peer groups ≠ established advisors. When Kay is the only person in the room without a visible LOI (or equivalent gap), maintain dignity: strip emotional disclosure from Lows, don't dwell on the gap, never lead a question with personal lack. Lead generously with the group's progress instead. "Not sleeping," investor-update pangs, and self-referential "I'm the one without X" framings are MONTH-SIX content with established trust — not month-two content.

**3. Strip continuation-vehicle language for traditional-searcher peers and investors.** Kay runs a traditional single-acquisition search fund. Traditional-searcher peer groups (WSN included) and her search-fund investor base operate inside that model. Any G&B Charter / HoldCo / multi-acquisition / portfolio / roll-up / permanent-capital / Bridge-Engine-Community-Jewel / Wertheimer-archetype language in their direction reads off-model and can propagate through shared investor/searcher social graphs. Safe substitutes: "the business I'll run," "my operating career," "the company I want to build," "my long-term vision for [this industry]." Litmus before every external draft: would a traditional-searcher investor reading this assume Kay is operating inside or outside the model they funded?

**Do not apply to:** Kay ↔ Claude internal strategy work (charter, niche ranking rationale, long-term planning) — full portfolio/HoldCo architecture is appropriate there. Saltoun, Guillermo, and other advisors aware of G&B's long-term vision are also safe audiences for that framing — only filter for traditional-searcher peers and search-fund-investor communications.
