Before investing more than 15 min calendar time with any new contact, apply both gates in sequence:

**Gate A:** Can this person make a named, personal introduction to a business owner of a **mature, transactable** business (not a startup, not a platform, not a fund portfolio co) within 5 years of a transition?

**Gate B:** Will they do it within 30 days, **unprompted by Kay**?

Fail either gate → industry expert. 30-min coffee max, warm keep-in-touch, no recurring calendar time. Revisit if they surface a warm intro within the 30-day window.

**Why:** River guides make introductions because they want to, not because Kay asks. Ecosystem people talk about introductions and don't deliver. The 30-day unprompted clock exposes which category a person actually belongs to.

**Time-weight pyramid** (given solo-parent constraints, ~8-10 hrs/wk of relationship time):
- River guide (passes both gates): 60% / up to 6 hrs/wk, recurring check-ins, proactive cultivation
- Industry expert (fails Gate B only): 20% / 30-min capped, no recurring calendar unless intro lands
- Peer/validation/ecosystem (fails Gate A): 20% / only when they initiate, no proactive outreach

**How to apply:**
Before adding any new contact to a nurture cadence, running a warm-intro finder check on them, or scheduling any repeat meeting, run Gate A + Gate B. If either fails, cap the relationship at its current depth. This is especially important for conference follow-ups, LinkedIn DMs, and broker/intermediary handoffs where the default has been "keep in touch" without a conversion pathway.

**Exit ramp script** (for over-invested ecosystem relationships):
> "I've really appreciated this — I'm in a phase where I'm going deep on a smaller number of relationships before our deal closes, so I'll be less visible for a bit. I'll absolutely reach back out when things shift."
