**Rule (HARD):** Never sign up for an annual SaaS plan. Always choose monthly. The only exception: when the vendor literally does not offer a monthly tier — annual is the only option. Even after long proven use, do not switch from monthly to annual. The 15-20% discount is never worth the lock-in risk.

**Why:** Two compounding receipts:
1. **Superhuman/Grammarly 2026-04-26.** Kay paid annually for Superhuman. Months later realized productivity value was unclear vs. Mimestream/Apple Mail + Gmail filters. Tried to cancel — Grammarly (post-acquisition billing handler) chatbot policy: "previous charges are not refunded, no human escalation for discretionary refund." Annual lock-in absolute. Sunk cost = unused months of the term.
2. **General pattern.** Annual discounts (typically 15-20%) are mathematically smaller than the cost of being trapped in the wrong tool for 12 months. The savings only materialize if the tool stays in active use for the full term — and you can't predict that on day 1, day 30, or day 90. Even after a year of proven use, switching to annual exposes you to the SAME lock-in risk for the next 12 months. Status quo of monthly billing is strictly safer with negligible cost.
3. **Initial soft version of this rule (45-day evaluation, then optional annual) was wrong** — Kay rejected it 2026-04-26 and replaced with the hard version.

**How to apply:**
- When proposing or signing up for any SaaS tool, default to the monthly tier. Period.
- If the vendor only offers annual: flag explicitly to Kay before purchase. State "this vendor doesn't offer monthly, so annual is the only path — proceed?" Get approval, not assumption.
- State pricing in monthly terms in any briefing/recommendation, never as "$X/yr saves Y%."
- Never recommend graduating from monthly to annual on existing tools, even after long use. Status quo wins.
- Retroactive: surface annual subscriptions in budget-manager audits as items to switch to monthly at next renewal opportunity. If the vendor doesn't allow mid-term switch, don't auto-renew — re-subscribe monthly after term-end.
- If Kay explicitly overrides ("I know I'll use it, just do annual"), respect it but log the override and surface in next budget audit.
