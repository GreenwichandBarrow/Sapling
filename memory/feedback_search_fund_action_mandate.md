G&B is a search fund. Investors capitalized Kay to **acquire and operate** an asset, not to analyze the market indefinitely. Every month of "waiting for clarity" burns runway, burns investor trust, and erodes Kay's $10-50M-by-50 timeline. **Not acting is a slow-motion failure, not a conservative strategy.**

## The Rule

Never frame strategic decisions as "act vs wait." That binary doesn't exist for a search fund operator. The real questions are always:

1. **WHAT do we acquire?** (Anchor selection, niche choice, target evaluation)
2. **WHEN within the fiscal year do we acquire?** (Sequencing within the constraint of action-required)
3. **HOW do we structure the acquisition?** (Equity rollover, deferred payments, tuck-in vs anchor, sale-leaseback, etc.)

## Why The Framing Matters

**Surfaced 2026-04-19** during the Acumen / HoldCo strategic exploration. Claude offered a "Path 1: Wait. Path 2: Use the macro as buyer's-market window" framing as if both were real options. Kay corrected: "this is a search fund. Not Acting is not an option."

The "wait" framing was wrong on three levels:
1. **Structural:** Search funds raise capital with the explicit promise of acquiring. Investors aren't backing analysis paralysis.
2. **Financial:** Burn rate continues regardless. Every month of "waiting" is ~$X consumed without progress toward exit.
3. **Strategic:** Macro never clarifies definitively. Conditions in 2027 will have new uncertainties; in 2028 different ones again. The thesis-aligned action under any conditions is to deploy.

## How To Apply

**When stress-testing a strategic decision:**
- Use macro/AI/competitive analysis to refine WHAT to acquire and HOW to structure
- Never use macro/AI/competitive analysis as input to a "should we act at all" question
- If a niche or anchor candidate fails the stress test, find a different one — don't conclude that doing nothing is the answer

**When Kay expresses uncertainty or fatigue:**
- The uncertainty is about CHOICE between options, not about WHETHER to choose
- Surface options + tradeoffs to help her pick
- Don't offer "wait and see" as a real option (it's a tell that you've drifted off-mode)

**When framing strategic questions:**
- ❌ Wrong: "Should we acquire Acumen, or wait for clarity?"
- ✅ Right: "Should we re-engage Acumen as the anchor, OR pivot to a different anchor candidate, OR run both threads in parallel?"
- ❌ Wrong: "Is now a good time to deploy capital?"
- ✅ Right: "Which deployable opportunity has the strongest fit to our thesis right now?"

**When the macro is genuinely worsening:**
- That's information about which segments to prefer (non-discretionary, AI-leverageable, recurring revenue), NOT permission to wait
- Pivot the WHAT, not the WHETHER
