When Kay directs focus to specific niches or priorities, the right action is **silent focus** — redirect attention and new effort to the priority items while keeping everything else in its current state. Do NOT formally drop, table, kill, or announce deprioritization of non-priority items unless she explicitly asks.

**Why:** On 2026-04-21, during a niche-ranking reset, Claude framed the non-top-two niches as "disqualified" and proposed "everything else comes off the active list until we close a deal or have a failure to learn from." Kay corrected: "I don't want to drop anything. I just want to silently put all my focus on the top options." Formally dropping niches has downstream costs — advisor conversations (Guillermo, Saltoun, Jeff), tracker status moves, notifying JJ/intermediaries, ending outreach cadences — that are irreversible or embarrassing to walk back. Silent focus is reversible. If primary choice stalls, the deprioritized option is still warm and recoverable.

**How to apply:**

1. **Tracker statuses stay as-is** unless Kay explicitly authorizes a Tabled or Killed move. Active-Outreach niches stay Active-Outreach even if they drop in priority ranking.
2. **Do not announce priority changes to advisors, investors, or external parties.** They hear what Kay is focusing on, never what she's deprioritizing.
3. **Ongoing outreach, conference registrations, DealsX list-building, JJ cadences — all continue on their current schedule** for non-priority niches. The system keeps running. New effort goes to priority items.
4. **Language discipline in drafts:** use "I'm focusing on X and Y" or "my top priorities are," never "I'm dropping A, B, C" or "we're no longer pursuing."
5. **Serendipity harvest stays open:** if a non-priority niche produces organic deal flow, warm intro, or advisor introduction, act on it. Silent focus doesn't mean blind to opportunity.
6. **If Kay wants a formal drop**, she will say so explicitly with words like "table," "kill," "remove from the tracker," or "no longer pursuing." Otherwise, default to silent focus.
