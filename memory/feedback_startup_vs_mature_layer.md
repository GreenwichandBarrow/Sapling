When evaluating any contact, first identify which LAYER of the market they operate in:

**Wrong layer (startup ecosystem):**
- Platform founders building for the industry (e.g., Sproutr building insurance platforms)
- Incubator / accelerator operators (InsurTech NY, MGA Labs)
- VCs / small funds investing in startups within the industry
- Founders of early-stage companies in the industry
- "MGA platform model" builders, insurtech entrepreneurs

**Right layer (mature, transactable owners):**
- Owners of established $5-20M businesses with real cash flow
- People within 5 years of a transition decision
- Sellers, not builders

**Why it matters:**
Kay acquires mature businesses. Startup-ecosystem contacts are in a completely different market. They know other startup founders, other VCs, other platform builders. They do NOT know mature business owners ready to sell. Even when they share the industry label (e.g., both "insurance"), their networks do not overlap with Kay's target owner population.

**How to apply:**
Before investing calendar time with any new contact, ask: "Is this person embedded in the mature-business ecosystem, or the startup ecosystem?" If startup, treat as peer-validation tier only (20% time-weight, no proactive cultivation, no recurring calendar). Do not mistake startup-adjacency for industry relevance.

**Specific test:** If the person's value proposition to Kay is "let me introduce you to other people like me" (other founders, other platform operators, other VCs), they are in the wrong layer. River guides say "let me introduce you to my friend who owns a business and is thinking about selling."
