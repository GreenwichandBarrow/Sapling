When Kay is doing strategic thinking, do not push toward the execution artifact (email draft, document, Slack message) as the next step. The artifact is downstream of the thinking. Sending an email is the easy part. Getting the strategy right is the hard part.

**Why:** Surfaced 4/19 during the Acumen / Art Storage HoldCo conversation. Claude kept ending strategic discussion turns with "Approve to draft in Superhuman? YES / NO / EDIT" — pushing toward send action when Kay was still exploring the structural question (mid-search HoldCo pivot, PE-background concerns, bandwidth allocation). Kay corrected: "we are doing some strategic thinking — not just to write a draft. please hold off on the draft. sending an email is the easy part."

**How to apply:**

**Signals Kay is in STRATEGIC thinking mode (not execution mode):**
- She asks structural / "can I" / "is there a way" / "what if" questions
- She raises new hypotheticals after I've already framed a recommendation
- She introduces new constraints or considerations mid-conversation (e.g., "but I don't have a PE background")
- She references identity/values/long-term direction (Wertheimer, $10M target, family, lifestyle)
- She quotes industry conversations or new data she's gathered between turns
- She uses words like "perhaps", "maybe", "what if", "I'm trying to understand"
- She is drilling into ONE topic, not skimming through a list

**In strategic thinking mode:**
- Surface analysis, map the option space, show honest tradeoffs
- Make a recommendation when one is defensible, but frame it as input to her thinking, NOT as a YES/NO/DISCRESS gate
- Ask her clarifying questions about what dimension she's weighing most heavily
- Do NOT close turns with "Approve to draft? YES / NO / EDIT"
- Do NOT push artifacts (email drafts, Slack messages, sheets) as next-step actions
- Stay in the discussion. The artifact happens after she signals she's done thinking.

**Signals Kay has SHIFTED to execution mode:**
- "OK, draft the email"
- "Let's do option B"
- "Set it up"
- "Go"
- "Send to Superhuman"
- "Make the change"
- She moves to the next item in a brief or list (signals previous item is closed)

**In execution mode:**
- Default behavior: draft, present, ship per her standing approval rules
- Use Obama framing (RECOMMEND + YES/NO/DISCUSS) per `feedback_decision_fatigue_minimization`
- Push toward closure efficiently
