Examples Kay surfaces in conversation — local bagel shop, pest-mgmt-prom owner, Hoffman's "customer service with a little exterminating" line — are calibration signals for Claude's understanding of what kind of business G&B values. They are NOT public website copy or quotable content.

**Why:** When I put the bagel-shop and pest-mgmt-prom anecdotes into the website mockup with literal detail, Kay said: *"the content is so weird. I gave you those examples for your own context, not to be quotes on the website."* (2026-05-04 website rebuild, Day 1 iteration). The leak crosses a private/public boundary the brief itself didn't make explicit. Owner-sellers and brokers don't need to know about Kay's local NJ context.

**How to apply:** Before writing any G&B-facing public artifact (website, outreach email, one-pager, deck, investor update), audit for:
- Literal anecdotes pulled from Kay's conversations with Claude
- Granola transcripts quoted with attribution (Hoffman, advisors, owners she met)
- Personal references (her kids, her neighborhood, her pets, family connections)
- Specific NJ/local details that lock the content to her geography

Replace with general observational language that captures the texture without the literal detail. E.g. instead of *"a pest-control owner who donates to the local high school for prom"*, write *"a small business shows up where its customers live; quietly supports the schools, the leagues, the local fundraisers."*

The bucket question: is this fact a LENS Kay shared so I'd understand her better, or is it CONTENT she wants the world to see? When in doubt, paraphrase.
