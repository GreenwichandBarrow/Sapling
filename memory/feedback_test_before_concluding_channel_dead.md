Before concluding that a channel (deal-aggregator platforms, intermediary outreach, a conference series, a social channel, etc.) is structurally off-mix, verify that every planned source in the channel has actually been tested live. "N consecutive zero days" with an incomplete source list is a measurement artifact, not a market truth.

**Why:** 2026-04-21 — I drafted a CIO recommendation to drop public broker platforms from the deal-aggregator based on 6 consecutive zero-yield days. On Kay's first challenge I acknowledged that the 5 most recently added sources (Acquire.com, BizScout, Searchfunder, Kumo, DealFlow Agent) hadn't run. On her second challenge I did the full audit: the SKILL.md approved list is ~38 sources across 4 channels; today's scan covered ~15. 23 approved sources never executed — including BizBuySell (Kay has an account, largest LMM broker), DealForce, all 4 industry-specialist advisors for the active niches (Cetane/MarshBerry/Reagan/Anticimex), and every single Channel 4 association deal board (NPMA/IIABA/IREM/NARPM). The "structural pattern" conclusion was drawn from testing the subset that actually executes — and specifically missing the ~40% of the approved list most likely to surface luxury-B2B owner transitions.

**How to apply:** Before ever concluding that a channel is "structurally dead" or "off-mix," perform a **scan-coverage parity audit**: (a) enumerate the approved source list from the skill definition (every channel, every section), (b) enumerate what actually executed in the last N days of scan artifacts, (c) flag the delta. If (a) > (b), the verdict is invalid — instead surface the delta as Ops/skill-coverage work. The scan subagent may silently only cover one section of the source list while the skill file implies it covers all. Audit both the source list AND the scan execution path before rendering pattern conclusions. Applies to deal-aggregator, outreach channels, conference pipelines, niche-intelligence sources, and any aggregation skill with a multi-section source list.
