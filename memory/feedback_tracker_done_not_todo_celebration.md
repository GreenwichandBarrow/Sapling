description: 2026-05-11 rule. Daily and end-of-day tracker prompts surface COMPLETIONS, not gaps. Pointing at "what's left" creates dread; pointing at "what's done" creates satisfaction and reinforces the habit loop.

When the system prompts Kay about her personal task tracker (TO DO M.DD.YY.xlsx), **focus on what got done, not what's left to do.**

**Forbidden framing:**
- "Open the tracker. Tick today's completions. **N items still outstanding.**"
- "**3 things you didn't get to today.**"
- "**You have N empty priority slots tomorrow.**" (in evening context)
- Anything that surfaces gaps + asks Kay to confront them at the end of the day.

**Approved framing:**
- "Tracker check-in: what got done today?" (open invitation, no pre-counted gaps)
- "Open the tracker, tick today's completions, save." (action-only, no gap surfacing)
- Surface DONE items in the morning briefing's Tracker Pulse line ONLY if they're a positive number ("3 wins yesterday, build on it"). Empty slots fine as a forward-planning count, not retrospective shame.
- End-of-day: celebrate completions. Forward planning happens at morning (when there's energy) or Sunday review (when there's time).

**Why:** Kay closed the tracker for 2 weeks in part because every time she opened it, she saw gaps + uncompleted carryover from prior weeks. Friction loop = abandonment loop. The fix is psychological framing: tracker = satisfaction surface, not accountability hammer. Source: 2026-05-11 Kay-Claude conversation on tracker habit design.

**How to apply:**

1. **`/goodnight` Sunday: focus on Sunday-of-week archive (what got done this week).** Surface completions Mon-Sat. Don't list incomplete carryover at goodnight time.

2. **`/goodnight` weekdays: optional single prompt.** "Open the tracker. Tick today's completions. Save." 90 seconds. No counting of what's left.

3. **`/goodmorning` Tracker Pulse line:** show overdue + empty slots + stale-items count for the day's planning. This IS forward-looking but it's morning energy time, not evening shame time.

4. **Sunday `/goodmorning` Weekly Planning Review:** surface carryover but FRAME as celebration first ("last week's completions: N") then planning ("what carries into this week").

5. **Dashboard tile:** Kay rejected adding a tracker tile to the Command Center dashboard 2026-05-11 — she keeps the tracker visually separate from the dashboard. Do not propose re-adding.
