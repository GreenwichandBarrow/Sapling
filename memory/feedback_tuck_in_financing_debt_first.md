When modeling G&B HoldCo capital paths, **debt + seller notes + anchor operating cash flow are the routine financing tools for tuck-ins.** Equity raises are reserved for major strategic moves (vertical SaaS acquisition, geographic platform expansion beyond cash flow capacity).

## Why This Matters

Surfaced 2026-04-19. Claude framed macro tightening as a constraint on "follow-on capital" with the implicit assumption that equity raises were the primary path. Kay corrected: **Datacor raised bank debt for growth/acquisitions, not equity. Bank or private debt is a real option, not a fallback.**

Most successful searchers (Brent Beshore / Permanent Equity, others) use minimal incremental equity for tuck-ins — they rely on operating cash + debt + seller notes. Equity raises are reserved for strategic step-changes.

## The Capital Stack for Tuck-Ins (in priority order)

| Source | Best fit | Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Anchor operating cash flow** | Smallest tuck-ins, ramped post-anchor | Effectively free | Acumen at ~$1.1M EBITDA funds modest add-ons after debt service |
| **SBA 7(a) acquisition loans** | Sub-$5M tuck-ins | ~Prime + 2.75-4.75% | Up to $5M, 10yr term, best fit for smaller targets (regional estate liquidators, smaller appraisal practices) |
| **Bank acquisition debt** | $5-20M deals | SOFR + 250-450 bps typical | Live Oak, Newtek, regional commercial banks specialize in lower-middle-market deals. **Datacor precedent: bank-debt-funded growth without equity dilution.** |
| **Private credit funds** | $10M+ deals, more complex structures | Higher than bank (~10-14%) | Fills middle-market gap. Faster than bank, more flexible terms, but pricier |
| **Seller notes** | Almost every deal | 4-6% typical | Standard 10-25% of purchase price as deferred. Acumen LOI already had $1.5M deferred — same pattern repeats per tuck-in |
| **Mezzanine debt** | $20M+ deals only | 10-14% | Usually only relevant for larger acquisitions; not routine for tuck-ins |
| **Equity raise** | Major strategic moves only | Most expensive | Voxme acquisition or new geographic platform — NOT routine tuck-ins |

## How To Apply

**When discussing the financial path for the HoldCo:**
- Default assumption: tuck-ins are funded by debt + seller notes + cash flow
- Don't frame "what if you need more capital?" as primarily an equity question
- Equity raise is the LAST resort, not the FIRST resort
- Macro tightening compresses equity availability faster than debt availability — debt is more macro-resilient

**When stress-testing the thesis under uncertain conditions:**
- Tighter equity markets ≠ paralyzed tuck-in machine
- Banks and private credit funds continue to lend on cash-flowing acquisitions even in down markets (they want yield)
- Seller notes are MORE available in soft markets (sellers willing to carry paper rather than wait for better cash buyers)

**When proposing follow-on actions:**
- Routine tuck-in cadence (1-2 per year): debt-funded, no investor consent needed
- Strategic step-change (Voxme acquisition, multi-metro platform launch): equity raise from existing investor pool at higher valuation post-anchor
