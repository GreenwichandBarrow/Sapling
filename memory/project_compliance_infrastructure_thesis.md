From extensive ChatGPT research (March 2026), Kay identified a strong pattern she gravitates toward:

**Core formula:** Regulation → Mandatory operational task → Outsourced digital/documentation service → Recurring revenue

**Kay's key insight:** "mandatory + operational + annoying = outsource"

**Characteristics of ideal targets:**
- Regulation-driven demand (can't skip it)
- Digital or documentation-based delivery
- Recurring annually (certification cycles, monitoring contracts)
- B2B (sold to organizations, not individuals)
- Fragmented market with founder-owned companies
- NOT easily replaced by AI (vertical expertise + people layer required)
- $2-5M EBITDA range

**Top niches identified (ranked by Kay's reaction):**
1. ADA Digital Accessibility & Document Remediation — Kay's stated favorite
2. Compliance e-learning platforms (harassment/HR compliance style)
3. SEC filing preparation & XBRL tagging
4. Safety Data Sheet (SDS) management
5. Records retention & information governance

**Pattern Kay consistently rejects:** Project-based consulting, one-off assessments, anything without continuous monitoring/recurring component.

**Important distinction:** This is a SECOND thesis track alongside the luxury infrastructure thesis. The compliance niches don't require Kay's luxury background as a differentiator — they require operational execution and tech enablement. Both tracks are valid.

**Food safety certification + hospitality:** Kay noted her network in high-end dining could create a wedge here. This is the one compliance niche that overlaps with luxury background.

**Why:** Kay's ChatGPT conversations revealed she's drawn equally to "boring but essential infrastructure" as to luxury-adjacent services. Both tracks should be pursued in parallel.

**How to apply:** Score these compliance niches against the same criteria as luxury infrastructure. Add to the Industry Research Tracker. Some may rank higher than luxury niches on margins, scalability, and AI-defensibility.
