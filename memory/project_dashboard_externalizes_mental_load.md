The Command Center dashboard's purpose, in Kay's own words on 2026-04-24 PM after seeing the merged Infrastructure mockup: *"I feel like this dashboard was all of the information I was juggling in my mind now on a very readable dashboard."*

**Why:** This is the load-bearing reason the dashboard exists — not vanity metrics, not investor-facing optics, not status reporting. It is a mental-RAM offload device. Kay was carrying skill-fire status, tool auth health, credit runway, calibration learnings, and pipeline state in her head; the dashboard moves all of that to a single readable surface so she can release the working memory.

**How to apply:**
- When scoping any new page, section, tile, or row: ask "what was Kay tracking mentally that this externalizes?" If the answer is nothing — if it's purely informational, decorative, or aimed at someone else — it doesn't belong.
- "Readable" is the test, not "comprehensive." If a section adds information without reducing what Kay has to track in her head, it's noise. (This is why the redundant skill-activity digest got cut and Tech Stack got merged into Infrastructure on the same day — both *added* surface area without externalizing new mental load.)
- When choosing between two design options, prefer the one that takes more weight off Kay's working memory, even if it's denser visually. Pober-style density is fine; cognitive density is not.
- Validation phrase to listen for from Kay: "wow I love …" / "this is what I was juggling" / "now I don't have to remember." When she says these, the page is doing its job — note what design pattern produced the moment so it can be reused.
