Apr 30 2026 — Kay resolved the 4 deferred implications from the Kristin Wihera search-fund wind-down debrief (4/23 WSN-cohort call). These now constitute G&B operating doctrine; the original defer is closed.

## 1. Email-decay pattern → OUTSOURCE

Kristin's cold-email channel worked Sept '23 → May '24 then collapsed (deliverability rot). G&B response: outsource cold email to DealsX (Kay-Email channels) and JJ (Call-Only channels). Kay does not run her own cold-email infrastructure.

**Why:** Email deliverability is a full-time technical job (warming, sender rotation, IP reputation, list hygiene); Kay's time has higher ROI on owner conversations + intermediary depth.

**How to apply:** Never propose Kay-run cold-email infrastructure or warming workflows. Route any new niche to DealsX vs JJ-Call-Only based on channel fit. Email decay is a vendor problem, not Kay's. If DealsX deliverability decays, the answer is vendor management, not Kay-built tooling.

## 2. Solo-search risk → ACCEPT, DELEGATE AROUND IT

Kristin solo-searched and burned out — life-pull plus isolation. G&B response: solo-search is the model (Kay is solo on the cap table), but the risk is mitigated through team build — JJ (calls), DealsX (cold email), Camilla (admin/scheduling), Sam (DealsX coordination), Claude (orchestration). Kay is not alone in the work even though she's solo as searcher.

**Why:** Partner-search would slow Deal 1 timeline + create equity dilution + introduce co-founder-conflict risk; team build is the chosen substitute. Kristin's case validates that a team-of-vendors-and-VAs is the right substitute.

**How to apply:** Do not surface "should we add a partner?" as a recurring question — answered. When proposing solutions to overload, default to delegation / outsourcing / automation, never to partner. If Kay raises co-founder herself in future, treat as fresh signal.

## 3. 35% IRR hurdle mismatch → SCREEN ON MULTIPLES + SIZE + INDUSTRY GROWTH

Anacapa-style 35% IRR hurdle creates a mismatch with the closeable-deal universe (Kristin's framing). G&B response: don't renegotiate the hurdle with investors — screen deals more disciplined-ly. The three factors that determine whether a deal can clear 35% IRR: **entry multiple paid, deal size (TEV / EBITDA scale), industry growth headroom**. All three must support the math, not just operational/buy-box fit.

**Why:** Investor terms are set (Anacapa-style hurdles are the cost of capital). The controllable variables are which deals enter the funnel and what we pay for them. Pushing back on hurdles burns capital relationships; over-paying or buying flat industries silently kills returns.

**How to apply:** When evaluating any niche, target, or LOI, the IRR math depends on multiples + size + growth in addition to operational fit. Don't approve a niche just because it scores well on the G&B scorecard — also pressure-test whether typical industry entry multiples + likely deal sizes + sector growth can clear 35% IRR. Surface the math when it's marginal, not just when fit is marginal. CFO agent owns this lens going forward.

## 4. "Investors are not therapists" → DOCTRINE STANDS

Investors are for capital + strategic counsel + warm-network leverage, not emotional support. Per Kristin's debrief, blurring the line burned bridges with her LPs. Doctrine confirmed.

Kay flagged personal interest in therapy but no current budget. **No G&B-system action.** This is a personal-life note logged once, not a recurring item to surface.

**Why:** Kristin watched her LPs disengage when she leaned on them emotionally. The relationship became transactional in the wrong direction. G&B's investor channel is preserved for capital + counsel only.

**How to apply:** When Kay vents about deal stress / sourcing fatigue / burnout signals, do NOT suggest "tell Jeff / Guillermo / the LP base." Don't propose paid-therapist solutions unless Kay raises budget herself. Keep emotional and capital channels strictly separate in any drafted communication. If Kay one day says she has the budget for therapy, that's a personal-life signal to acknowledge once and move on — not a G&B-system change.
