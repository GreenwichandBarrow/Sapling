## The declaration — April 14, 2026

After months of circling (feedback_thesis_spinning), Kay named it cleanly:

> "I want a hold co. Im certain of it now. Its been circling but to me - thats the family legacy. I can buy a company thats more female empowered but also buy something more general community like pest, and the legacy is the 'family' of companies."

## The origin story

Her journey started at Chanel asking: *do I want my boss's job, or my boss's boss's job?* The answer was neither — the person she wanted to be was **the owner of Chanel**. The Wertheimer family (Mousse Partners) owns Chanel plus ~250 other companies.

**Wertheimer model applies to STRUCTURE, not SCALE.** Kay is explicit (Apr 14 2026): she does NOT want 250 companies, a $1B empire, or public attention. She wants the family-legacy HoldCo structure and the multi-generational optionality — at a scale where she knows every CEO and lives under the radar. Target: 5 companies, ~$10-25M total EBITDA, ~$50-80M enterprise value. Personal net worth goal: $50M by 50 (NOT $1B).

The Wertheimer sons structure is the appeal: one son operates (Chanel), one allocates (family office). The HoldCo creates **multi-generational optionality** — future generations pick the company or role that fits their interests and strengths. Risk + values + opportunity architecture combined. The Wertheimers themselves are famously private — proving the quiet-legacy model works at any scale.

**Kay specifically identifies with Alain Wertheimer** (Apr 14 2026) — quiet, small meetings with a few individuals, does not want to be the face of the companies. CEOs are the faces; she is the owner-allocator. This confirms her operating role post-G&B-exit: **allocator, not operator**. Time spent on capital allocation, CEO hiring/evaluation, quarterly owner reviews, M&A, system/process design. NOT on front-of-house sales, conferences, panels, investor days.

**Deal selection implication:** buy businesses where a strong CEO can run it without Kay being in the foreground. Rules out: founder-brand businesses, personality-dependent sales, businesses without a promotable #2. Favors: existing management layers, service/compliance/infrastructure where operations matter more than personality, retiring-owner with ready operations team.

## What this resolves

- **Niche tension resolved:** Female-empowered (jewel niches) + community-embedded (pest, services) are not competing buckets. They're siblings under one legacy HoldCo. The question becomes *order of acquisition*, not *which bucket is right*.
- **Timeline resolved:** Permanent hold, multi-generational. Not 5-7 year exit.
- **Capital structure resolved:** Family HoldCo = family capital + debt (Snell model, different buy box). Existing G&B investors honored by first deal, HoldCo emerges as chapter 2.
- **Buy box unifies:** Businesses worth owning forever, in categories she'd be proud to hand down.

## Refinements (Apr 14 2026, same session)

**Insurance = GEICO float, but ONLY in family HoldCo.** The GEICO move (insurance float funding other acquisitions) requires permanent-capital discretion — which is incompatible with G&B's LP structure. Under G&B, insurance cash flow belongs to the fund, not to Kay for redeployment. Therefore insurance is **Deal 2 (family HoldCo), NOT Deal 1 (G&B).**

**G&B Deal 1 = exit-bound growth business, NOT insurance.** G&B's first acquisition should be something that appreciates via growth + multiple expansion, sellable at 5-7 years, doesn't require float. Candidates from current pipeline: Specialty Healthcare SaaS (Home Health Agency Software — Adam's ECP pattern, proven ~15x ARR exit template), a female-empowered jewel niche (TBD), or pest control in luxury markets.

**Family HoldCo = no outside investors.** Kay decided Apr 14 2026 she wants to go it alone on the family HoldCo. No CCV raise, no Shoreham-style restructure, no conversion of G&B LPs into HoldCo partners. Self-funded from CEO salary + debt (Snell-style). Slower compounding traded for full family ownership + permanence + "built something for my family" feeling. No LP governance to dilute the Wertheimer/multi-generational thesis.

**LP structure has two doors.** Some investors want returns + exit; some want permanence + staying connected. First deal honors both — exit available for those who want it, conversion to HoldCo equity partner available for those who want permanence. Don't force a binary. Let LPs self-select.

**G&B name morphs.** The search fund name becomes the HoldCo name. Brand continuity from day one. The **G&B entity** (successor vehicle) should be the acquirer, not Kay personally — matters for tax, estate, legacy transfer. Raise with Jeff, Guillermo, and legal counsel before first deal closes.

**Community = values filter, not retention tactic.** Kay's bagel shop story: the owners leave a bowl for animals, give treats to her kids, make an extra bagel for the homeless guy. That's the filter. Not "community-embedded because it's sticky" but "community-embedded because the owners make the world better." Test every acquisition: *do the people running this business make the community better? Would Kay want her kids to know these owners?* This is what separates G&B from a PE roll-up.
