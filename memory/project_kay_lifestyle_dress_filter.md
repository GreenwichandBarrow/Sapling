**Insight from Kay 2026-04-29 reflection** (after attending NJPMA Business Management Workshop at Topgolf Edison NJ):

> "I liked the casualness of the pest management meeting today. I feel like I can dress preppy and casual as a business leader, not have to be all dressed up as I have been for over a decade. I got very tired of the office impression needed and I think it might continue if I go into art insurance."

**The filter:** lived-experience comfort with dress code is a real selection criterion for the niche Kay ultimately acquires into. After a decade of formal corporate / luxury-services dress, the casual-preppy NJPMA workshop felt like relief.

**How this should weight niche evaluation:**

- **Niches that allow preppy-casual operator dress (positive fit):**
  - Specialty pest management
  - Specialty equipment service (trade-shop dress codes)
  - Lower-middle-market manufacturing and distribution
  - Most blue-collar services
  - Heritage-style holding companies

- **Niches that perpetuate formal corporate dress (negative fit, weighted against):**
  - Art insurance (UHNW client-facing requires formal)
  - Private banking / wealth advisory (industry uniform)
  - Luxury-services-to-luxury-clients (Kay-as-CEO would still need to dress for clients)
  - Possibly: art storage if Kay personally engages with collectors and museums

**Caveat — does NOT mean drop art insurance:**
- The strategic / business merits still stand
- Margot's specialty insurance brokerage thesis is independently strong
- Lifestyle filter is a tiebreaker / personal-cost factor, not a primary kill criterion
- Kay can hire customer-facing executives in a luxury niche; she doesn't have to be the front-facing dressed-up face

**How to apply going forward:**
- When weighing two niches that score similarly on traditional criteria, lifestyle-dress-fit becomes a deciding factor
- Don't surface this as a primary scorecard category (per `feedback_niche_ranking_searcher_criteria_only.md` — niche rankings use traditional searcher criteria)
- DO use it in 1:1 strategic conversations and final-decision moments
- Cross-reference with `user_kay_life_goals.md` (WFH lifestyle, $10M exit) and `feedback_kays_approach.md` (lived experience matters)
