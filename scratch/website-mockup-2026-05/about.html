About

A business is more than
what's on its books.

It is the people who built it. The customers who depend on it. The community it answers to.

Most great service businesses are sold to the wrong buyer. Owners who spent thirty years answering their phone on Saturdays end their careers signing paperwork they barely understand, watching what they built turned over to someone whose plan begins with the words “synergies” and ends with a five-year exit.

It does not have to go that way. The same kind of business that took decades to build can be handed to a single buyer who plans to keep it the way it is. Long-term capital, no fund clock, no auction, no rush. A conversation that lasts as long as it needs to.

Greenwich & Barrow is a long-term holding company in formation. One business at a time, held for the long run, run by people who answer their own emails.

What An Owner Deserves

When the conversation begins.

Time, not pressure.

A first conversation that is not a sales call. A second conversation that does not require a deck. A third conversation that is about the business, not about the deal.

A conversation, not an auction.

Direct. Private. No 200-page CIM. No round of competing bids. The price is fair or the call ends politely.

A buyer who answers the phone.

One point of contact, start to finish. The same person on the first call as on the closing. No team handoffs.

A future the customers won't notice.

The way the business is run is exactly what makes it worth buying. The plan is to keep doing what already works, for as long as the customers keep showing up.
